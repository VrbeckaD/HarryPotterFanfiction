    <blockquote class="userstuff summary">
      <p>His “private missions” had become more of a way of Dumbledore suggesting he stay as far from the people he once loved the most, and Remus understood. As he turned the corner and passed a small convenience store, he thought that once (if?) the war was over, he’d probably just disappear into the Muggle world. Things would be easier, then. </p><p>No more than two hours later, a bit of his past stood waiting at his door. He didn’t hesitate to look him directly in the eye, although the man in front of him did. He was staring at anything but Remus, and he was clutching something in his hands. When that something began to cry, the horrifying truth came to Remus before the man had a chance to explain. He looked down at his feet and for the first time in what felt like forever, tears were blurring his vision. He took a deep breath and, regaining his composure, faced his everything, even now. Even after all that had happened. He pointed his wand at him, which he hadn’t forgotten to tuck into his coat before leaving, and spoke in a voice he recognized no longer. </p><p>“You’d better start explaining, Black, before I curse you into next year.”</p>
