    <h6 class="landmark heading">Summary</h6>
    <blockquote class="userstuff summary">
      <p>One day, a young girl sits on the edge of a well. On this day, she falls in. Then the next, she’s not even a ‘she’ anymore. He’s Peter Pettigrew — doomed to die at the hands of his (betrayed) friend’s son.</p><p>Peter is selfish, bitter and brash. He’s not some fairy tale hero, he doesn’t care if everyone around him dies, as long as he lives. The marauders are annoying and childish. Survival is his priority, and he can’t afford to face distractions. </p><p>This just makes the fact that soulmates are now apparently a thing all the more godawful.</p><p>(And then another day years later he falls into an entirely new impossibility, Remus Lupin in tow, right into the third book of Harry Potter. Smack bang on the other Peter Pettigrew’s grave).</p><p>It’s confusing and graceless, and entirely something that would happen to him of all people.</p>
    </blockquote>
