  <div class="chapter preface group" role="complementary">
    <h3 class="title">
      <a href="/works/20743736/chapters/49286897">Chapter 1</a>: Summer Part 1
    </h3>

    <!-- only display byline if different from the main byline -->


  </div>

  <!--main content-->
  <div class="userstuff module" role="article">
    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p> </p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Lily lazed in the shade of the willow enjoying the first day of her summer holidays.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Lily,” her mother’s melodious voice issued from inside the house.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Coming Mum,” Lily sighed, so much for just relaxing for the day. “Come on Lion,” she called to the cat like creature resting above her in the branches of the tree. The furry creature flicked his tail indignantly before deigning to jump from his perch and saunter after her. She giggled a little mischievously as she remembered Tuney’s squeals of horror when the kitten had been presented to their mother on her last birthday. Since then Tuney had avoided the odd looking ‘cat’. So much so, that anyone would think he was a carrier for the plague. Lily mused that it was almost like Tuney had a radar for things that were magical. Even more amusing was watching Lion as he deliberately put himself into Tuney’s path. He was sitting at her door making small meeping mews to wake her up in the morning before her alarm went off. He was there when she emerged from the shower, and every night when she came home, he was sitting in the lounge room watching, tail twitching and yellow eyes gleaming as they followed the tall girl as she walked to the kitchen for dinner and stayed staring at her as she ate her meal.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Oh, I wondered where Lion had got to,” Mrs Evans said as Lily entered the kitchen the kitten scampering in quickly after her, narrowly avoiding the closing door.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“He’s been following me around since I got home,” Lily shrugged. Well… when he hadn’t been stalking Tuney!</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Most intelligent cat I ever saw, he actually fetches things for Nanna Anne when she asks! Especially on the days she‘s feeling poorly. I saw her ask him for her bed socks the other day and as calm as you please he jumped into her drawer, dug out a pair and carried them to her in his mouth! Now before I forget an owl arrived for you.” She indicated the grey fluffy bird perched on the back of one of the dining room chairs.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Thanks Mum. How’s Nanna?” Lily asked as she reached to remove the note from the bird’s claw.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Not doing so well, but that’s to be expected, there is only one cure for what she has.” Lily’s Mother gave a little huff to clear a suddenly tight throat. “Now that I have gotten your attention away from your books, what are you up to today?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Well, I need to feed the Smith’s and the Jones’ dogs, and I’m looking after the Peterson twins tonight from seven till ten,” Lily recited as she opened the note and scanned the messy script quickly.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <em>Dear Squirt,</em>

<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“We thought we would teach Lily how to fly,” Lily’s head whipped around so fast her hair flicked her in the face.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Really?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Really, really.”<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p> </p>
<p>Sandwiches eaten, Fabian grabbed three broomsticks from the shed and they headed back to the field leaving Molly to wrangle Bill who was protesting vehemently that he didn’t need an afternoon nap and much would rather go flying with Unca Gid and Unca Fabe!</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Alright then, I’ll stay lower then you, so I can catch you if you fall ok?” Fabian reassured the nervous girl. “Not that you will!”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Do you remember how to mount?” Lily demonstrated “and kick off?” she pushed hard against the ground and rose a little then lent forwards and landed with a nervous smile.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“So now kick off pull up on the broom slightly to get a bit more height then lean forward just a little and we’ll head over to the trees.” Lily did as instructed flying steadily towards the orchard.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span>“Ok corners next. We’ll take off, then instead of going straight, we’ll go around the field. If you gently lean to the side, the broom will turn that way and go down slightly. If you just want to turn, gently pull the broomstick the way you want to go, you apply pressure with your hands and thighs.” Lily nodded, pushed off and did a lap of the field. They then repeated the circuit in the opposite direction.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Doing well!” Fabian encouraged as he flew next to her “I don’t think you need me down below anymore. Let’s go again but a bit faster.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Slowly Lily’s confidence grew, by the end of the day she could dodge through the trees and was nearly keeping up with the boys.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“I can’t believe it! You should try out for the team this year,” said Gideon.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Thanks but no. I don’t like the idea of those bludgers!” she shuddered.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Too right, they hurt. We had best take you back, so Moll can take you home.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Thanks for everything.” Lily gave each boy a quick hug when they arrived back at the Burrow before grabbing hold of Molly’s arm and disappearing with a pop.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Well what are we going to teach her next time?” Fabian asked his brother.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“I think we need to talk to Arthur. I mean, protego is great but she can’t very well walk around school casting that all the time, can she?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Yeah not as often as they curse her at any rate, she’d be exhausted.”</p>
<p> </p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Lily curled up in the chair beside Nanna Anne’s bed and read as the old women slept.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Knock, knock, knock.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>She quietly rose and answered the back door.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Sev what are you doing here?” she whispered noting his black eye. “Come in and let’s put something cold on that. Mum’ll be back in a moment. You can stay here the night.” He didn’t say anything as she sat him down on the sofa. Grabbing a bag of peas from the freezer she wrapped them in a tea towel and pressed them gently to his face.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span>Ten minutes later footsteps could be heard on the drive and Mrs Evans walked in accompanied by Eva and Harry. The blonde headed twins ran to Lily for a hug before noticing the teenage boy and tentatively smiling at him.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Sev are you alright?” Mrs Evans asked with a concerned frown noticing the impromptu ice pack immediately.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Not the best,” he muttered grudgingly accepting hugs from the children. Mrs Evans pulled away the cold bag and looked at his face.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Well you’re going to have a splendid black eye. You’ll be staying for the night. Lily go set up a mattress in your room, he can sleep in there.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“It’s ok, I won’t stay long, don’t go to any trouble. I don’t want to be a bother,” Sev asserted.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“You’re not going back there, not tonight,” Mrs Evans was firm. “I’ll send Ewan down to let your Mum know where you are when he gets home from work.” Severus gave up on the argument; even he had to admit that sometimes it was nice to be cared for.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“What happened Sev?” little Eva asked patting his knee with her small hand.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“My Dad got a bit angry.”<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>Bang.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span>A glass that had been sitting on the kitchen bench burst showering the room in shards. Eva jumped in fright and began to cry. At the sudden noise Lily came thundering down the stairs.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>“What’s going on? Is everyone ok?” Looking at the pieces glass on the floor “Was that you Sev?” he shook his head.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Lily crouched down in front of Eva and wiped her tears “What’s the matter Eva?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“I’m scared! What if Sev’s Dada hurts him? Like my Dada hurt my Mama,” Sev and Lily exchanged a look- could it have been accidental magic?</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Sev is going to stay with us for a bit, so we’ll look after him ok?”<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>This seemed to reassure the small girl. The crying dwindled to a sniffle and she wiped her eyes (and nose) with her hands. “Ok.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Now run out the back and play.” Lily gave both the little ones hugs and sent them out the back to play before grabbing the hoover and beginning to clean up the mess.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>It had taken a while, but Lily had studied the book that Severus had given her for her last birthday thoroughly, filling a whole notepad with extra notes and formulae. In the end she was sure that she had done it!<span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Created a new spell. Well, if she was truthful it wasn’t a completely new spell, more like an adaption of an old one, but that adaption, if it worked, would surely solve one of her biggest problems at Hogwarts. Looking at her notes again she felt a small ball in her chest that she hadn’t felt for a long time, pride.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">   </span>Another week passed, and Lily returned to the Burrow, it was a Saturday and for once Arthur was home.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Hello Mr Weasley,” Lily greeted him as she walked into the house ahead of his wife. “I wanted to ask you something, if you have the time?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Well anything as long as I can ask you some questions too,” the man gave a good-natured grin.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Sure,” she readily agreed, “Well I want to make up a spell; in fact, I think I have already. Could I show it to you and could we test it?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Why did you want to make up a spell?” he turned his astonished face to look at his brothers-in-law, they both shrugged.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Well I couldn’t find anything that did what I wanted, and my friend gave me a book that kind of explains how to create spells. The easiest way is by adapting an already known spell, which is what I think I’ve done, so now I want to test it out. I figured I should try it with a proper grown up wizard like you or Molly first, just in case I got it wrong.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Well, alright then, what is it supposed to do?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“It should put a protective shield on the skin to help prevent cuts and small hexes like the Incisus curse. The incantation is Protego Dermis and you just point the wand at the middle of your forehead.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Ok, give us a demonstration.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“I’ve never tried it before but… ok here goes….” She took a deep breath and then said clearly, “Protego Dermis.” Nothing happened, Lily was disappointed.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Don’t give up yet,” the older wizard stated calmly. “Were you concentrating hard enough? Determination and focus are what decide if a spell is going to work for you, try again, be forceful, visualise the effect that you want.”<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“PROTEGO DERMIS!” she shouted, and a visible golden web extended from her wand and settled over her skin to disappear a moment later.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“It tingles!” She exclaimed excitedly. “Can we test it now?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>Arthur hesitated slightly “With the Incisus hex? Are you sure?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Yes please, if I can get this to work, it will make a big difference to all the Muggleborns at school.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Ok, are you ready? - Incisus,” his effort was at best, half-hearted as he wasn’t confident her spell had worked but still the light beamed from his wand, Lily flinched instinctively and then looked at her arm.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Nothing!” she let out a happy squeal, “Now how do I make it stay on for long periods of time?”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“In general, the strength of the spell is also decided by the strength and determination of the caster.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“So I just need to practice?”<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Given that all indications are that you are quite the strong witch I would say yes.”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“That was bloody brilliant!” the oldest brother crowed.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Gideon!” Molly scolded. “I’ll tell Mother!”</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“But Moll,” whined Gideon, “It was incredible!”<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Now what other spells are you going to teach me?” She turned her beaming face to the boys.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“We aren’t teaching you anything else!” Lily’s smile fell.</p>
<p><span class="Apple-converted-space">  </span>“Until you teach us how you did that!” and the trio went out into the yard to practice once more. After consultation with Arthur, Gideon and Fabian planned to teach Lily Protego Duo and the summoning charm before the end of the holidays. When they returned to school they would seek advice from the Professors on appropriate spells.<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></p>
<p>The spells weren’t the only lessons they taught Lily that holidays. To encourage her situational awareness, they spent days practicing tripping her randomly as she ran around the orchard playing tag. Over time Lily became quite adept at dodging and blocking tripping hexes and she would start each day at the Burrow by applying the skin shield which they would then test whenever they stopped between lessons, until she could make it last two hours before she needed to redo it. Satisfied that they were making progress Lily was much happier to return to Hogwarts.</p>
