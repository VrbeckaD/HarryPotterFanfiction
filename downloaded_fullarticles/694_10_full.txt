        <div class="summary module" role="complementary">
          <h3 class="heading">Summary:</h3>
            <blockquote class="userstuff">
              <p>At the dusk of the Heroic Age, on the shores of the Aegean sea, a bastard prince of famed lineage and a priest of Artemis embark on an epic journey to rival the old legends. Their bloodline, the choices they make and the relationship between them will set the course for the future of our civilization. </p><p>After all, who says that Odysseus was the last great hero of antiquity?</p>
            </blockquote>
        </div>

  <h3 class="heading">Notes:</h3>





    <blockquote class="userstuff">
      <p>Here it is! The story that everyone I asked said they wanted to read but nobody was writing.</p><p>This monster of a fic is the lovechild of my passion for history and mythology and my current obsession with the Scamander brothers. And I say it's a monster of a fic (by my standards) because at the time of posting the first chapter I have already written six but the breaking point is nowhere in sight. </p><p>There will be a lot of references to classical Mythology here. I will cite my sources at the bottom but know that I mostly avoided Ovid and the later Roman interpretations to keep this purely Hellenic. Most of the definitions for the more obscure concepts will be woven into the narration, but if not, I will post extra notes at the end explaining terms and contextual clues. Never hesitate to ask me anything though. I love to ramble :D</p><p>Please enjoy and don't forget to tell me if you do </p>

    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p align="justify">Theseus froze when a swift arrow flew just under his nose and embedded itself in the bark. It had taken him by surprise, so he was lucky no real assassination attempt was actually taking place.</p><p align="justify">Turning in the direction the arrow had come from he crossed his arms over his chest, raising an eyebrow. Newt! Are you trying to kill me now?</p><p align="justify">Sorry, The bushes answered in a sarcastic tone didnt see you under all that beard.</p><p align="justify">The greenery parted and out stepped a young, lean man holding a bow. His full mouth was slightly curled upwards in a candid smile. Not for the first time, Theseus breath caught momentarily at the sight of him, drinking in with hungry eyes the peppering of freckles over the rosy skin and flaming red hair.</p><p align="justify">Wait a second! He rushedtothe boy, extending his hand to run his fingers over the exposed nape. The last time hed saw Newt he still had wild curly locks collected in a ponytail that reached his shoulders. Now it was cut to a finger length, the back shorter than the fringe, which still seemed determined to imitate an angry bush. The silver diadem of Artemis gleaming unchanged under it. Is that a declaration of intentions?</p><p align="justify">Its a declaration of no longer being able to deal with a very bad tangle. Newt swatted his hand away, huffing. But if Im lucky it will also deter pesky men invading this shrine for other reasons than bringing offerings to my lady Artemis.</p><p align="justify">Theseus laughed, relieved. Good, and here I thought I would need to change my approach. I dont look good in braids.</p><p align="justify">You cant be serious Newt actually seemed taken aback by the implication and Theseus winked, leaning down to peck his cheek Im always serious when it comes to you.</p><p align="justify">Newt didnt stop him but didnt lean in either, just narrowing his eyes, as if trying to figure out whether Theseus was pulling his leg or not.</p><p align="justify">As any Attican youth, him cutting his hair meant he was now considered a man, and therefore could choose for himself and seduce an eromenos, a younger pupil and a lover. Younger being the key there, unless he wanted to be made fun of and his masculinity questioned. Not that it really mattered in Newt's case since, as a priest of Artemis, he had sworn himself to chastity. It had come as a huge disappointment to Theseus, who had been persistently courting him ever since he had first noticed how beautiful Newt was. He hadnt been the only one trying his luck back then but, thankfully, remained one of the few people that Newt had kept in contact with once hed retired to the sacred forest.</p><p align="justify">They fell into step with each other, walking back to the shrine following a wide path through the trees, Newts bow thrown over the quill on his back. So how did it go at Delphi? He asked.</p><p align="justify">You got my message? Theseus was pleased Wasnt sure you would. And well, I have some things to tell you. I didnt come here just to stare into your pretty eyes, you see? He grinned, bouncing on the balls of his feet, suddenly excited now that he remembered the reason hed rode there.</p><p align="justify">I can imagine Newt chuckled, shooting a sideways glance at his visitor. As if judging Theseus messy hair and beard. He was used to seeing a much cleaner version of the prince of Attica, who had never lost the underlying hope to sway him from his priestly duties and therefore always made sure to look as dashing as he could when he came to visit. The oracle say anything good, or... comprehensible?</p><p align="justify">That question seemed to put a damp on Theseus spirits, shoulders dropping momentarily. However, before he said anything, they came in sight of the shrine and a loud barking overwhelmed any other sound around. A giant, black dogcame out of nowhere, rushing their way, powerful legs almost flying in the air, teeth bared and ears up. He jumped straight at Theseus, almost toppling him over and, with one long swap of his tongue, covered the full side of his neck and head in drool.</p><p align="justify">Ouuuff! Fido! Get down! Stop, stop you giant oaf!</p><p align="justify">Never one to obey orders given by anyone but his owner Fido continued to waggle his tail at the speed of sound and licking Theseus face.</p><p align="justify">Finally Newt,who haddoubled over laughing, gave out a sharp whistle and the dog leaped away, running in circles around them and then skipping to Theseus side. He didnt try to attack his face again but panted happily when the man ruffled the fur between his ears.</p><p align="justify">See? <em> He </em> loves me.</p><p align="justify">I love you too. Newt shrugged, still snickering under his breath You just dont know how to take a hand and stop trying to grab the arm.</p><p align="justify">I want to grab more of you than just the arm. Theseus couldnt resist, but Newt scrunched up his nose.</p><p align="justify">Are you really trying to flirt with me while looking, and smelling, like a bowl of dog food?</p><p align="justify">Oh, right.</p><p align="justify"></p><p align="justify">In the end, he managed to clean himself up in the stream behind thetemple and came back to Newt sitting on the steps with Fidos head on his lap and a plate of figs by his side.Theseus took a seat too and helped himself to a few pieces of fruit.</p><p align="justify">So, I take you came from Delphi with mixed feelings, Newt asked.</p><p align="justify">Theseus nodded. You know about the rumors coming from Crete and the Peloponnese? Weve been providing sanctuary to people that decided to leave. They say dire times are coming. Something big, and bad. Newt nodded, mouth drawing in a thin line. Yes, hes been having troublesome dreams and feelings as of late too. My father wanted me to go to Delphi and ask the Oracle about the future of Attica. I figured I could get a prediction for myself as well, about you know, he waved his hand what I should do in life.</p><p align="justify">Still dealing with your crisis I see?</p><p align="justify">Its not a crisis, but a valid concern. Im a legal citizen and a bastard prince. My brothers are much older, well prepared, and very ready to take the throne the moment my dad sneezes the wrong way. I cant spend my entire life hunting and showing up to feasts. And dad didnt even let me join the army, even if he was the one that has been insisting my whole life I should have the best trainers. For what? Shooting geese? Im named after the greatest hero in our history, and yet I feel as useful as a-</p><p align="justify">An infant princess, I know. Newt rolled his eyes, very familiar by now withthat tiradeThe Oracle?</p><p align="justify">Oh, right. Theseus pepped up Well, I wanted to get two prophecies but the old hag only gave me one. Hear it out tho, He cleared his throat, before speaking in a voice an octave lower:</p><p align="justify"></p><p></p><p>
  <em>By Ichor spilling in the East</em>
</p><p>
  <em>And blood of mortals in the west</em>
</p><p>
  <em>Dawn of the new godsreborn</em>
</p><p>
  <em>The mark of wisdomfindsits home</em>
</p><p></p><p>
  <em>Within the holdof his own kin</em>
</p><p>
  <em>The sword of Theseus blessed by sin</em>
</p><p>
  <em>And in theglory ofAthens throne</em>
</p><p>
  <em>Shall western world bathe evermore</em>
</p><p></p><p></p><p>Newt looked at him, puzzled. "Ichor is the blood of the gods... it's two mentions of blood in one couplet."</p><p>"Yeah, sounds bad. But then it talks about Athens' glory! That's so amazing, right?" Theseus grinned excitedly.</p><p>"I guess..." Newt didn't seem very convinced, his brow creasing in deep thought "It mentions your sword as well?"</p><p align="justify">Not mine! I mean, the prophecy is a riddle, as expected, but I'm pretty sure it refers to the sword of my great-grandfather, the most impressive hero of all times! Theseus, son of Aegeus! His swordmust bethe key to save the city. And it has to be wielded by one of his blood. So, anyone from my dads line, really.</p><p align="justify">Newt seemed even more puzzled than beforeThat's a bit too straightforward, dont you think? If it's supposed to be a riddle... are you sureit means his actual sword?</p><p align="justify">Well, it cant be his dead boner, so I'm guessing yes. And it even makes sense! The sword that Aegeus buried under a rock until Theseus was old enough to lift it and find it. The sword that he used to best his first enemies and make a name for himself before arriving to Athens already a hero, the sword that saved his life when wicked Medea tried to poison him! It must be a divine relic now! Charged with power! The current Theseus' eyes were gleaming. Newt put up a hand.</p><p align="justify">Yes, I grew up in Athens, I know all the stories of the life of Theseus. Where is that sword now?</p><p align="justify">See? Heres the problem- The prince settled back down Its lost.</p><p align="justify">Lost?!</p><p align="justify">A nod I sent a word beforehand, had all the library searched. The last record we have of it is when my great-uncle Arcamas took it with him to Troy. My grandfather mentions in his memories that the last time he saw it was when his brother and he set off to rescue their grandmother, who was a slave to Helen. However, on their way back they faced many enemies and, at some point, Arcamas lost the sword and replaced it with a Trojan spear. That was the last time it was ever seen.</p><p align="justify">A tense silence descended upon them, interrupted only by Fidos heavy breathing.</p><p align="justify">So the sword is still in Troy? Newt asked finally.</p><p align="justify">Its a possibility. Theseus agreed, looking up at the purpling sky. He should be departing soon if he wanted to reach the city before night fell.</p><p align="justify">Theseus</p><p align="justify">Yes?</p><p align="justify">What stupid thing did you come up with?</p><p align="justify">Theseus chuckled. Glad you ask. I convinced my dad to let me go in search of it!</p><p align="justify">Newt turned to him, frowning. You and what army?</p><p align="justify">No army. Troy might be burned down and abandoned, but we cant risk the Thracians to think they are under attack. No, Im going by myself and a small company.</p><p align="justify">Thats a long journey. Newt mumbled, looking down at his hands, that had stopped cradling through Fidos fur. He was right of course, Troy was wayfurther than Delphi and god knows how long will it take them to dig through the ruins of the city. Like looking for a needle in a haystack. Have you come to say goodbye then?</p><p align="justify">No Theseus wondered how to put it into words and decided to go straight to the point Ive come to ask you to accompany me.</p><p align="justify">What!?</p><p align="justify">Look Theseus raised his hands, trying to explain The outskirts are becoming more dangerous day by day, with all these southerners coming and turning to pillage. Its not advisable for you to remain here, living on your own in the middle of a forest.</p><p align="justify">What am I? A defenseless maiden?</p><p align="justify">Of course not! You are a skilled archer and the best tracker I know. And you are favored by an Olympian Goddess. But what if you are attacked by a mob? Or an invading army comes? I think, and father agrees, that it would be best for you to return to the city.</p><p align="justify">Newt huffed, crossing his arms over his chest I dont like it. Its too narrow, full of people and smells like piss.</p><p align="justify">I know. So the alternative is for you to come with me. Your skills will be of great use in the expedition and I cant promise the ship isnt gonna be crowded or will smell like flowers, but at least you wont be stuck in one place, worrying about me day and night, right?</p><p align="justify">Newt looked like he was gonna argue again but that last part actually made him shut his mouth. However, he wasnt sold yet, so Theseus pressed on.</p><p align="justify">Cmon. Killed and raped, probably in that order, by an invading army? Cramped in your fathers old home next to a rowdy market? Or going on to live a great adventure with me across the sea? We will be like the pairs of heroes of old! You can be my Pirithous.</p><p align="justify">Newt smiled involuntarily, raising his eyes to meet Theseus</p><p align="justify">You are aware of how he ended, right?</p><p align="justify">Theseus shrugged Unless you find your lust and try to ravish the queen of the Underworld, you are safe. And even if you change your mind on the celibacy thing I will be right there too, so you are still safe.</p><p align="justify">Now Newt full on snorted How reassuring. He said sarcastically. Then looked aside, into the darkening woods. What about the rest of the prophecy? The blood of gods spilled in the west?</p><p align="justify">"Could be Troy, could be even further west, irrelevant to our quest. I'm not planning to fight any god in a long-abandoned city in ruins, or dwell on lines I can't understand. I would rather focus on the ones I do, and I would love for you to help me out."</p><p align="justify">Newt smiledwhen Theseus gave him the<em>pretty please</em> look"I'll think about it."</p><p align="justify">Theseus beamed. You can bring Fido if you want. His fat ass could use an adventure as well.</p><p align="justify">The dog raised his head and looked at him with giant, betrayed eyes.</p><p align="justify">Dont listen to him Newt cooed, flattening the animals ears to its skull, hes just jealous of your powerful hindquarters.</p><p align="justify">His hindquarters are bigger than a mares Theseus kept teasing Speaking of which- I should go now or Ill be caught by Nyx.</p><p align="justify">Newt agreed. He and Fido accompanied Theseus to the edge of the forest, where he had left his horse tied to a tree, but the younger man was distracted the whole way, lost in deliberations.</p><p align="justify">We are leaving in five days, Theseus said, smiling gently. from the Athenian harbor.</p><p align="justify">He bent down to kiss the corner of Newts lips; a dry, chaste contact of barely any pressure, but long and warm, feeling the flutter of Newts eyelashes on his own skin. When he straightened up Newts eyes were closed. He opened them slowly and gently leaned in, to rest his forehead on Theseus shoulder for a good minute.</p><p align="justify">Ill think about it. He repeated, reluctantly stepping back.</p><p align="justify">Theseus beamedat him and mounted his horse. Coming from that man, its as close to an agreement as he was gonna get.</p>
