    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p dir="ltr">Luna sat in a compartment, swinging her feet while she read the Quibbler. She wore a faint smile on her face that she always wore, but this time it was different.</p><p dir="ltr">She had friends! Actual friends! Her bestfriends were two Gryffindors, Neville and Ginny. She sent them letters all summer and even sent some to Harry.</p><p dir="ltr">She didn't think Ron liked her very much, but that didn't bother her. Hermione was nice to her, but she could tell she didn't really like it when she talked about magical creatures her father wrote about, but it was okay, she was just a little close-minded. Maybe one day she'd come around.</p><p dir="ltr">The compartment door opened and Neville poked his face in. He gave her a confused look while he came in and sat across from her. "What's on your face?"</p><p dir="ltr">She brought her fingers to the glasses she was wearing and her smile widened. "They are called Spectrespecs and they help me see Wrackspurts." She explained.</p><p dir="ltr">"What are those?" Neville asked curiously.</p><p dir="ltr">"They are invisible creatures that make your brain go fuzzy."</p><p dir="ltr">He nodded his head in understanding. Harry Potter pokes his head through their compartment door. "Er, can I join you?"</p><p dir="ltr">"Of course." Luna beamed.</p><p dir="ltr">He sat down next to Luna happily. He asked about her glasses and happily explained everything to him as she did for Neville.</p><p dir="ltr">"How is the Quibbler doing?" Harry asked curiously.</p><p dir="ltr">"Amazing, the interview with you is still selling our magazine. Daddy is quite pleased."</p><p dir="ltr">Harry was about to reply when Romilda Vane poked her head into their compartment.</p><p dir="ltr">Luna noticed her already changed into her uniform and her skirt was shorter than the requirements. She gave Harry an alluring smile. "Harry, do you want to join me and my friends in our compartment?" She looked at Neville and Luna with disdain.</p><p dir="ltr">Luna was used to the looks, but Harry didn't seem so pleased to see the look she shot his two friends. "No, I'm fine here, thank you. These are my friends." He tried to force a friendly grin, but his displeasure showed on his face, making Romilda surprised.</p><p dir="ltr">"O-okay." Luna had a run in with her and her gang of Slytherin girls. She knew one of them to be Daphne Greengrass. Her sister Astoria was a year younger. She was quiet and kept to her small group of friends.</p><p dir="ltr">Luna didn't hate anyone, but she very much disliked those Slytherins. But she knew not all were bad and the ones that were, were only that way because they were misguided, she hoped one day they'd see their mistakes and grow. Would he live without pain?</p><p dir="ltr">She noticed across from her table was Draco at his. He sat his chin in his palm and looked very disinterested. She imagined Snape was happy with this arrangement.</p><p dir="ltr">Draco stared at nothing, unable to think of anything other than his interaction with the Dark Lord.</p><p dir="ltr">

  <em>voice</em>
  <em>. </em>

He had to do this, he had no choice.</p><p dir="ltr">The idea of being a death eater, excited him at first, but now... he feared Voldemort. If only his father didn't let the Dark Lord down. He didn't want to kill his headmaster, but he didn't want to be killed either.</p><p dir="ltr">Over the summer he had multiple thing prepared for the assassination, if one thing failed, another was ought to succeed. He didn't want to have to do it in person, he couldn't. He was a coward.</p><p dir="ltr">He hated himself for being a coward. He also had to fix a broken vanishing cabinet to get death eaters into the castle. God, he was so stressed out and depressed.</p><p dir="ltr">A part of him just wanted to kill himself, but he feared for what would happen if he did. Would the Dark Lord kill his family in retaliation? Most definitely.</p><p dir="ltr">"Draco, are you okay?" Pansy asked him softly.</p><p dir="ltr">He broke up with her so he could focus on his task. He didn't have time to have a girlfriend, especially one demanding as Pansy. She always wanted attention and wants to snog and no matter how much Draco wanted to snog her, he couldn't. He was treated like a man now, he couldn't enjoy the moment.</p><p dir="ltr">"Fine." He muttered. She was still his friend and he still cared for her in a way. "Just tired."</p><p dir="ltr">"You see Potter come in with Loony Luna?" Blaise snickered.</p><p dir="ltr">"I bet he snogged that freak." Pansy giggled.</p><p dir="ltr">"Definitely." Draco was only half-listening. He just wanted to retreat from social interaction.</p><p dir="ltr">He looked forward to when the feast ended and he could go off to bed, only for dark thoughts to swim within his brain.</p>
