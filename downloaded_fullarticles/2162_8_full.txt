  <em>
    <span>clack,</span>

<p>

<p>
  <span>He sits at the edge of her desk and eyes her with a smile. “Not what I meant.”</span>

<p>

<p>

<p>

<p>

<p>

<p>

<p>

<p>

Last time he and Theo visited, hospital-size barrels of hangover potion wouldn't have been enough to—” he remembers Isobel is, technically, his supervisor. Catches himself. “Help them. Er. We had a fun, but slightly overwhelmingly raucous time?”</span>

<p>

<p>

<p>

It thunders in his ears for a moment — an out-of-breath, gut-punched moment. “Thank Merlin I know Blaise is straight as a die, or I’d actually be jealous.”</span>

<p>

</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>
  <span>“Harry,” he says. It comes out as a question, which instantly makes him feel a bit stupid. </span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>Harry's smile almost falters, then valiantly stays on.</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“Forgotten me already? I know it's been a while.”</span>
</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>
  <span>It's Draco's turn to laugh, and he pulls back to gaze up at Harry's face.</span>
</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>
  <span>“You seemed like you needed a minute.”</span>
</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>
  <span>Draco disengages from their embrace. He reaches for Harry’s hand, the one that was in his hair, and pulls him to his feet. “I do. And I appreciate it.” He takes the mug from Harry’s other hand and places it on the table. “This is all a bit… sudden.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“Not expecting surprise visitors?” Harry asks with a pleased little smile. “Colour me relieved.”</span>
</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>

Tell him everything that he feels it’s still too early to tell, but that needs to be said somehow. </p>
<p>

</p>
<p>
  <span>“It's hard to tell sometimes. With you.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“Is it?”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“You're so… collected, all the time.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“I don't </span>
  <em>
    <span>feel </span>
  </em>
  <span>collected.”</span>
</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>
  <span>Harry places his hands on Draco's shoulders, puts an inch of distance between them. “Miss you? Oh, not at all.” He points at the mug on the table with a wry shrug. “I only crossed an ocean for that exceptional cup of tea.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>Something must have passed on Draco's face, because Harry cups his cheek then, runs his thumb under his eye. “You’re an idiot. Of course I missed you.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <em>
    <span>“I'm</span>
  </em>
  <span> the idiot? </span>
  <em>
    <span>You're</span>
  </em>
  <span> the one who traveled halfway round the world because you couldn't go another minute without seeing me.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>Harry smiles, a mixture of bashfulness and pride. “Yeah, I couldn't. And now I’ve gone through all that trouble for you. How do you suggest you make it up to me?”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“I have a few ideas… and time. Isobel gave me the day off tomorrow.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“Yeah, I know. I may have begged for her understanding of my special situation.”</span>
</p>
<p>

<p>
  <span>“I told her I expected you to fuck me so thoroughly, I'd need a good 48 hours to get my fill of you.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>Draco's mouth falls open in horror. “You </span>

  </em>
</p>
<p>
  <span>Harry bursts out laughing. “The actual content of that conversation will remain between me and Isobel.”</span>

<p>

<p>
  <span>“I've traveled this far for you. I can travel a short distance down the hall.”</span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>“Thank you.” He can't believe his luck. Harry Potter, his sexy, infuriating, wonderful, and very new lover — </span>

  </em>

  <em>
    <span>something</span>
  </em>
  <span>. Hasn't it? It </span>
  <em>
    <span>must</span>
  </em>

<p>
  <span>Instead he kisses him, tender and hot. “Thank you for coming, love.” And says once more, against Harry's smiling lips: “Thank you.”</span>
