          <div class="userstuff"><p> </p><p>
  <span>It was a risk, really. A stupid, irresponsible, selfish, risk. </p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Please Moony...just a few hours. Somewhere safe. Please...I can’t...this isn’t...I can’t find myself here. I might as well be given to those bloody soulsuckers.” </span>
</p><p> </p><p>

</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>On went their boots, their gloves. Zips and buttons were fastened. Remus spelled the fire out and Sirius extinguished the lights. They gave the cabin a last look over.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I don’t want to leave.” Sirius sighed, staring at the door. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I know…”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Going back to that mausoleum...it’s killing me.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I know…” Remus frowned, wishing with everything in him that he could change their situation.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Thank you.” So many emotions were imparted in those two words. Remus felt the despair, the gratitude. The love Sirius felt and his need for reassurance, comfort, company. The </span>
  <em>
    <span>‘I’m spiraling and I need you, I love you, you’re stopping me from floating away into nothing, shattering into dust. But I can’t do it alone, please, please, never leave. Don’t ever leave. You are my soul.’ </span>
  </em>
  <span> </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>It was a mirror to his own thoughts and he reached for Sirius, drawing him close and sinking into the arms that came up to cocoon him. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>They clung to each other, faces buried in the other’s neck. There were no more words. Just the air in and out of their lungs, ragged breath. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>It was an eternity but no time at all when they finally pulled apart, fingers lingering on sides as they stared into each other’s eyes. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <em>
    <span>Alright?</span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <em>
    <span>Yeah</span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <em>
    <span>We can do this.</span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <em>
    <span>We can do this.</span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Still no words. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Sirius wrapped his hand around the door handle and pulled. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Look Remus, snow.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Think it’s snowing in London?”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Probably not.” </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>They stepped outside into the light, fluffy, flakes.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“First of the season.” Sirius grinned, turning to pre-emptively silence Remus. “I don’t fucking care if it’s snowed up in Scotland or Russia or anywhere else. There are places the snow never melts, or stops falling. It actually doesn’t snow much in the arctic, did you know? So whatever other facts you have up there can stay up there. This is our first snow of the year.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“It is.” The corner of Remus’ lip quirked.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Thank you.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Sirius and Remus walked out into the clearing, hands clasped, and stared up at the clouds and the stars. Sirius stuck out his tongue until a flat flake landed atop, melting instantly. “I can’t remember the last time I enjoyed snow.” He muttered before catching another. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“You will. You’ll remember the snowball fights and hiking through the forest. You’ll remember how awful the snow looked on the way home from the Leaky, but how perfect it was on the windowsills. You’ll remember that you would always put a stasis spell on it so it would stay until spring.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“You’d only let me do the back windows of the flat where the neighbours couldn’t see.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Having perfectly fluffy sills after a week of winter rain tends to get noticed.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Spoilsport.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I try.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I love you anyways.</span>
  <span></span>
  <br/>
  <br/>
</p><p>
  <span>“After the war we’ll live somewhere you can put all the snow charms you want.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Deal.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Sirius caught three more snowflakes as they worked their way through the clearing and into the trees. And he continued to bombard Remus with facts about the arctic until they reached their designated spot for disapperating.   </span>
</p><p>
  <br/>
  <br/>
</p><p>
  <span>They landed in a tiny alley with a loud crack and clung hard to each other until their heads and stomachs stopped spinning. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>When they slipped inside Grimmauld Place, it was silent. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Where did the twins go?” Sirius whispered, loudly. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Shhhh…” Remus hissed, one hand up to signal that they should listen. After a minute, the barely audible sounds of cutlery could be heard from the direction of the kitchen. “Who the hell is eating at one in the morning? That twins were supposed to-”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Shhh…” Sirius mimicked, grinning wickedly. They stared hard at one another for another second before barely stifling a laugh. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>Footsteps approached and both men froze. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Severus said you were down for the night.” Kingsley’s large frame took up the entire doorframe.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“We are. Were.” Sirius answered quickly.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“...you’re in muggle winter clothing.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Yes...it...the heating is very poor upstairs. We came down to get...layers.” Remus replied.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I see.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Which we got...so...back upstairs we go.” </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“The Weasley boys are in the kitchen. I’m sure they’d like to see you, know that you...got your layers safely.” Kingsley had raised a knowing brow. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“...oh. Er...thanks. We’ll stop in, then. Thanks.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“You’re welcome. You should also brush the snow from your hair incase someone else is up.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Right, good point. You’re a good man.” Sirius reached up to pat a strong shoulder. “We have snowballs if you’re interested.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“...”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Right. Not. Kitchen it is. Come on, Remus. The twins want to see our coats.” </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Torn between embarrassment and the urge to laugh himself sick, Remus nodded, offering a grateful smile before following. God forbid the man see inside the satchel and find the mishmash of drugs, charmed snowballs, half-eaten sandwiches, and a book of muggle fairy tales.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“When the fuck did Kingsley show up? He’s off rota tonight.” Sirius muttered, one hand up to push open the swinging door.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Just be grateful it wasn’t Moody.”</span>
