</p><p>The darkness had been lying within him, dormant but waiting, for eighteen long years. And all the while it grew, feeding off the scorn and hatred of the Dursleys, of Malfoy, of innumerable others.</p><p>
  <em>Never tickle a sleeping dragon. </em>
</p><p>He didn’t know when it had begun to stir. Perhaps it began when he cast <em>Sectumsempra </em>on Malfoy and a rush of elation passed through him like a thunderbolt instead of the horror he was expecting to feel. Maybe it was upon Dumbledore’s death, when he had thought to himself <em>well, it was about time. </em>Maybe it was even further back, when he secretly smiled at people thinking he was the Heir of Slytherin; of being just a little bit disappointed when he was not.</p><p>Whatever its provenance, it was here, and it was immutable and immovable.</p><p>“The Dark Lord is dead,” proclaimed Harry Potter triumphantly, to a wave of cheers from the crowd.</p><p>He bent, picked up Voldemort’s wand. A hush fell. The crowd looked on eagerly, certain he was going to snap it in two as a symbol of victory.</p><p>Harry raised the wand high above his head. Smiled. “Long live the Dark Lord.”</p><p>A handful of people laughed nervously.</p><p>It was the last sound they ever made.</p>
