<p>Falling Behind</p>
<p>~</p>
<p>Harry groaned. “That was terrible!” </p>
<p>Draco snorted. “Like that wasn’t what you were planning on saying earlier?” </p>
<p>Harry coughed. “Whether or not that’s true, and I’m not saying it is, it’s irrelevant now since you made us a proper meal.” </p>
<p>“Humph.” Draco relaxed in Harry’s arms, apparently mollified. </p>
<p>“So,” said Harry, “what should we do tomorrow?” </p>
<p>Draco pursed his lips. “We have two days before we return to work, we should take advantage of them.” </p>
<p>“Isn’t that what we’ve been doing?” Harry exhaled. “Maybe we should talk to our colleagues. We’re behind on the news.” </p>
<p>Draco shrugged. “It couldn’t hurt.” </p>
<p>~</p>
<p>Harry began to stand, but Draco clung. “What are you doing?” he asked.</p>
<p>“I thought I’d Floo Ron, see what’s happening at the Ministry.” </p>
<p>“Right <i>now</i>?” Draco rolled his eyes. “We’ve two more days suspension, Harry. Let’s wait until the last day to catch up.” </p>
<p>“But we’re probably behind,” said Harry weakly. </p>
<p>“All we can do about work problems is worry for two days.” Draco brushed his lips against Harry’s. “I, for one, would rather not think about anything more strenuous than what position I’d like to try with you next.” </p>
<p>Harry grinned. “Well, when you put it that way—”</p>
