        <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Work Text:</h3>
          <div class="userstuff"><p>Dudley Dursley answered the door after checking carefully.  Through the distorted glass he could see that there was a pretty girl standing there.  He opened the door and smiled broadly at her.  A very pretty girl with blonde hair and red lips.   She seemed to be looking over his shoulder into the depths of the house.  </p><p>"Is Harry in?  Harry Potter?" she asked in a low, sultry voice.  </p><p>"Potter doesn't live here.  But perhaps I can help you?"  He tried not to leer.  He had learned that it put women off.</p><p>"I know he doesn't live here, but I thought perhaps he might be here on a visit.  The holidays, you know," she said, looking left and right as if he might have Potter tucked away behind the frost lined rosebushes.</p><p>"Haven't seen him," Dudley said, letting the resentment into his tone.  It was quite annoying.  This was the third woman this week looking for Potter.  The other two had been pretty, too.  It wasn't right, beautiful women looking up Potter this way.  "Why do you want him, anyway?"</p><p>She laughed as if he had made a good joke.  He stood up straighter and said, "No, really.  Why the bloody hell would women be showing up here looking for Potter?"</p><p>She eyed him up and down and then decided to answer. Her eye took on the gleam of a conspirator sharing deep secrets as she leaned forward a little and said, "You’re his cousin, right?  So you know he defeated the Dark Lord at Halloween.  He's a hero, he's young and good looking, and he's rich.  Of course he's going to be followed about.  From your words I take it I'm not the only woman who's come here?  And I thought I was so clever!"  She pouted, apparently vexed with herself that her idea hadn't been original.</p><p>"Third this week.  But you're all daft.  Potter, rich?  He doesn't have a penny in his pocket.  Good looking?  He's a scrawny git with glasses.  And he's about as heroic as a mouse."  The fool always ran away when confronted.  If he could.  Dudley smiled at some fond memories.</p><p>"Are you sure this is the Dursley residence?" The young woman leaned to check the number.  "Of course Potter's rich.  He got that reward.  Fifty thousand galleons is nothing to sneeze at, you know.  Plus all he must have inherited.  He could be ugly as a bucket with all that, but perhaps you've not seem him recently?  He's a very nice looking man!  Although he is a little short." </p><p>Galleons? Then she was one of Them.  Dudley scowled.  Who else would come looking for Potter, after all.  And...money?  Jealousy grew in Dudley's little heart.  His mouth opened and he thought of the perfect thing to say.  </p><p>"I meant, why are you girls showing up here?  Potter's gay, you know."</p><p>"Gay?" the woman's brow wrinkled as she repeated the word.</p><p>"Light on his feet?  Camp as a row of tents?  Likes boys?" He slammed the door shut.  Instead of going away, that idiots were shouting again, louder and...pop...now there were five!  Dudley slumped against the door and groaned.  </p><p>The next morning the headline of the Prophet screamed, "Harry Potter's Secret Goblin Lover?"</p><p>"Where are they getting this tripe?" Potter sighed against the warm naked chest of his not-at-all-goblin lover.  </p><p>"Who cares," Severus Snape muttered into the messy hair shoved up under his nose.  "We can take advantage of it.  If the truth ever comes out, no one will believe it any more than they believe the dozen even more ridiculous stories that came before."</p><p>Harry nodded, then said, "Severus," and began to gnaw on the bony shoulder nearest his lips.
