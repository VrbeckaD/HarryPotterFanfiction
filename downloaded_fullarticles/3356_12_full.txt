        <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Work Text:</h3>
          <div class="userstuff"><p>Now that he has Sirius, he’s not going to ask anything that’s not reasonable.<br/> <br/> Right?<br/> <br/> Because that would be insane. That would be cruel. And he’s not cruel. Maybe he’s not as nice as people seem to think he is, people who know him personally, that is. He’s not that nice. But he’s not <em>cruel</em>, either. And if he wants to be, if he thinks for a second about pushing Sirius against the closed door, it’s just that he’s been alone for too long. And they’re just thoughts. He would never act on them.<br/> <br/> He’s going to be kind to Sirius. Yeah, that’s what he’s going to be. He’s going to make tea and give Sirius a blanket and put the man to the sofa. He’s going to ask about how Sirius has been. And he’s not going to ask about things he’s not supposed to. He’s <em>not.</em> He’s going to do this <em>right.</em><br/> <br/> Right?<br/> <br/> His hands are shaking a little. He hides them behind his back.<br/> <br/> “Remus?” Sirius says, biting his lower lip, his eyes going back and forth on Remus’ face. Sirius’ voice isn’t completely steady but not afraid, either. Sirius knows Remus is nice enough.<br/> <br/> Or, actually, Sirius knows Remus was nice enough fourteen years ago. Fourteen years ago, Remus never talked back. Remus never pushed Sirius against the wall and asked what the hell it was that was always lingering in Sirius’ eyes, was it doubt, was it <em>really</em>, what fucking <em>right </em>did Sirius have to doubt <em>Remus</em> when he knew perfectly well that Remus would have done <em>anything, anything</em> for a chance that Sirius might love him -<br/> <br/> But no.<br/> <br/> No, that’s not how it was. That’s how he thinks it was, and he’s been thinking about it for fourteen years.<br/> <br/> Anything changes in fourteen years.<br/> <br/> Memories change.
