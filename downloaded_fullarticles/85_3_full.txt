    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>The great hall is restless with activity. Its the middle of October, and there are floating candles and pumpkins scattered throughout the air. Students are laughing and talking loudly, and sixth-year Hope Mikaelson cant hear herself think.</p><p></p><p>She sighs, knitting her eyebrows in irritation and drumming her fingers against the hard wood of the table. When her family ring catches in a dent, she clenches her fist with another sigh. This one even heavier than before.</p><p></p><div class="gs">
  <p></p>
  <div class="">
    <p></p>
    <div class="ii gt">
      <p></p>
      <div class="a3s aXjCH">
        <p></p>
        <div dir="auto">
          <p></p>
          <p>She understands her fathers pride over the family name, but having to constantly wear the Mikealson ring annoys her. Sometimes it felt suffocating, and right now, squeezed between countless bodies, its enough for her irritation to spike once again. </p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Hope raises her hand to her collar and loosens her Slytherin tie, looking around. Shes confused as to why food hasnt magically appeared in front of them yet.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Its about thirty minutes past dinner time, and the students still have no idea why theyre waiting. They were told to come to the great hall early for dinnerHope can faintly remember something about an important announcementbut nothing has happened yet.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>The purebloods eyes wander to the front, where the headmaster and professors are sitting regally. She notices that some of them look quite anxious.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>She wonders why, but after a few seconds of thinking, she decides that she doesnt care.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Before long, the students fall into a rush of silence as Headmaster Dumbledore stands up.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Quiet, quiet, Dumbledore says, as if hes Merlin himself or something, and Hope almost rolls her eyes. She glances away for a second and accidentally meets the stare of that curly-haired boy from Hufflepuff shooting her heart eyes. What was his name?<br/><br/></p>
          <p><br/>Lesley?<br/><br/></p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Landon? Oh, right. Landon. Thats the name of the boy staring at her without trying to hide it at all.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p><em>He should really stop doing that</em>, Hope thinks absently before turning back to Dumbledore.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Good evening, everyone, Dumbledore greets, pausing dramatically. Tonight, we welcome two students from Beauxbatons Academy of Magic that wish to finish out their studies here at Hogwarts.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>A murmur of excitement spreads across the dining hall, but Hope doesnt share it. Instead, her shoulders sag in disappointment. She just wants to eat.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>We never get transfers! a fifth year whispers excitedly across from her, and Hope grumbles underneath her breath. Shes starving, and done with waiting.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Please embrace them pleasantly as you did your fellow classmates, Dumbledore finishes, and Hope didnt realize he was still talking.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>He moves to the side and gestures to his right, and Hope catches a glimpse of two girls she hadnt noticed before. One is slightly taller than the other and clearly blonde, with an upturned nose and small features. Her robes are plain but immaculate. When Hope turns to look at the other one, she does a double-take.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>The girl is beautiful, thats obvious. Her hair is slightly wavy, a gorgeous brown color that Hope cant tear her gaze away from. She blinks and wills herself to look somewhere else, anywhere else, but she finds that she cant. </p>
          <p></p>
          <p>The girls eyes have a fire in them that directly contrast Hopes own blue ones, and something just underneath Hopes skin sparks. It jolts her back to reality.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Hope glances away, her heart beating with a quickness she doesnt yet realize. She stares down at the table, but all she can see is pouty lips and innocent eyes. She risks a glance back and the other girls eyes catch her own for barely a split second, but its enough to scare Hope to never look again.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Minutes pass before Hope regains herself, the magic in her veins thrumming with attention.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Elizabeth Saltzman! Professor McGonagall calls, and then Hope realizes theyre actually being sorted by that stupid fucking hat. Her eyebrows furrow with another discovery.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p><em>Saltzman...as in the famous vampire hunter Alaric Saltzman?</em> Hope thinks. <em>As in one of the greatest enemies of her father? As in...</em></p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Saltzman. Great. Another fucking muggleborn, Penelope Park curses next to her, a snobby frown down-turning her lips as the blonde walks up to the hat. Her friends hot, though. Heres to hoping shes at least a half-blood.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Hope frowns then, too, and turns away from Penelope. Blood purity differences are still alive and well in Hogwarts, and although Hopes family instills these values in her daily, shes never really caught on. Shell put on a show, but she doesnt really care how much magic someone has in their bloodline. Even though she knows she should.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Her father, however, really cares about it. In their world, hes known as the Great Evil, having a history of committing many crimes and getting away with all of them.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>The great hall grows quiet again as the sorting hat is placed on top of Elizabeths head. It takes a minute or so before it finds its answer.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>GRYFFINDOR!</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Elizabeth looks almost pleased as she takes off the hat and stands up. Everyone claps pleasantly like theyre supposed to, and she quickly joins the Gryffindor table. Hope almost laughs when she sees how disappointed Ravenclaw and Hufflepuff are. On the contrary, her own house is indifferent. The second they heard Elizabeths last name, they stopped paying attention. No muggleborn would ever get sorted into Slytherin.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Josette Saltzman! Hope only has a second to think how pretty the name is before Penelope starts to rant about blood purity again. </p>
          <p></p>
          <p>P, if you dont shut the fuck up... Hope trails off, and Penelope gets the message to stop talking clearly enough.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>The hall is still quiet as Josette walks up to the stool, her footsteps resounding loudly in the silence. She sits down, and McGonagall places the hat on her head.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Nothing is immediately said, and just when Hope thinks the hat has made a decision another minute goes by. Josette is whispering furiously, but strangely no one can hear a single thing. She appears to be...<em>arguing with the hat?</em> Its almost seven minutes later when the hat finally decides.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>SLYTHERIN!</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>A hush tumbles over the already silent body of students, and Hopes heart gets stuck in her throat. It pounds against her vocal cords, and no sound allows itself to come out.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>She watches as Josette stands up, but the entire Slytherin table is frozen.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Its unheard of</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>
            <em>A muggleborn in Slytherin? What the fuck was the hat thinking?</em>
          </p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Hopes eyes still remain unblinking until she realizes that everyone is looking at her for direction. She's only a sixth year, but her last name is enough for everyone to practically worship her or be afraid of her at this school.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p><em>I don't know what to do</em>, Hope wants to scream, wants to tell them that she's just hungry and starving for some mashed potatoes. Penelope elbows her pointedly, and Hope realizes that she still hasn't said anything yet. </p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Josette is standing awkwardly in front of McGonagall, not knowing what to do also. For her part, McGonagall doesn't know what to do either. The situation is almost laughable, really.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Hope feels her bones like lead, her joints like jelly and it's hard to move. Her insides are cement, and when she looks up she only sees Josette.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>She lifts her elbows off the table, the magic inside commanding her every move before she can think about it. Her hands collide together and Hope knows that thisthis momentwill change her life completely. She starts to clap, and the entire Slytherin table follows suit after a moment of hesitance. Josette smiles, and Hope wonders what she was even worried about in the first place.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Just before the applause ends, a glass shatters meters away, dropped by the Divination teacherSybill Trelawney.Seconds go by, but she makes no move to pick it up or use a spell to clean it.In fact, she's frozen, staring blankly in front of her like nothing else exists. In a monotone expression, her voice begins to carry throughout the great hall.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>"When the sun rises,</p>
          <p>the end of peace will set,</p>
          <p>and Merlin will stir in anger</p>
          <p>with the first hour of the morning.</p>
          <p>He will reign destruction on nature,</p>
          <p>turning the seas to ice</p>
          <p>and the forests to fire.</p>
          <p>Two will need to come together</p>
          <p>one purest of blood,</p>
          <p>from the house of the snake,</p>
          <p>and another with none to her name,</p>
          <p>but from much of the same.</p>
          <p>Only their unioncan truly free us from Merlins wrath.</p>
          <p>And the Great Evils heir</p>
          <p>shall becomeour savior."</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>At the words Great Evil, Hope sees everyone's heads turn in her direction once again. She watches as Josette's eyes follow everyone else's, and suddenly Hope feels very, very insecure. She keeps her head up, her shoulders and back ramrod straight, but inside she's freaking out.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>Sybill Trelawney and her family are known for their prophecies, and Hope vaguely wonders if this is one of those cases. Trelawney snaps out of it a second later, looking around in confusion, and Hope's heart drops to her stomach in dread. Although Trelawney is a bat-crazy lunatic and her predictions have no real value, Hope feels like something is wrong deep down.</p>
          <p></p>
          <p>She doesn't feel so hungry anymore.</p>
        </div>
