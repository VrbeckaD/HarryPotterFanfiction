          <div class="userstuff"><p class="p1">1. First Year</p><p class="p1"> </p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> Dean was too excited to sleep. He and his new roommates — one of whom was apparently famous — had spent most of the night getting to know each other. But now the moon shone in through the window and, one by one, the breathing of the other boys had deepened as they drifted off. </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> Dean sighed, rolling over in hopes that his mind would let him rest before the first day tomorrow. But as he began thinking again about learning <em>magic </em>and pet rats and toads, a voice caught his attention. </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> “Psst. Hey, you still awake?” The voice said. </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> Dean sat up, and found Seamus Finnigan watching him from the bed next to his. </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> Dean whispered, “Yeah.” </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> Apparently taking that as an invitation, Seamus climbed out of his own bed and plopped down on Dean’s. He was a small boy, short and round, and he grinned at Dean underneath a freckled nose. </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> “I can’t sleep either. Too busy thinking about tomorrow, and I’m not used to being away from home.” Seamus said, his Irish lilt pleasant to Dean’s ears. </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> “Same here,” Dean agreed. </span>
</p><p class="p2">
  <span class="s2"> They began talking again, trying to keep their voices low. Dean began to worry out loud about how they would find their classes, whether it would be hard to learn magic, whether they had to take calculus, but Seamus’ Dumbledore impression made Dean laugh so hard that Ron Weasley almost woke up.
