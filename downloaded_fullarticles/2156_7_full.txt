    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>Draco was eight years old, sitting on the patio with his mother when the first of the drawings appeared. It was a warm day, and one of the house elves had brought them a pitcher of sweet tea, and with one hand on the cold glass he used the hand opposite to work up one of his sleeves, revealing little doodles on the inside of his forearm. His mother, busy with her spoon dipping into her sorbet, glanced up at the movement and dropped her spoon with a clatter. She sucked in a sharp breath.</p><p><br/>
"You shouldn't draw on yourself Draco, you have plenty of parchment for that," She offered a strained smile.</p><p><br/>
"I didn't draw on myself, mother."</p><p><br/>
"That's what I was afraid of," Narcissa replied, her delicate hand worrying the pearls at her neck. "Pull your sleeve down."</p><p><br/>
"But it's so hot!"</p><p><br/>
"I know darling, but you mustn't let your father see."</p><p><br/>
"But I didn't draw themâ€”" Draco started, and his mother's hand touched his own.</p><p><br/>
"You're special, Draco, you've always been special. Just trust me for now, please?"</p><p><br/>
Draco frowned at his mother's stressed expression, and the smile that didn't reach her eyes. He rolled his sleeve back down.</p><p>â€”â€”â€”</p><p>When Draco was ten, his father began sculpting him into a 'proper Malfoy heir'.
