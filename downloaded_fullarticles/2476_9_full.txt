          <div class="userstuff"><p>
  <u>
    <strong>February 14. 2010</strong>
  </u>
</p>
<p>
  <strong>4:58am</strong>
</p>
<p>The hour is ungodly early as he startles awake, silencing his alarm and rolling to his feet in one fell swoop. He glances toward the bed and smiles at the sight of messy locks peeking out the top of the blanket. Ever so quietly, he tiptoes toward the loo.</p>
<p>“Draco?”</p>
<p>It is a sleepy mumble, but he freezes instantly, listening as breathing resumes it’s steady rhythm until he is sure he is asleep. Draco doesn’t breathe again until the door clicks closed behind him and he is facing the mirror.</p>
<p>Nineteen hours, two minutes to go. “Mission: Avoid the Auror” was well underway toward being successful.</p>
<p>He runs the water and showers as quickly as he can.</p>
<p>“You can do this.” He mumbles, running the blade quickly across his chin.</p>
<p>His reflection smirks back at him.</p>
<p>“Shut it, you.” He hisses as his mirrored image holds up its hands in mock defense.</p>

<p>“Thank you.” He murmurs over his shoulder, smiling as he walks through the gates.</p>
<p>“Step right up! Try your hand!”</p>
<p>Draco’s gaze flits from side to side as he weaves his way between the booths, looking for Harry.</p>
<p>“This way, sir! Over here!”</p>
<p>Draco smiles, shaking his head as he continues on. The sounds, the crowd, the games and rides mingle on the air as he surveys the crowd for Harry.</p>
<p>“Step right up. Try your luck!” Another voice shouts near his head, and Draco steps to the side to avoid him.</p>
<p>“Oi, Malfoy. Try this one!”</p>
<p>He whips around and his eyes narrow as he sees George Weasley standing in front of a booth wearing the most ridiculous hat he’s ever seen.</p>
<p>“Weasley.”</p>
<p>“Draco.”</p>
<p>“Nice hat.”</p>
<p>“Care to play?”</p>
<p>“No, thanks. Where’s Harry?”</p>
<p>“Who?”</p>
<p>“Oh, for fuck’s sake. I played this game with your father this morning.”</p>
<p>“Try your luck?”</p>
<p>Draco stares at the milk bottles lined up behind George.</p>
<p>“Sounds like a terrible way to celebrate Valentine's Day.”</p>
<p>“It’s nearly midnight. Barely Valentine’s Day, Malfoy.”</p>
<p>“What if I win?”</p>
<p>“You get a prize.”</p>
<p>“Harry?”</p>
<p>“Not allowed to give away people, Malfoy. Even the Muggles have standards.”</p>
<p>Draco finds himself laughing out loud. “Fine, I’ll play, George.”</p>
<p>“That’ll be three tickets.”</p>
<p>Draco hands over the tickets and sidles up to the booth. He looks side to side and surreptitiously reaches for his wand.</p>
<p>“No, Malfoy.”</p>
<p>“What?”</p>
<p>“No magic.”</p>
<p>“I can’t possibly do this without magic.”</p>
<p>“It’s the rules.” George shrugs.</p>
<p>“Fine.” Draco grumbles and reaches for the rings. He tosses them through the air and watches, his lips pursed as they plummet to the ground.</p>
<p>“Shit luck. Try again?”</p>
<p>Draco nods.</p>
<p>“Three more tickets.”</p>
<p>Draco hands them over and tries again.</p>
<p>“Closer.”</p>
<p>“Barely.” He grumbles and hands George another three tickets. Planting his feet, he bends his knees and squares his shoulders.</p>
<p>“Be the bottle, Draco.”</p>
<p>“Shhhh. I’m concentrating.”</p>
<p>“You know, you weren’t bad at quidditch. Thought you’d be better at this.”</p>
<p>“Weasley, would you shut up.”</p>
<p>“No, really. Harry talks about it all the time. Ooooh, Draco, so talented.”</p>
<p>“I was a seeker, George.”</p>
<p>“Interesting. A bit slow for a seeker, aren’t you?”</p>
<p>Draco chucks a ring at his head.</p>
<p>“Might’ve made a decent beater.”</p>
<p>“Give me another go.”</p>
<p>“Last three tickets, mate.”</p>
<p>Draco blinks as he hands them to George and realizes all of his tickets are gone.</p>
<p>“I’ll buy more.”</p>
<p>“Ooooh, do you have any money?”</p>
<p>“No.”</p>
<p>“Tickets cost money, Malfoy.”</p>
<p>“Lend me some.”</p>
<p>“No can do, Draco. Bad for business to pay for the customers. You’ll need to win fair and square.”</p>
<p>Draco rolls his eyes. “I’ve been playing for over an hour, Weasley. I haven’t hit a bottle yet.”</p>
<p>“Rotten luck, mate. Last try.”</p>
<p>Draco finds that he is nervous, sweating even as he holds the ring in his hand and lines up with the bottle.</p>
<p>He tosses the ring and it misses.</p>
<p>Swearing, he lines himself up again. The ring flies from his fingers, circling the neck of the bottle before sliding to the ground below the table.</p>
<p>“So close!” George cheers.</p>
<p>Draco’s heart is pounding in his head as he looks at the last ring in his hand. He closes his eyes, taking a deep breath as his arm winds backward. His shoulder rotates and then his fingers are opening and it is flying through the air. He opens his eyes just in time to see George’s wand hand flick and the ring settle atop the closest milk bottle.</p>
<p>“Look at that, you’re a winner!”</p>
<p>“You did that.”</p>
<p>“I did no such thing.”</p>
<p>“I watched you!”</p>
<p>“Think I’d have made you a bigger winner. 25 points, mate. Fairly pathetic.”</p>
<p>“George.”</p>
<p>“Consolation prize.” George is holding out a small box, a smirk playing at the corner of his lips. “Go on. Take it and move along, I’ve a line of people behind you. Don’t want to get fired on my first day, Malfoy.”</p>
<p>Draco takes it, holding the box in one hand and his half eaten sandwich in the other.</p>
<p>“What’s this?”</p>
<p>“Go on. Stop blocking my queue, Malfoy! Don’t want anyone else jealous they don’t get the same prize. Move along!”</p>
<p>Draco rolls his eyes and heads away from the stall. It is nearly midnight, on Valentine’s Day, and he hasn’t seen Harry yet. He has no one but himself to blame, but it stings nonetheless. Holidays are important to Harry, and he was selfish to ignore that. Again.</p>
<p>He moves to open the box, but then he’s being shunted along with the crowd and afraid he’ll drop it. He shoves it into his coat pocket and looks for an escape from the horde of people. He realizes they’re in a queue now, packed so tightly he can barely lift his arms.</p>
<p>“Excuse me.” He’s trying to step around the people behind him, to leave the line and find Harry. They ignore him, pushing him forward. “Angelina?”</p>
<p>“Keep walking, Draco.”</p>
<p>“I— I uh, I don’t want to ride this. I need to find Harry.”</p>
<p>She simply smiles, eyes gazing straight ahead.</p>
<p>“Next!”</p>
<p>Draco blinks and realizes he’s standing at the front of the line.</p>
<p>“Weasley?”</p>
<p>“Where’s your ticket?”</p>
<p>“What ticket?”</p>
<p>“For the Ferris wheel.” He jerks his thumb behind him.</p>
<p>“I’m looking for Harry. I don’t want to ride it.”</p>
<p>“No can do, mate. Didn't you look at your prize?”</p>
<p>“Oh.” Draco is fumbling with the box in his pocket. “No, I— it was too crowded and I—.”</p>
<p>“You’re holding everyone up. Open it.”</p>
<p>Draco’s fingers close around the box as he huffs. “Give me just a second, Ron.”</p>
<p>The box is light, a rectangle about the length of his finger and not much wider. Inside lays a single ride ticket and, he grins, teeth sucking into his bottom lip, a green Chinese finger trap.</p>
<p>“That’s the ticket!” Ron is pushing him forward into the bucket car and sliding the door closed behind him.</p>
<p>“Weasley! Wait! Where’s Harry?”</p>
<p>A sudden explosion of fireworks is surrounding them as the Ferris wheel begins. Draco looks wildly out the window. It’s midnight. He missed it. He missed all of Valentine’s Day. He missed Harry.</p>
<p>“Put it on.”</p>
<p>Draco whips around to look at the other side of the bucket.</p>
<p>“Harry!”</p>
<p>“Put it on, Draco.”</p>
<p>“I’m so sorry. I know today was important to you, and I’ve ruined it all. It was so selfish and so stupid. I shouldn’t have run out this morning. I shouldn’t have ignored your envelopes.”</p>
<p>“Draco, put it on.”</p>
<p>“I thought you were hurt or dead. And then I thought you were leaving me. I really thought… I thought I’d finally mucked it up for good. Even the savior of the wizarding world only has so much patience. And you think I’m uninterested in marriage. Or you. Or us. But none of it’s true. I love you. I want to continue proving that to you for the rest of our lives. I don’t want to pull away. I want to marry you, Harry. So you can’t leave. I’m going to fight for you. I don’t want this to end. I—”</p>
<p>Harry is reaching forward and suddenly the toy prize is in his palm and he’s so close to him, Draco can barely breathe as the panic and exhaustion of the day descends on him.</p>
<p>“It’s not Valentine's Day any longer, Draco. It’s after midnight. Put. It. On.”</p>
<p>“Okay! Fine. But, are you listening to me, Harry!” Draco takes the trap and jams it onto his finger. “There! Are you happy? Will you—”</p>
<p>Draco gasps as Harry reaches over and suddenly they’re wearing it on their fingers together. He pulls away and finds their hands firmly bound.</p>
<p>“We’re stuck.”</p>
<p>Harry is staring at him, eyes dancings with merriment. “It’s supposed to be symbolic.”</p>
<p>“Now we’re stuck?”</p>
<p>“Well, yes. I’m not leaving you, Draco.”</p>
<p>“You’re… stuck with me?”</p>
<p>“I want to be with you. Bound to you. For life.”</p>
<p>Draco is silent.</p>
<p>“Even when you pull away, I’ll stay.”</p>
<p>“But… Valentine’s Day is important to you.”</p>
<p>He shrugs. “So are you. And you didn’t want to get engaged then. I understand.”</p>
<p>“But Valentine’s Day is over.”</p>
<p>“Yes.”</p>
<p>“And I want to.”</p>
<p>“You want to what?”</p>
<p>“Marry you.”</p>
<p>“Good.” Harry is laughing. “Then push forward. Release it.”</p>
<p>“So sappy.” Draco rolls his eyes, bringing their fingers together. He gasps as the trap disappears, shrinking from the middle toward each of their fingers.</p>
<p>“Harry— how… how did you get this?” He’s staring at his hand, holding it into the air. The gold band glints in the colorful blinking lights cast by the fireworks and carnival lights.</p>
<p>“Your dad.”</p>
<p>“You saw him?”</p>
<p>“Yes.”</p>
<p>“In Azkaban?”</p>
<p>“Pulled a few strings. Asked his permission.”</p>
<p>“You asked my father’s permission?”</p>
<p>“Of course. Tradition is important to you.”</p>
<p>“This is not exactly a traditional relationship.”</p>
<p>“You’re worth it.”</p>
<p>“You went to Azkaban and asked my father’s permission to marry me, and arranged all of this?”</p>
<p>Harry nodded. “Is that a yes?”</p>
<p>The Ferris wheel has come to a halt now, Ron is opening the door and they’re exiting the ride. The lights and magic, rides and faceless people begin to fade, dissipating in tiny bursts of magic around them. Ron trots off, disappearing into the dark with Angelina and George.</p>
<p>“You— you did all of this?”</p>
<p>“George helped. Ron helped. Angelina helped. But, yes.”</p>
<p>“An entire fair?”</p>
<p>“Carnival in a Can. A new product George created.” He laughs. “This was the test run.”</p>
<p>“It’s fabulous.” He’s staring around, and realizes they’re in an empty lot now. The ground is wet and the air is cold. He turns toward Harry, glancing from the ring on his finger, back to his face.</p>
<p>“Draco?”</p>
<p>“Yes. Yes, Harry. The answer is yes.” He intertwines their fingers as they walk back toward the sidewalk, kissing his knuckles chastely.</p>
<p>“Let’s go home, Harry.” He wraps an arm around his shoulders. “And while we walk, I’m going to finish this.” He laughs, opening the takeaway container and eating another bite.</p>
<p>An after Valentine’s proposal, his favorite sandwich and a man who thinks he worth it.</p>
<p>“You’ve got yourself a groom, Potter.”</p>
<p>Harry laughs and Draco leans in to kiss him.</p>
<p>“I do.”</p></div>
