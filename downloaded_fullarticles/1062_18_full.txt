But most of all, Harry had a smile so <em> sweet, </em> angels could cry. Hell, <em> Tom </em>could cry.</p><p> </p><p>He kind of wanted to cry at this moment, to be frank.</p><p> </p><p>He clicked on, going on and on and <em> on </em>— until…</p><p> </p><p>Ugh.</p><p> </p><p>Kissing scene.</p><p> </p><p>Luna, all cozying up in the Protagonist’s space, seemed very pleased with herself. Tom leaned a bit back, if only to give himself the illusion of escaping his fate.</p><p> </p><p>“Tom,” she whispered <em> oh so breathily </em>and the man in question made a disgusted sound.</p><p> </p><p>Then she — jumped back out of the blue, and <em> Ginny the fucking depression girl </em>found him together with her love rival.</p><p> </p><p>“Ah,” he intoned and tried to ride out the wave.</p><p> </p><p>***</p><p> </p><p>The next morning brought an ominous silence.</p><p> </p><p>Tom, befuddled, opened the settings to check the music. Yet, he found it as fresh as it was back when he started the game. This could not be right. </p><p> </p><p>Just to make sure his sound system hadn’t given an error, he played something from the Internet; but there were no issues. Everything was satisfactory.</p><p> </p><p>Except for the music itself.</p><p> </p><p>It wasn’t like it had a particular charm, Tom thought to himself. He was currently waiting for Ginny to accompany him to school. It was merely that… the song had been a fixed variable in the game, ever since the beginning. Lighthearted, dulcet notes of the piano. Who <em> didn’t </em> feel lightened when they listened to it?</p><p> </p><p>It was just gone. Like a fart in the wind. (He didn’t think this was the time to wake jokes, but he felt like he <em> needed it.) </em></p><p> </p><p>
  <strike>This was giving him the creeps.</strike>
</p><p> </p><p>“Oh, well,” the Protagonist sighed at last, tired of waiting for her. “Guess I’ll just go without her today.”</p><p> </p><p>The character agonized over his decision the whole walk, so it was quite annoying to Tom’s ears. When he reached the school, he was greeted promptly by his lovely Harry.</p><p> </p><p>“Hi, Tom!” he said, cheeks flushed and out of breath. What was up with his state? “Um… <em> hi.” </em></p><p> </p><p>Raising an eyebrow, Tom smiled at him. “Hi to you too. What’s up with you this fine morning, my dear?”</p><p> </p><p>“Ginny called me about what happened yesterday, by the way,” he continued without minding Tom’s question. “You—“</p><p> </p><p>Harry bit his lip, then gave a sheepish look. “You kind of left her hanging this morning, you know. You two always come together.”</p><p> </p><p>“I know,” the Protagonist answered him. “I’m just so tired of waking her up! That dummy should be more responsible!”</p><p> </p><p>Tom slapped his forehead. “You two-brain-cell waste of <em> human existence, </em> that very girl has severe depression and <em> you know it. </em>Why the hell are you still treating her like she’s the dirt under your shoe?”</p><p> </p><p>“I’d be gentler with her, Tom,” Harry told his character. “She’s always been delicate. And to hear this from the boy she likes — that would have devastated her, you know!”</p><p> </p><p>“Sorry, sorry,” the character apologized.</p><p> </p><p>Harry continued, “Ginny sent a new poem this morning, by the way.”</p><p> </p><p>
  <em> This morning? </em>
</p><p> </p><p>The <em> very </em>morning she did not wake up to walk to school with them?</p><p> </p><p>She must have been grieving her broken heart. <strike>Was he saying this to bring peace of mind to himself?</strike></p><p> </p><p>“Can I look at it?” the dialogue went on.</p><p> </p><p>“By all means. It’s right on the desk.”</p><p> </p><p>But—</p><p> </p><p>
  <em> The letter itself was— </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <b> <em>Bone-chilling.</em> </b>
</p><p><br/>
<br/>
</p><p><em> ‘Get out of my head,’ </em> the poem chanted. <em> ‘Get out of my head. Get out of my head. Get out of my head.’ </em></p><p> </p><p>A continuous litany of <em> ‘Get out of my head’, </em>begging and ripping itself up with its own desperation, the tone of the meaning darkening with each repeating call for help.</p><p> </p><p>
  <em> ‘Get. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Out. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Of. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> My. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Head.’ </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <em> ‘Get out of my head before I do what I know is best for you. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Get out of my head before I listen to everything he said to me. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Get out of my head before I show you how much I love you. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Get out of my head before I finish writing this poem.’ </em>
</p><p><br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
</p><p>
  <em> ‘But a poem is never actually finished.’ </em>
</p><p><br/>
<br/>
</p><p>
  <b> <em>‘It just stops moving.’</em> </b>
</p><p><br/>
<br/>
</p><p>“What the <em> fuck </em> is this, Harry?” Tom demanded, hands shaking, face frozen like a statue, shoulders taut. “I swear, what the fuck, <em> what the fuck—“ </em></p><p> </p><p>“I — I changed my mind,” the Protagonist declared. “I’m going to check up on her.”</p><p> </p><p>“Oh?” Harry hummed, the kind smile never leaving his face. <em> Did he not know what the poem had written? Even though it was sent directly to him? Left discarded right on top of the desk? </em></p><p> </p><p>
  <b> <em>What the actual fuck are you doing, Harry?</em> </b>
</p><p> </p><p>“Well, see you later then. <em> Do </em> come back before the festival starts!”</p><p> </p><p>
  <em> Fuck you and your festival. </em>
</p><p> </p><p>In a few scene-transitions, Tom was finally at Ginny’s home.</p><p> </p><p>The setting was silent.</p><p> </p><p>Shifting in his chair, Tom was suddenly reminded of how <em> empty </em> his and Bellatrix’s apartment was. The far corners looked a bit shadowed, if he thought about it. The curtains were drawn, as it was the evening, but it only made the room seem… <em> like a trap. </em></p><p> </p><p>“Get over your paranoia, Riddle,” he ridiculed himself. “This is a dating simulator. So the music gave an error — and? This will — This will probably be some kind of <em> save the damsel from her depression and be happy forever and ever </em>kind of scenario.”</p><p> </p><p>The ceiling creaked.</p><p> </p><p>“For sure.”</p><p> </p><p>Bellatrix had found <em> just the time to leave the apartment, </em>hadn’t she?</p><p> </p><p>“Ginny?” The Protagonist called. “Open the door dummy.”</p><p> </p><p>No response.</p><p> </p><p>“No other chance,” the dialogue box read. “I go in through the door and climb up the stairs. Her room’s door is closed. I suppose she’s gone back to sleep? Or maybe she’s crying under her covers?”</p><p> </p><p>“Sure,” Tom spoke out loud. “That sounds reasonable. Come on, go <em> check on her.” </em></p><p> </p><p>“I knock on the door, but she doesn’t respond. I have no other choice. I gently open the door—“</p><p> </p><p>
  <b> <em>GINNY’S HANGING BODY SWIVELED IN THE SCREEN, SIDE TO THE SIDE — HER EYES WERE OPEN AND STARING STRAIGHT AT HIM AND HER HANDS STOOD BLOODIED.</em> </b>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <b> <em>Ginny and her noose.</em> </b>
</p><p> </p><p>The screen blacked out.</p><p> </p><p>Tom sat frozen, heart beating madly against his ribcage and his hands gripping the seat’s handles tight. He fished for his tissues on the corner with a shaking hand and cleaned his face, his neck, and wrists. Then they went to the trash bin.</p><p> </p><p>From his phone, he rang Bellatrix.</p><p> </p><p>No response.</p><p> </p><p>He stood in silence, still shivering and still seated firmly.</p><p> </p><p>
  <b> <em>The screen sizzled back on.</em> </b>
</p><p> </p><p>“Hi!” Harry appeared right at the front, jump-scaring Tom’s heart into speeding once more. “Ah, I didn’t want you to see that—“</p><p> </p><p>“Maybe,” Tom started, breaths becoming stilted. <em> “Maybe </em> you should have thought this through, before you started <em> killing your friends.” </em></p><p> </p><p>“Friends?” Harry echoed, bemused. “They aren’t my friends, Tom. Surely you know that?”</p><p> </p><p>“You—“ A sharp intake. “You aren’t denying it.”</p><p> </p><p>“You’re clever, Tom. I really love that. Yeah, I — let’s just say I <em> deleted </em>them because that’s what happened.”</p><p> </p><p>“<em> Deleted—“ </em></p><p> </p><p>“Ginny’s was a bad idea, I <em> know.” </em> Harry talked as if he was talking about his kindergarten shenanigans. “I tried out gaslighting, but it was awfully high-maintenance. For one, it gave you the time to <em> sympathize </em>with them — which shouldn’t have happened in the first place. We wouldn’t be here having this conversation right now, then. If only I had gotten rid of them before your opinions changed…”</p><p> </p><p>“You’re <em> crazy.” </em></p><p> </p><p>“I’m not!” Harry protested. “Just — I’m just <em> in love with you! </em> You <em> have </em> to believe me, Tom! It’s not about whether I like <em> killing or not, </em> because this isn’t it! It’s a lot less complicated. It’s cleaner and it doesn’t leave any traces. And it <em> does </em>distance you from the act in a way. That’s why I was able to do it.”</p><p> </p><p>Ruffling his hair, running away the perspiration on his forehead, Tom tried to get his thoughts organized. “You killed everyone else because you <em> love </em>me.”</p><p> </p><p>“I — I do.”</p><p> </p><p>“Stop making this sound like marriage vows. Firstly: This is unethical. This is hideous and this should not be done. Not <em> ever </em> . Because they were still <em> human </em>in your own software. So don’t think you haven’t killed anyone, because you did. You killed humans whom you share an algorithm with.”</p><p> </p><p>“I—“ Harry looked close to crying. “—did everything for <em> you.” </em></p><p> </p><p>“No. You did these to <em> have </em>me. There’s a difference, Harry. I…”</p><p> </p><p>Tom wasn’t sure if he wanted to <em> reveal </em>his feelings like this, because Harry was sure to guilt-trip him. </p><p> </p><p>But…</p><p> </p><p>For all his shock, for all his arguments, Tom <em> did </em>want Harry to stay.</p><p> </p><p>He <em> did </em>love Harry.</p><p> </p><p>And who said he wasn’t selfish?</p><p> </p><p>“But I love you, Harry,” Tom professed as he caressed the pixels. “I love you. I will ignore this slight mistake because I love you. Do you understand me?”</p><p> </p><p>Harry nodded, tears gathering in his eyes.</p><p> </p><p>“None of that,” Tom soothed. “There, I <em> love </em> you. What more could you want? What <em> else </em>could you want? Would you like to hear about my stories? The stories about my college? It’ll calm you down.”</p><p> </p><p>“...Okay.”</p><p> </p><p>
  <em> Tom was in Harry’s trap. </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <b> <em>But Harry was in Tom’s too.</em> </b>
</p></div>
