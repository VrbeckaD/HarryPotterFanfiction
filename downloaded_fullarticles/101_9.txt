          <div class="userstuff"><p>Harry followed the laid stones as they branched out further, becoming more decrepit the deeper into the forest they went. The clear signs of care fading; for no one would willingly leave the safety of their small town for some trifle rock wall.</p>
<p>He followed them, though. Climbing atop few and jumping from one stone to the next. Harry followed them like it was the spine of some great beast, and he was but a small adventurer discovering ruins from a tremendous battleâ€” a story in every broken branch and dry bone.</p>
<p>He would close his eyes and feel the extraordinary exhale of the beast ruffle his hair and it would smell of oak and holly trees, smell of last night's rain and decaying leaves in the morning. He would hear a mighty roar that would quake the earth and scare the nesting birds. Harry would reach out and feel leathery, scaly skin and giant protruding horns.</p>
<p>He would befriend this fantastic beast, and it would take him away to places unimagined. To warm smiles and open arms. To where food is a plentiful and bursting with flavour. To someplace he could grow old and weary, with stories to inspire generation after generation. To what Harry would lovingly call Home, with a fondness in his belly and a grin so bright and big that it would hurt his cheeks.</p>
<p>Then he would open his eyes. And Harry would still be a lone little boy in the wood.</p>
<p>Or he would be if he hadn't noticed two piercing red eyes watching his every move from farther into the shadowed forest...</p></div>
