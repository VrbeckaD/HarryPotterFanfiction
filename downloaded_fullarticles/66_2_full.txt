    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>Aziraphale watches as rain sloughs down the bookshop windows. He waits for Crowley in silence and warmth, feeling quite safe within the paper and leather embrace of his bookstore. It had been raining for rather more than two days now, with only brisk pauses to space between the lot of it and let the city breathe. Aziraphale didnt mind the rain  it made for a wonderful reading atmosphere  but the mud beneath his shoes became a bit tedious as walks with Crowley became more common. Still, he knew that anyone around Crowley could practically smell his love for rain. Aziraphale often wondered if The Almighty had put him in charge of that very first rain, 6000 years ago and before hed fallen. He did seem quite fond of the stuff. As if summoned by the Angels thoughts, a speeding black Bentley approaches the store in an obviously illegal soeed, and he shakes his head with a smile.</p><p>Speak of the demon, he mutters, directing it to no one in particular and clasping his hands together before he walks to the front of the store to pull his door open. A swaggering, sultry Crowley wrenches himself up and out of his car. He ends up practically launching himself drunkenly towards the shop, and his legs twist in a dizzying ballet of broken steps. His eyes twinkle with yellow, his cheeks flush with red, and his fangs poke out a bit more than usual under a full toothed grin.</p><p>Angel!
