          <div class="userstuff"><p>
  <span>Now, let it be said. Darcy and I were sisters.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>We love each other.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>However, we’re sisters.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>We hate each other.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The best example of this was the events of what happened our Third Year of Hogwarts. This being a time I went by Mosag Snape and she went by Daisy Smith.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>It was somewhere in the third week of it. My sister was eating breakfast with Ron. The two were debating over a selection of chocolate frog cards. Daisy was proud of her Newt Scamander card.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>She was trying to weasel a more rare card from Ron. He was being stubborn.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Usually I was down first for breakfast. For the purposes of being dramatic I let her arrive first. Walking up beh</span>
</p><p>
  <span>I put down the first sticky note.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy looked at it in confusion. “73?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“73.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Why am I holding a note that says 73?”</span>
</p><p>

</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>Days to Slapsgiving: 5</span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>The Sunday before Slapsgiving, they met at Hagrid’s for tea.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Negotiations had not gone well...for Daisy at least. Morgan was having a blast.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You’re a </span>
  <em>
    <span>bitch</span>
  </em>
  <span>.” Daisy groaned. “We made this bet for fun. Now it’s like a fight to my death.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Not my fault you didn’t accept my offers.” Mosag conceded. “Also, way to be considerate. Talking about a Death March in front of Buckbeak.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy scoffed. “Harry and Hermione will save him. I don’t </span>
  <em>
    <span>need</span>
  </em>
  <span> to be scared. It’s stupid. And ridiculous. </span>
  <em>
    <span>Like this Bet</span>
  </em>
  <span>.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You agreed to it!” Mosag reminded. “So suck it up.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy whined.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>==CD==</span>
</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>Days to Slapsgiving: 3</span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>They were walking out of a Care of Magical Creatures class.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag was standing at one end of their group, followed by Harry, Hermione, Ron, and lastly Daisy.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“So I begged the teacher to teach me the Slap of Three Hundred Million Suns-”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You said ten!” Daisy argued from behind Ron.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Ten </span>
  <em>
    <span>Hundred </span>
  </em>
  <span>Million Suns-” Mosag corrected herself, not missing a beat.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy realized her mistake. She groaned out, dripping her head on Ron’s shoulder. “Kill her for me, would you?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Why would I do that?” Ron asked, concerned for his safety at the mere idea.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You’re ginger. Ginger people are soulless beasts.” Daisy waved a hand to Mosag. “She’s doing a better job of it than you! Defend your nature!”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Ron grimaced. “She can have it.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Harry and Hermione laughed. Mosag puffed her chest proudly.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>==CD==</span>
</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>Slapsgiving</span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Today was the day.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The sun was shining.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag was pampering her Slap Hand.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy was going to be Slapped.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>She walked around Hogwarts like she was in a funeral march. She jumped at turning every corner, every opening door, too loud voices. Everyone knew to be cautious around Daisy Smith today.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>In contrast, Mosag was a delight. She engaged in class, talked with her classmates about homework, she even bloody </span>
  <em>
    <span>skipped</span>
  </em>
  <span>. She was on Cloud Nine.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The only odd thing was how they were behaving towards each other.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag was</span>
  <em>
    <span> ignoring</span>
  </em>
  <span> Daisy. In the same way as she did when she gave Daisy the first note. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“When are you going to Slap her?” Harry asked during lunch.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag popped a chip in her mouth. “It’s due by sunset.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“So...?” Harry paused for clarification.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag grinned. “I procrastinate.” Harry chuckled in agreement. “</span>
  <em>
    <span>You</span>
  </em>
  <span>, Mister Potter, don’t get to argue with me about</span>
  <em>
    <span> procrastinating.</span>
  </em>
  <span>”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Harry laughed again, albeit awkwardly. She had a point.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>So, came dinner time. Mosag hadn’t Slapped Daisy yet. According to them, the rules stated she had until 7:58 (their sunset) until the Slap was void. Daisy knew Mosag was going to milk out every second of fear that she could.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>At dinner, Daisy sat across from Mosag. It would be harder for Mosag to hit her from across the table. It gave Daisy time to prepare for the blow, form some kind of counter. She could roll backwards from it, or perhaps hope that another student would protect-</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Oh who was she kidding? The other students wanted to see this as much as Mosag did. The Slytherin especially. Any form of mudblood torture was good as tea for them.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Which gave Daisy her</span>
  <em>
    <span> idea.</span>
  </em>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy shot up in her seat. She pushed her palms on the table to glare at Mosag. “You’re really gonna Slap me today? Good. Good for you. Now you can prove to everyone that Purebloods are better than Muggleborns.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Many in the room gasped.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag just gave her sister a curious look. She wondered when her sister was going to play that card.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>To Daisy, though, she saw the face of a girl realizing what horror she’d unleashed. Madness always brought the best in her. “Yeah, that’s right.” Daisy cheered. “That’s what you wanted, wasn’t it? To show everybody once and for all that muggleborns aren’t real witches. No real witch would take a Slap like this, or be demeaned like I have for the whole school year. You’re finally proving to them who’s superior.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy lifted her hands off the table. She spread her arms wide, invitingly.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Well go ahead. Do it.” Daisy challenged. “Slap me right here, right now. Show them what you </span>
  <em>
    <span>really</span>
  </em>
  <span> think about mudbloods.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>She glared in her sister’s eyes, to dare Mosag to even try.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag...was a little proud.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Wow. You have a point.” Mosag mused.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy’s glare softened- only slightly. “I do?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You do.” Mosag conceded. She lowered her hands to the table, pushing her chair out. She rose to her feet so she was on level with Daisy (albeit she was naturally a few inches taller). “I shouldn’t be Slapping you now. Especially not with Sirius Black running around, making everybody think about Pureblood Superiority and the-” She glanced to Harry. “Obvious reasons why he disliked muggleborns.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag took a step away from the table.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The entire Great Hall held their breath.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy was smiling now. “No...Slap?” She almost let herself hope.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag smiled. There was no predatory glint in her eye, or want for blood shining on her teeth. It was a kind smile you give a friend on their birthday. “No.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy threw her hands up in the air. “I’m free!” Daisy shrieked to the ceiling.</span>
</p><p>
  <b>
    <em>SLAP</em>
  </b>
</p><p>
  <span>Daisy fell to the floor.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It was still sunset.” Mosag raised her palm high. “THAT’S </span>
  <em>
    <span>FOUR</span>
  </em>
  <span>!”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The Great Hall applauded.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Mosag turned to them, using her slap hand to give a Royal Wave. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>She went back to Daisy. The brunette witch hadn’t gotten up from the floor. She was holding her cheek in her hand. </span>
  <em>
    <span>“What is this feeling, that’s put you in your place?”</span>
  </em>
</p><p>
  <span>“Oh don’t start singing!” Daisy moaned.</span>
</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>“A hot red burning on the side, of your face!” </span>
  </em>
  <span>Mosag sang louder.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Slow jam too?! Why must I suffer!?” Daisy cried out. She held her cheek in her hands, curling up on the floor.</span>
</p></div>
