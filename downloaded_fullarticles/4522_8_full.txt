    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p align="center">
  <strong>The Hedgehog's Dilemma</strong>
</p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p align="center">"<em>We are our own dragons and our own heroes. We must rescue ourselves from ourselves.</em>"<br/> â€• <strong>Peter S. Beagle</strong></p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p align="center">
  <strong>Chapter One: The Girl &amp; The Muddy Road</strong>
</p><p>Ginny put her hands on her hips, then spread them, doing a quick turn on her toes. "What do you think?" she asked. She was wearing what looked like a collection of blue foam triangles.</p><p>Sucking on a mint, Hermione surveyed her doubtfully. "I think you becoming friends with Parkinson has completely warped your brain," she finally said, shaking her head a bit. And honestly, the thought of the wizarding world's Gossip Girl being best friends with <em>Sarcasma, Queen of Snark</em> was terrifying, but Hermione supposed that if the worst that came out of it was some questionable fashion choices, she had no real complaints. "Is that one of hers?"</p><p>"Yes. And it's brilliant," Ginny huffed. "I'm not even really asking <em>you</em>. All you wear are cardigans. Draco?" She twisted to look at him hopefully where he was busying himself at the counter.</p><p>He didn't even turn around. "No," he intoned, flatly. "Tea?"</p><p>Sighing, Ginny dropped her head back in defeat for a moment. "Really? Marco <em>loved</em> it. He wants it for the runway at his expo in <em>Milan</em>. I thought you gay blokes really went for this stuff." She wrinkled her nose a bit. "You know what? I think dating Harry, who wears the same jumper <em>every day</em>, has warped <em>your</em> brain." Flipping her hair over her shoulder, she swept out of the kitchen.</p><p>Hermione turned her attention back to her book as the Floo roared to life in the living room.</p><p>Draco set a mug at her side. She could smell the faint tang of lemon, and marvelled at the fact that he knew how she took her tea. Two years ago, when Harry and Draco had started dating, he would have sooner thrown the kettle on the <em>floor</em> than offer anyone a drink. Now, she couldn't so much as <em>glance at</em> Grimmauld Place without being given something to sip.</p><p>"Thank you," she murmured, crushing the mint between her teeth quickly so the flavour would be gone by the time the tea had cooled.</p><p>She was buried in her book when he sat down next to her. For a moment, he just sat there, and she could feel his eyes on her. Knowing, at this point, how much he enjoyed making people uncomfortable, she ignored him and focused on dissolving her mint.</p><p>As she curled her fingers around the handle of the mug, he spoke. "I'm not, you know."</p><p>"Not what?" she asked, absently.</p><p>"Gay."</p><p>Sipping delicately at the tea, she winced. The leftover sweetness of the mint lingered on her tongue, and the drink tasted entirely too bitter in comparison. "I know," she said, dismissively. "You're bisexual. You told me that last week. And the week before."</p><p>Draco nodded, and she saw his fingers tapping on the mug out of the corner of her eye. "Neither's Potter."</p><p>Slowly, Hermione raised her eyes from the book to stare at him, squinting a bit. "I know." She knew she sounded short, but why did he have to start talking about this inane nonsense when she was right in the middle of reading a sentence? "I think I know how my best friend identifies. But thank you, for reminding me. <em>Again</em>."</p><p>A look of frustration passed over his face, and he chewed on the inside of his cheek for a moment in wordless agitation before he took a gulp of his tea. She stared at him for a second longer, waiting for him to say something (maybe <em>repeat it, again</em>, or something, since he seemed so fond of it). When he just sat there in mute irritation, she turned back to her book, finishing the chapter and her tea.</p><p>"I should get going, Harry's taking forever," she sighed. "Tell him I'll get the book from him at work, or something, okay? Thanks for the tea, Draco."</p><p>"Of course, Granger," he muttered. Despite becoming friendly with everyone, he hadn't been able to break out of his habit of surnames, and they'd long since stopped correcting him on it. She deposited the mug in the sink on her way out.</p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p>She left early for work the next day, stopping into Weasleys' Wizarding Wheezes with a brown paper bag. The shop was empty at this time of day, and the clerk's counter was similarly so. "Ron!" she yelled, glancing at her watch. She wasn't going to be late for his sake.</p><p>He popped out of the back, a broad grin on his face that widened <em>impossibly</em> further when he saw the bag in her hands. "Yes!" he cheered, trotting eagerly behind the cash register to grab it from her. "What is it, today? Is it that Asian chicken salad? That was <em>amazing</em>. No, wait. Is it that seared tuna?" He reached in and tried to wrestle the container out. "Salmon? <em>Salmon</em>!"</p><p>Eyes widening a bit, Hermione ordered, "Calm down. Honestly."</p><p>Inhaling sharply, he closed his eyes, his face contorting a bit as though he were about to burst into sobs. "Oh, great Muggle God, who rules from the heavens and has a flowing Dumblebeard, I thank you for this bountyâ€”"</p><p>"Oh, my God." She turned to go.</p><p>"Oi!" She glanced over her shoulder at the second level, where George was leaning over the railing. "You're not even dating him, anymore, and you still bring him lunch? Where's <em>mine</em>? If this keeps up, I'm going to think you're not even in love with me, anymore."</p><p>"That's a good thing. It means the delusion is passing," she drawled.</p><p>Ron put the container back in the bag, smug. "Hey, maybe if you manage to make her into <em>your</em> ex-girlfriend, she'll start bringing you her leftovers, too. I put <em>five whole years</em> of my life into these leftovers. Five years that I'll never get back." He caught Hermione's narrowed eyes and swallowed. "And the five happiest years of my life. Obviously."</p><p>"Keep it up, Weasley. Maybe she <em>should</em> stop bringing him lunch. But cooking for one was impossible, and bringing Ron leftovers both delighted him and kept him from eating greasy fish and chips on a daily basis.</p><p>Sighing, Hermione trudged down the sidewalk. At least she had work to look forward to.</p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p>She was purely and utterly focused when hands slammed down on either side of her, hitting the desk like a thunderclap. She swallowed a scream, jumping and clasping her hand to her throat.</p><p>Harry chuckled. "Hey."</p><p>"Oh, my <em>God</em>," Hermione hissed, turning a bit to dig her elbow into his side. "You nearly gave me a heart attack!"</p><p>He was still laughing as he shied away from her elbow. She had to admit, as much as his attraction to Draco Malfoy <em>confused</em> her, it had at least brought a smile back to his face after the war. After several years of watching him flit in and out of depression (something Ginny had not even felt up to the task of dealing with, after a while), seeing him so <em>happy</em> was more than enough reason to not question his relationship with the tetchy blond.</p><p>Besides, she supposed she saw the appeal. Draco was a handsome man, and Hermione <em>supposed</em> she could see how his petulant brattiness might be endearing. If she squinted, that is.</p><p>"I couldn't resist," Harry admitted, completely unrepentant.</p><p>She rolled her eyes, shooting him a mild glare as she turned back to her work. She sighed heavily when he leant on her desk, scooting between it and her. When she scowled up at him, he offered her a grin. "You skipped lunch, didn't you?"</p><p>"No," she muttered, taking care to hold his gaze so he wouldn't sense the lie.</p><p>His eyes narrowed a smidgen as he stared into hers. "Liar," he accused. "What did I say was going to happen if you skipped lunch, again, Hermione?"</p><p>"You were going to stop <em>crumpling my papers</em> with your <em>bum</em>?" He crossed his arms, ever-patient, and she wrinkled her nose when she realised he wasn't going away. "Do we have to?"</p><p>"If I don't punish you, how will you <em>learn</em>?"</p><p>She hesitated. "I'll eat double lunch tomorrow."</p><p>"That's <em>not</em> how nutrition works," he scoffed. "Up. <em>Up</em>, up." She sagged as he grasped her arms, trying to become dead weight as he yanked her up. It didn't work, and he dragged her out of her seat with ease. Pulling her out of her cubicle and steering her down the corridor, he leaned into her boss' office to announce, "I'm stealing Miss Granger for her lunch hour!"</p><p>"Good," Halfweather shot back.</p><p>Harry beamed, curling his arm around her shoulders and exuding an obnoxious amount of cheer as he led her towards the lifts. He listened with half an ear, having heard it all before, his eyes on the sandwich sign as he tried to figure out what he wanted.</p><p>"And then who comes in? <em>Malfoy</em>. The older, <em>ruder</em> version; not yours," she huffed, her face pinching with instant dislike. "And, surprise surprise, he does <em>not agree</em> with my initiative."</p><p>"Well, you probably should have seen that coming."</p><p>She threw her hands up. "What does he even care? He can't <em>afford</em> to pay his elves? He has money coming out of his pores! Turkey and swiss!" she barked at the poor sandwich-maker, who jumped a bit. Hermione winced. "Sorry, sorry, I'mâ€” it'sâ€” not you, I'm justâ€” in general..." It has nothing to do with his ability to afford it."</p><p>"That's insane," she enunciated, her hands forming frustrated claws in front of her as she struggled to remain calm. "He is so infuriating. I've never met a man whom I so dearly wanted to shove into a waste compactor."</p><p>Harry blinked. "Star Wars?"</p><p>"Yes," she admitted. She'd watched it the night before.</p><p>"You need new DVDs."</p><p>"My DVDs are fine. Will you focus? Lettuce, tomato, and mustard," she told the sandwich girl, tiredly. "I need someone on my side who can counter Malfoy's rich-boy clout."</p><p>"Lettuce, mayonnaise... Onions? Onions," Harry decided. "And what, someone who <em>can't</em> be bought off by Malfoy's pile of riches? Do you want me to help you find a unicorn, while we're at it? Perhaps a phoenix, or two? Jesus, himself?"</p><p>"Well, at least I have your oh-so-helpful sarcasm to see me through," she said, tersely, grabbing a bag of crisps and fishing for her purse.</p><p>"I've got it," Harry said, and Hermione let her purse drop back to her side as he paid, grabbing some bottled waters out of the fridge before heading to the table. "I don't know how you can stomach those."</p><p>"I don't know how you can stomach Draco, but <em>I</em> am polite and kept my incredulity to myself," she sniffed.</p><p>His grin was immediate. "Really? You have <em>no idea</em> how I can stomach him, huh? What was that thing you said last New Year's, while you were pissed out of your mind, about his arseâ€”?"</p><p>"That was a joke," she interrupted, feeling her cheeks heat. "Also, I was drunk and being stupid."</p><p>"Oh, right, of course. So when you said that thing about it looking like it was chiseled by Michelangeloâ€”"</p><p>"Harryâ€”"</p><p>"â€”and then being discarded for being <em>too</em> perfect, that was just you joking," he finished, in a perfectly innocent tone. "I suppose you grabbing it was just you joking, too, huh?"</p><p>She scrubbed her hand over her face, mortified. "Al<em>right</em>. So he's fit. That doesn't mean he's not obnoxious."</p><p>He held up his hands. "No argument, here. I just wanted to make sure we were on the same page about your jokes, is all. I'd hate for us to have a miscommunication."</p><p>She unwrapped her sandwich with a scowl. "You know, I really hate you."</p><p>"Oh, you really don't," he returned, confidently, his grin returning so widely that it put dimples in his cheeks. When her scowl intensified, he leant forward and booped her on the nose, yanking his hand back before she could slap it out of her face.</p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p>When her mobile rang, she jogged for it and then crammed herself into the corner of her bedroom â€” the only place in her flat where she got reception. The magic made things go a little crazy in the building. "Hi, Dad."</p><p>"Hello, Lambchop. Are you still popping by on Sunday for your mum's birthday?"</p><p>"Yep." The call began to get choppy, and she shoved herself harder into the corner of the room until it evened out. "What'd you get her?"</p><p>"Bracelet."</p><p>She snorted. "Creative, as usual," she said, biting back a faint smile. When Rose Granger failed to hint at things she wanted with an appropriate lack of subtlety, she received jewellery. After over thirty years of marriage, she'd learned her lesson and now just stuck a picture of what she wanted on the fridge with a magnet. She must not have specified this year.</p><p>"You're one to talk," David retorted. "Let me guess: A gift certificate to a restaurant."</p><p>The pause was as guilty as it was indignant. "No," she managed, after a moment of spluttering. "I got her something much better than that. Thank <em>you</em> very much."</p><p>"Oh really? What is it? I can't wait to hear."</p><p>Another pause. "You're cutting out," she said, primly, although the connection had never been more crystal clear than in that moment. "Better hang up. Bye, Dad!" He was laughing when she hung up, her nose wrinkled. "How much is it for?"</p><p>"Two hundred and fifty. Pounds," Hermione added.</p><p>His eyebrows shot up. "Wow, you are a <em>good</em> daughter."</p><p>"<em>Thank</em> you. It's nice to finally be appreciated. So..." She shot him a winning smile. "Care to buy it off me? I think Draco would even like this place; it's pretty posh. The kind of place where you bring in a gift certificate for two-fifty and you still end up fishing out your wallet. He'd have to wear something Muggle, of course."</p><p>"Hmm. I don't know. <em>Full asking price</em> for a gift certificate you can't use?" he asked, sceptically.</p><p>She slumped against his desk, groaning. "I swear, you have become so insufferable since that blond idiot sunk his claws into you," she complained. "What do you <em>want</em>?"</p><p>"Come with us."</p><p>Shaking her head emphatically, she said, "Nope," complete with a popped 'p.' "Absolutely not. I'm not going to sit around while you two have a <em>date</em> around me. I'll be trapped there by societal conventions, unable to escape while you two make doe-eyes at each other. I'll be utterly miserable."</p><p>"It won't be a date," he promised. "And you won't be the third wheel. He's always complaining about how my friends are riff-raff and we don't do anything nice as a group. This way, I can shut him up."</p><p>"How can you sit there and say with a <em>straight face</em> that I won't be a third wheel?"</p><p>"I promise."</p><p>Her nose wrinkled. "Can we bring Ginny?"</p><p>"I am not stepping a single foot outside with her in public until she purges Pansy from her closet," Harry said, grimly. "Besides, I want to go this Saturday, and she'll be at her fashion expo, wearing something completely ridiculous."</p><p>"Ron?"</p><p>"Is closing on Saturday and chews with his mouth open."</p><p>Hermione made an exasperated noise. "<em>Some</em> fourth person, then. If I have to sit across from you while you two play footsie under the table, I <em>will</em> go home and shut my head in the oven," she swore. "I haven't dated anyone since Ron, and I don't need you two flaunting your happy relationship at me in close quarters."</p><p>"You have become so bitter," he said, wonderingly. "You haven't even been single two years yet, and you are just a pile of bitterness wrapped around a burgeoning cat lady."</p><p>She straightened from his desk with a huff. "Forget it."</p><p>Harry laughed as he grabbed her wrist, keeping her from stomping out entirely. "Listen. I'll buy it off you. I'll treat you to dinner at some posh restaurant. And we will sit there and talk about house-elf rights right in front of him. You can't tell me you don't find the thought of that <em>a little</em> appealing."</p><p>Hermione tilted her head a bit, considering it. Watching Draco get all tetchy <em>might</em> be kind of fun. "No snogging in front of me," she ordered.</p><p>"I will not snog Draco in front of you," he promised, gravely. He waited, eyebrows raised.</p><p>She inhaled deeply, pursing her lips in thought. Something to do with gardening, she supposed.</p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p>"What about these ones?" Ron picked up a packet, hopeful as ever.</p><p>Hermione glanced at them. Carrots. "She has those," she said, as she'd said about the last <em>six</em> seed packets Ron had tried to get her to buy. He put them back with a sigh and reached for strawberries. "Too high maintenance."</p><p>He stopped reaching, throwing his hand up in wordless frustration. "I give up. Since the woman has the world, apparently, just get her a card."</p><p>"I am <em>not</em> getting my mum a <em>card</em> for her birthday."</p><p>"Hermione, I'm no good at present-picking," he began, and she turned to glare at him, her lips thinning. Help me find something, or go back to eating a family-sized bag of crisps every day at noon," she grit out.</p><p>Sighing, he turned back to the seed wall. He pointed. "Those."</p><p>"Has them."</p><p>"Oh, Merlin, someone kill me," he whispered.</p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p>She <em>did </em>end up getting the strawberries, if only because she was pretty sure her dad would disown her if she brought any more vegetables into his house. She hadn't noticed herself gaining any weight, but then again, her work clothes were generally shapeless and (as she'd just discovered) fairly forgiving.</p><p>Maybe this was just a hallmark of turning twenty-five. It was all downhill from here.</p><p>"God, I <em>am</em> becoming a cat lady," she whispered to her reflection, horrified. She was going to have to start going on walks, or something. Well, it was too late to try and get anything else, now, so she just shrugged helplessly at herself and headed for the Floo.</p><p align="center">o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o0o</p><p>"I'm here!" Harry appeared at the top of the stairs and held up a finger to indicate he needed a minute, and she waved him away as she headed for the kitchen, thirsty.</p><p>Draco was already there, at the table. He did a double-take when she entered, a sneer already etching itself onto his handsome face. "Merlin, Granger, do I need to take you shopping?"</p><p>Plastering a plastic smile on her face, she yanked the fridge open. "And it is just a <em>delight</em> to see you, as well, Draco.
