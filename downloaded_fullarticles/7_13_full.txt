She blinked when she felt someone take her hand and looked over to see Daiki holding it and he smiled at her for the first time that morning. </p><p>A few minutes later Ayano finished and got up, and Mac looked down to a bandaged knee. She saw Ayano look at her son with a strange look on her face before she smiled warmly and shook her head a little. Mac just thought adults were weird. </p><p>You should be fine now, Ayano said, you did well. </p><p>Mac blushed a little and looked down. </p><p>What happened? Ayano asked looking at the kids. </p><p>I fell, Mac whispered before Daiki could say anything. I was running and I fell. </p><p>I see, Ayano said again and Mac wondered what she saw did she know? She couldnt know! </p><p>Thank you for your help, Aomine-san, Mac said getting to her feet and bowed politely to the woman just like the teacher who taught them Japanese back in England had told her to do. </p><p>Think nothing of it, Mac, Ayano smiled kindly, and call me Ayano-Obasan. </p><p>Mac felt her eyes widen slightly as she stared at the woman who smiled at her again, Mac nodded carefully feeling a warmth in her chest as she did. </p><p>Youre very good in Japanese, Ayano commented. </p><p>I liked the studies, Mac said shyly. </p><p>Perhaps you can help Daiki with his homework, I tell you, that boy is only interested in basketball. </p><p>Basketball is important! Daiki spoke up defensively, </p><p>Mac just nodded and smiled a little. </p><p>Come on, Kenzie, Daiki said and pulled her with him and outside again. Mac followed him in silence and soon they were on the basketball court. No surprise there, Mac thought amused. </p><p>You didnt fall, Daiki said abruptly. </p><p>I did; Mac said defensively folding her arms over her chest. </p><p>Your cousin and three other boys were chasing you! Daiki said, dont tell me you just fell! </p><p>I tripped when they were chasing me and fell, Mac said glaring at him, so I <em>did</em> fall! </p><p>With help, Daiki wouldnt let go. Why is he bullying you? Tell your aunt and uncle! </p><p>Mac glared at him. </p><p>You think that will do anything good?! Mac snapped at him. They know and they dont care! No one cares! </p><p>I care, Daiki said quietly. Mum would care. </p><p>You cant tell her, Daiki! Mac said suddenly worried. You cant tell anyone! </p><p>Why? Daiki asked confused and defiant at the same time as only a child could do. Shell help. </p><p>Help with what? Mac asked, So, my cousin doesnt like me. Its not something anyone can do anything about. I only have them, Daiki. If not for them I would be in an orphanage and thats a horrible place! I have heard all about them, and I cant go to a place like that. </p><p>Daiki looked at her with wide eyes and Mac felt frustrated tears fill her eyes so she quickly turned away from him so he wouldnt see and removed her glasses to rub her eyes with the back of her hand.  </p><p>Please, Daiki, Mac said pleadingly as she turned around and stared at him, her glasses back on. </p><p>Fine, Daiki agreed and Mac smiled relieved at him. Lets play some basketball. </p><p>Mac laughed and he blinked and stared at her before he smiled and the sombre air around them lifted as his laughter also sounded. Mac suddenly realised, she had her first friend. A happy and warm feeling filled her chest as she caught the basketball he threw at her with his usual happy smile. All tenseness from earlier forgotten and only the two of them were left on the court. She grinned happily as she rushed into the game. </p>
