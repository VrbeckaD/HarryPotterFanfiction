<p> </p>
<p>Ginny Weasley had a problem. A blonde-haired problem called Luna Lovegood.</p>
<p>Ginny was close friends with the Ravenclaw girl, and had been for several years. They hung out in shared classes, chatted during break-time, and did their homework together.</p>
<p>Luna was…<em>unusual</em>. She seemed uninterested in mundane things like the newest hit song on the Wizarding Wireless Network, and the latest dating gossip about who was dating whom. Instead, she devoted her time to discovering new magical creatures which Ginny wasn’t sure even existed, sprouting random facts about things like the stars, and making her own jewellery out of butterbeer corks.</p>
<p>Ginny liked this.</p>
<p>Ginny liked Luna.</p>
<p>It had happened slowly. She wasn’t sure when, but over-time, she had found herself staring at Luna, gazing at the long dirty-blonde hair, the enormous grey eyes, and the way she bit her lip when she was nervous about something.</p>
<p>As was the case now, as two large Ravenclaw fifth-years stood in front of her, their arms crossed, and their eyes glinting maliciously. Luna’s head was bowed slightly, and her hands clamped nervously around her bag.  </p>
<p>‘What’s wrong, Loony?’ one of them crowed. ‘Cat got your tongue?’</p>
<p>Ginny, who had been walking across the entrance hall to meet Luna before their shared Charms class, felt her hair bristle with anger.</p>
<p>‘Oy; leave her alone!’ she yelled, marching in between them and Luna, and glaring furiously at them, her hands balled into fists. ‘Push off!’</p>
<p>The two Ravenclaws looked down at the short Gryffindor.</p>
<p>‘What’s it got to do with you, Weasley?’ one of them drawled, crossing their arms. ‘Some bodyguard you are. Well, I suppose, if are you hanging out with Loony Love—’</p>
<p>‘And just <em>what</em> do you think you are saying to my sister and her friend?’</p>
<p>The two Ravenclaws turned. The tall freckled face of Ron Weasley was glaring down at them, his prefect badge glinting in the morning sun breaking through the enormous doorway behind him. Normally, Ginny wouldn’t have wanted his help, but having a protective older brother who could dock points from bullies had its advantages.</p>
<p>‘Come on,’ one of the fifth-years muttered to the other, and the two of them beat a hasty retreat up the staircase. Ron continued to glare after them, before turning his attention back to Ginny, who had put a supportive arm round Luna’s shoulders.</p>
<p>‘You okay, Gin?’</p>
<p>‘Yeah,’ she muttered, now patting Luna softly on the shoulder. ‘Those twerps were just being rude to Luna. <em>Again</em>.’</p>
<p>Ron gave Luna a friendly smile, which Luna returned weakly.</p>
<p>‘Should I chase them down and give them detentions?’</p>
<p>‘Ron…’</p>
<p>‘Or maybe ask Hermione about the school’s policy towards bullying?’</p>
<p>‘Ron.’</p>
<p>‘Or maybe me and Hermione should speak to Flitwick about it—’</p>
<p>‘Ron!’</p>
<p>Ginny was now glaring slightly at him, not entirely sure why she was so angry all of a sudden.</p>
<p>‘Oh,’ Ron mumbled, looking awkward. ‘Sorry- I’m just trying to help.’</p>
<p>‘You’ve already done that!’ she spat. ‘I’m got this!’</p>
<p>Ron’s eyes lingered slightly on Ginny’s hand, wrapped softly around Luna’s shoulder.</p>
<p>‘I see you have,’ he said, shrugging. ‘Sorry about that all, Luna. Those two are horrible. Hope you feel better soon.’</p>
<p>He wandered away back into the Great Hall with his hands in his pockets, looking a little hurt.</p>
<p>Ginny let out a little sigh of frustration. She hadn’t meant to be so rude. Oh well, he’d survive.</p>
<p>‘Thank you, Ginny,’ murmured Luna, turning her enormous grey eyes to Ginny. ‘You’re very nice.’</p>
<p>Ginny felt her stomach give a little flip in her stomach. She realised that her hand was still wrapped around Luna’s shoulder and was stroking her arm gently- Luna’s skin felt soft under her robes, and her a few strands of her blonde hair was gently touching Ginny’s arm.   </p>
<p>‘No- no problem,’ she stammered, taking her hand off of Luna’s shoulder hurriedly. ‘Hey, shall we start heading over to Charms?’</p>
<p>‘Oh, yes,’ said Luna dreamily, as they began to climb the stairs. <em>Was it just Ginny’s imagination, or did Luna look a little flushed?</em> Ginny guessed it was the nerves from when those two bullies were being rude to her. ‘How have you been getting on with your practice for the separation charm?’</p>
<p>‘Oh, er, well…’</p>
<p>‘Oh, Ginny,’ Luna sighed. Ginny tried not to let her wander off to a daydream she’d had of Luna sighing Ginny’s name in <em>very different</em> circumstances. ‘You didn’t forget, did you?’</p>
<p>Ginny grinned at the blonde. Actually, she had practiced, but she liked Luna helping her with her spell-work, <em>especially</em> when Luna guided Ginny’s hand for the spell. </p>
<p>Luna sighed again, sounding a little amused. Ginny’s heart gave a little flutter.</p>
<p>‘Well, I guess I’ll have to help you out,’ Luna smiled, dimples forming in her pale cheeks.</p>
<p>Ginny returned the smile.</p>
<p><em>Oh, this was bad</em>, she thought to herself, <em>this is so bad…</em></p>
<p> </p>
<p>‘Now, class,’ Professor Flitwick instructed to the class, standing on a large stack of books. ‘I hope you’ve all been practising those separating charms for today!’</p>
<p>There was a general murmur of assent from the class, many of whom looked like they hadn’t quite woken up just yet.</p>
<p>Professor Flitwick gave a little exasperated sigh under his breath. First-period lessons were almost the most difficult to teach, especially on a Monday morning.</p>
<p>‘Well,’ he continued brightly to the room at large. ‘Let’s get started then! And don’t forget to be very careful with the wand-movement!’</p>
<p>The class, sprawled out over several long benches, divided off into pairs. Ginny turned to Luna, who was sat at the end of their bench.</p>
<p>‘Ready to roll?’ she asked, tying her long red hair into a ponytail so it wouldn’t get in the way of her spell-work.</p>
<p>Luna nodded.</p>
<p>Next to them, Colin Creevey was attempting to remember the exact wand movement, much to the irritation of his partner, a Ravenclaw girl who Ginny could never remember the name of.</p>
<p>‘It’s a twirl, and then a sharp jab downwards, Colin!’</p>
<p>‘I’m sorry!’ the small Gryffindor exclaimed, embarrassed. Ginny tried not to chuckle as she looked round at them. Colin was a sweet guy, even if his spell-work was somewhat erratic. He was like a cute little brother, which was ironic considering that Ginny had six older brothers and did not think of <em>any</em> of them as cute (well, Ron had his moments when he was staring longingly after Hermione Granger). </p>
<p>‘Miss Weasley, are you with us?’</p>

<p>Ginny cleared her throat hurriedly.   </p>
<p>‘Sorry, Professor,’ she said, picking up her wand. ‘We’ve starting now.’</p>

<p>Luna let out a slight gasp. Ginny turned.</p>
<p>Luna’s hand was still holding hers, and a soft glowing pink thread was wrapped around their intertwined hands, keeping them together. </p>
<p>Ginny’s eyes widened.</p>
<p>‘Oh dear,’ said Professor Flitwick, tapping the thread with his wand worriedly. ‘It appears you accidentally casted the conjoining charm.’</p>
<p>
  <em>Conjoining charm?</em>
</p>
<p>Flitwick pointed his wand at the two girls’ interlocking hands and performed the separating charm. But the thread did not disappear. The head of Ravenclaw house widened his eyes, looking baffled.</p>
<p>‘P-professor…’Ginny stuttered, her stomach giving a funny lurch. ‘Can you not remove it?’</p>
<p>‘By the looks of it, Miss Weasley,’ Flitwick continued, stroking his chin thoughtfully. ‘You might have cast a semi-permanent charm. As it wasn’t the correct wand-motion, this could fall under the banner of an experimental spell. There isn’t much we can do but wait until it disappears.’</p>
<p>‘Semi p-permanent?’ Ginny stuttered, her eyes growing wider in shock. ‘How long do you think it will take to wear off?’</p>
<p> ‘Oh, I’d say just under a day.’ Flitwick said, matter-of-factly.</p>
<p>‘<em>A day?!</em>’</p>
<p>‘Oh well,’ said Luna, brightly. ‘I guess we’ll have to wait for it to disappear.’</p>
<p>Ginny looked round at her. Luna was smiling sweetly, and not looking remotely embarrassed to have their hands joined the way they were.</p>
<p>‘I’ll pass on the message to your other classes today, so you can take the rest of the day off,’ said Flitwick. ‘Obviously, you won’t be able to write anything down if your hands are tied together.’</p>
<p>Ginny nodded nervously.</p>
<p>‘Are you okay, Ginny?’ Luna asked, as Professor Flitwick moved back to his desk, and the class resumed their practice of their charm.</p>
<p>Ginny’s felt her heart give a heavy thud.</p>
<p>‘Yeah, I’m fine.’</p>
<p><em>This is really bad</em>, she thought, <em>this is really bad…</em></p>

<p>‘So, I was thinking,’ chirped Luna, her eyes wide and cheerful. ‘Maybe we should have a look over what we’ll be missing in the lessons we’re not going to.’</p>
<p>‘Er, yeah,’ said Ginny, rubbing her neck with her free hand. It felt rather warm. ‘That sounds good.’</p>

<p>Ginny felt her mouth fall open, and her face burn.  </p>
<p>‘You…  You were awake?’ she squealed, terrified. ‘Luna, I’m…  I’m so sorry!’</p>
<p>Luna inclined her head to the side, looking puzzled.</p>
<p>‘What would you be sorry about?’ she asked, still smiling. ‘It was very nice, Ginny. I’ve never been kissed by a friend before.’</p>
<p>Ginny’s stomach felt like it was melting into her legs.</p>
<p>‘Although,’ Luna continued, a slight blush appearing on her cheeks. ‘I suppose we’re more than just friends now, considering that you’re….in love with me.’ ’</p>
<p>Ginny’s heart flew into her mouth, and her stomach gave an enormous lurch. Luna had heard her confession; she’s hadn’t been asleep.</p>
<p>‘W-wait!’ she stuttered, her head spinning. ‘Luna, people don’t just become more than friends because one is in love with the other. That usually only happens when…when….’</p>
<p>She trailed off. Luna moved her face closer, so that Ginny could count the lashes on each of Luna’s grey eyes.</p>
<p>‘<em>When both fall in love with each-other?</em>’ Luna finished, looking happier than Ginny had ever seen her. ‘Yes. I love you, Ginny Weasley.’</p>
<p>Luna cupped Ginny’s startled face in her free hand, and tenderly brought her lips to Ginny’s.</p>
<p>Ginny’s brain seemed to malfunction. All she could think about was Luna’s sweet lips against hers, and the sweet vanilla scent of her hair, and how soft her skin of her palm was against Ginny’s face.</p>
<p>After what felt like several glorious hours, their lips parted. Ginny was breathing heavily, and she could feel her heart pounding in her ears. Her hair was probably a mess, and her palms were sweaty, but she found it hard to care.</p>
<p>‘Do...  Do you really mean it?’ Ginny asked, taking Luna’s free hand in hers.</p>
<p>Luna nodded, her eyes sparkling.</p>
<p>Ginny leaned in towards Luna, and the two of them eased backwards onto the bed, so that Ginny was lying atop of Luna. Their hands were clasped together, and Ginny could feel Luna’s body pressed against hers.</p>
<p>Ginny pressed her lips to Luna’s again, and Luna moaned slightly. As one, their free hands disconnected and found each-others hair; Luna’s pale fingers stroking Ginny’s tangled red knots, and Ginny’s hand losing itself in Luna’s dirty-blonde locks.</p>
<p>Unbeknownst to both of them, the magical thread began to dissolve; leaving only their two entwined hands, which continued to hold each-other tenderly.</p>
