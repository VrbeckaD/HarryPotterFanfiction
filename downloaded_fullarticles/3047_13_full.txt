    <h2 class="title heading">
      The Extraordinary Presidents
    </h2>
    <h3 class="byline heading">
      <a rel="author" href="/users/RandolphChurchill/pseuds/RandolphChurchill">RandolphChurchill</a>
    </h3>


        <div class="summary module" role="complementary">
          <h3 class="heading">Summary:</h3>
            <blockquote class="userstuff">
              <p>There are a lot of Presidents in fiction. So who's in the White House in a world where all of fiction is true? A short description of all the presidents of the United States, from it's birth to it's death.</p>
            </blockquote>
        </div>

        <div class="notes module" role="complementary">
  <h3 class="heading">Notes:</h3>





    <blockquote class="userstuff">
      <p>This is an attempt to put together a list of all the Presidents who could have served in the League of Extraordinary Gentlemen universe, where all of fiction is true. It will have a short blurb on every president's policies and the events that happen during their time in office and will cover the United States from it's founding to it's abolition. Apart from Washington, Hamilton and Lincoln, I haven't included any real people, although several of the Presidents will be analogous to real people. There will be a list of references every ten chapters, explain all the references. Finally, some of this will contradict parts of the League-Verse after Black Dossier, although nothing particularly major. </p><p>I hope you enjoy and please feel free to read and review.</p>
    </blockquote>

</div>

    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>1789-1797 George Washington (Independent)</p><p>Washington was the first American President and in the opinion of many, the best. Washington had previously served as commander of the American armies during the Revolutionary War, where he had slain hundreds of redcoats single handedly, riding on his giant bald eagle. Washington's rule was wise and noble, as he sought to ensure that the United States would always be a font of liberty in the world and never become too involved in the affairs of other nation. Washington was famously completely unable to lie, which helped him to earn the trust of the American people. Fearful of giving the president too much power, Washington opted to seek only two terms as president. Upon leaving the Presidency, he was carried off to heaven by the goddesses Columbia and Victoria.</p>
