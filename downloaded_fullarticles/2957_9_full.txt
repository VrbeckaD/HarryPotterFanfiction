  <span>It wasn’t as if Anthony Goldstein had ever been raised to think that there was a </span>
  <em>
    <span>problem</span>
  </em>
  <span> with being interested in other boys. His Uncle Isaac was bisexual, and married to a man, at that – and his Uncle Dan was gay. His parents wouldn’t even be upset that he was interested in a Pureblood boy – his own mother was a Pureblood (of course, if he wanted to </span>
  <em>
    <span>marry</span>
  </em>
  <span> a Pureblood boy, his parents probably </span>
  <em>
    <span>would</span>
  </em>
  <span> expect him to convert, but that wasn’t on his mind at the moment).</span>
</p><p>
  <span>His family being fine with him dating boys and goyische Purebloods aside, he really didn’t think they’d approve of Zacharias Smith. Just because of his personality. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>His parents didn’t put up with any sort of ego or snide remarks (at least, not any that came from someone besides his Aunt Miri, but she was </span>
  <em>
    <span>family</span>
  </em>
  <span> and that was different). He knew his Uncle Isaac and Aunt Miri thought that his father was too serious – and while Anthony might quietly agree sometimes, his mother was kind and level-headed and also knew when to relax and let things go. </span>

</p><p>
  <span>“I, er, well – I certainly didn’t have the best taste when I was your age. Spent the months leading up to my bar mitzvah mooning over some arrogant Gryffindor while denying it the whole time.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“That hardly sounds healthy,” Anthony said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It wasn’t,” Uncle Isaac said. “And you know, you haven’t got to go out with someone just because you fancy them. Sometimes you’re better for not dating them.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“What was Uncle Dan like when you met?”</span>

</p><p>
  <span>“I think Dad went to the opposite end of that.”</span>
</p><p>

I still don’t particularly like putting myself out there in non-professional settings, but I introduced myself to him before he was even finished putting away his tallis.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“But he was kind?”</span>
</p><p>

  <span>“I said </span>
  <em>
    <span>private</span>
  </em>
  <span> beliefs, Anthony,” Uncle Isaac said. “And I’d recommend against going for people who you know to be full of themselves. It rarely ends well.”</span>

</p><p>

</p><p>
  <span>“You fancied Rolf’s dad?” Anthony asked.</span>

</p><p>

Both of his uncles worked full time, and while they didn’t necessarily keep regular hours, he knew they were grateful for his help, and they always invited him to stay for dinner.</span>

    <span>boys,</span>

</p><p>
  <span>“Who?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Since when?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You haven’t said anything!”</span>

  <span>“He?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Of course he will! You’re a catch!”</span>
</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>“He?”</span>
  </em>
</p><p>
  <span>“Yes, </span>
  <em>
    <span>he,”</span>
  </em>

  <em>
    <span>but</span>
  </em>

  </em>

</p><p>

</p><p>
  <span>“Go with you?” Zacharias asked.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Yes,” Anthony said. He didn’t elaborate further, because he really wasn’t sure what to say, and he had a feeling that rambling would make things worse.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Is this some kind of joke?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“No,” Anthony said. “Why would it be a joke?”</span>

  </em>
  <span> completely hopeless today.”</span>

  </em>
  <span> make a point of running around like an overgrown bully.</span>

  <em>
    <span>trouble.</span>
  </em>
</p><p>

</p><p>

</p><p>

</p><p>

  </em>

    <span>Snape</span>

  <em>
    <span>he</span>
  </em>

</p><p>
  <span>“Hogsmeade,” Zacharias said. “I’ve thought on it. I’ll go.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Oh,” Anthony said. “Then, er… I’ll meet you outside the Great Hall?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“All right,” Zacharias said.</span>
</p>
<hr/><p>
  <span>“What do you mean, you didn’t ask if it was a date?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I was so surprised that he’d even said yes,” Anthony said. “I didn’t really think about it.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You,” Michael said, “are a bisexual </span>
  <em>
    <span>disaster.</span>
  </em>
  <span> You know that, Anth?”</span>

They made an odd group, at least if you asked Anthony, but at least they were all generally nice.</span>

</p><p>

  <em>
    <span>mean</span>
  </em>
  <span> anything.”</span>

    <span>that</span>
  </em>

Ernie, Hannah, and Justin invited Anthony and Zacharias to hang around with them, but, while Anthony was trying to figure out a polite way to decline without putting any pressure on Zacharias, Zacharias said, “No; we’ve got plans,” and pulled him away.</span>

</p><p>
  <span>“Well, I’m not going to,” Anthony said. “It’s not as if I stopped and talked to </span>
  <em>
    <span>everyone.</span>
  </em>
  <span> I saw Neville Longbottom in Honeydukes, and I didn’t start up a conversation with him.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Yeah, but that’s </span>
  <em>
    <span>Neville Longbottom,”</span>
  </em>
  <span> Zacharias said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Neville’s perfectly decent,” Anthony said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You’re too nice.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Anthony shrugged. “Maybe I am. It’s never caused me problems in the past.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It’s causing a problem right now,” Zacharias said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“What’s that?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Well, for one thing,” he said, “I can’t tell if you like me or if you </span>
  <em>
    <span>like</span>
  </em>
  <span> me. You know. Fancy me.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Oh,” Anthony said. He tried to seem nonchalant, as if he didn’t really care one way or the other what Zacharias thought of this whole thing, but – as rude and haughty as he’d been all day, there was something about that that just made Anthony like him </span>
  <em>
    <span>more.</span>
  </em>
  <span> Really, what was wrong with him? </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Which is it?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Which do you want it to be?” Anthony asked.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“That’s such a bullshit Ravenclaw answer,” Zacharias said. “Answer the question.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Anthony sipped at his Butterbeer. He had to seem confident and sure of himself. “Both.” His voice didn’t even waver, not one bit. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“How can it possibly be both?” Zacharias asked.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It’s quite simple, really,” Anthony said, “I like spending time with you as a friend, but I’d also like to snog you. I don’t think one outweighs the other, and the two can and do exist concurrently.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Zacharias rolled his eyes. “Fucking Ravenclaws,” he muttered. “So you fancy me?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Yes,” Anthony said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Okay,” Zacharias said. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>He didn’t volunteer any further information, and Anthony rather felt that his own daring and nerve was spent. He was a Ravenclaw; he didn’t have an unlimited supply of courage. Zacharias didn’t leave, which was a good sign.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>After about ten minutes of sitting in silence, Zacharias asked, “Do your dormmates know you’re – gay?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I’m not gay,” Anthony said. “I’m bisexual. But yes, they know.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I think Finch-Fletchley is.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Anthony shrugged. “I wouldn’t know.” Justin hadn’t said anything to him, and while Anthony </span>
  <em>
    <span>might</span>
  </em>
  <span> be inclined to agree, he also knew that he didn’t understand anything about posh Muggles or Muggleborns – who was to say that they weren’t all like Justin? “It’s not as if we have some secret club.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I like girls,” Zacharias said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“So do I,” Anthony said.</span>
</p>
<hr/><p>
  <span>Anthony didn’t know why, but the next week in Potions, he invited Zacharias to celebrate Chanukah with him. Zacharias hadn't even really spoken to him since they'd gotten back from Hogsmeade, so common sense said that he wasn't interested. Still, Anthony asked. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It's on the seventh floor, across from the tapestry of Barnabus the Barmy teaching trolls ballet. We usually start after dinner sometime, and loads of people bring their friends, because otherwise there'd be less than a dozen of us.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Zacharias rolled his eyes and asked him to add the standard ingredient to their potion.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>So, really, it wasn’t like Anthony expected him to show up. He knew he’d get over it, eventually, and he didn’t even bring it up as he chatted to Hermione Granger over latkes and watered down wine (he wasn’t sure who’d decided they needed to water down the wine for the older students rather than just giving the younger ones grape juice, or even if such a thing was halachically permissible, but such was life).</span>
</p><p>
  <span>He walked back to Ravenclaw Tower with Esther and Gary Margolies, and didn’t talk to Michael or Terry about it. Instead, he opened up</span>
  <em>
    <span> The Dark Forces: A Guide To Self-Protection</span>
  </em>
  <span> and worked through his reading for Defense Against the Dark Arts.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The second and third nights passed in much the same manner, so Anthony didn’t expect anything different from the fourth night. He was in the middle of a discussion with Hermione about the egg from the first task of the Triwizard Tournament and what the horrible screeching could possibly mean when Zacharias showed up.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Hermione raised her eyebrows, and Anthony did his best to look impassive.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Anthony, hello,” Zacharias said. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Hello,” Anthony greeted. “Hermione and I were discussing the clue for the second task.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Diggory’s already got it sorted,” Zacharias said, “hasn’t Potter?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Hermione rolled her eyes. “How has Cedric Diggory got it sorted?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I’m not telling you that,” Zacharias said. “Potter’s not a legitimate champion.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It was lovely to talk to you, Hermione,” Anthony said, sensing danger. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You too, Anthony,” Hermione said, giving Zacharias a very dirty look.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Anthony walked Zacharias over to the refreshments table. “We’ve got latkes and sufganiyot and wine.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Wine?” Zacharias asked.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It’s watered down,” Anthony said. “But it’s not completely terrible. The latkes are really good, though – the potato pancakes. We’ve already lit the candles, but – everyone’s still around.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You’re always like this, aren’t you?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Like what?” Anthony asked.</span>
</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>“Nice.”</span>
  </em>
</p><p>
  <span>“I – try to be,” he said. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It’s frustrating, you know.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“What is?” Anthony asked, because surely Zacharias wasn’t talking about him being </span>
  <em>
    <span>nice.</span>
  </em>
  <span> How would that be frustrating at all?</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You,” Zacharias said. “You’re frustrating. You’re too nice. And you don’t seem to </span>
  <em>
    <span>want</span>
  </em>
  <span> anything from me.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Anthony shrugged and bit into a sufganiyah. It prevented him from having to answer and completely embarrassing himself.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I don’t know what to make of it. Of you. And I like girls, you know. You’re not a girl.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“No,” Anthony said. “I’m not a girl.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Zacharias poured himself a glass of wine. “I don’t like it.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You haven’t even tried it.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Not the wine,” he said. “I don’t like how – being around you is – I don’t like it, all right?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Then why did you come here?” He tried to let the words roll off of him; after all, Zacharias had shown up, and he was talking to him. If he’d wanted to avoid Anthony, it would have been only too easy.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Because you – infuriate me.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I </span>
  <em>
    <span>infuriate</span>
  </em>
  <span> you?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Yes! Absolutely! I was trying to study, and I couldn’t stop – thinking about your stupid face and how absurdly nice you are to everyone.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Oh,” Anthony said. That didn’t sound infuriating to him.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I like </span>
  <em>
    <span>girls,”</span>
  </em>
  <span> Zacharias said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Maybe it was because he repeated it, maybe it was because Anthony was only too familiar with that line of thinking, but it suddenly became clear to him.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You fancy me,” he said.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Zacharias turned bright red. “I do not! I like </span>
  <em>
    <span>girls,</span>
  </em>
  <span> and you’re not a girl!”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I like girls, and </span>
  <em>
    <span>you’re</span>
  </em>
  <span> not a girl, and I fancy you. It’s not necessarily one or the other, you know. If this feels like – how you feel for girls, then that’s usually a pretty good sign. Of course, we could always snog to be sure.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Zacharias looked him up and down. “All right.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Anthony had not expected that to work. “All right?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Yeah,” Zacharias said. “But not here. Not in front of people. There’s got to be a classroom down the corridor, or something, yeah?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Oh, this was actually happening. Okay. This was – definitely not how Anthony had expected the night would go, but he could adapt. “Sure,” he said, trying to seem cool. “I’ve just got to come back for my menorah. The – candelabra.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It’s not as if it’s going to take all night,” Zacharias said. “I really have got to study.”</span>
</p></div>
