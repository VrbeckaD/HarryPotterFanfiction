Dudley and Vernon were both Alphas, and they had liked to trigger instinctive responses in her when she was a cub. It would have been hell on her if hed been able to use Command on her.</p><p>(<em>It was something she noticed during the last days of training her classmates. Most Alphas were not strong enough to use Command on her, even when they <em> wanted </em> to, and her scent- interpreted differently by everyone she came across- tended to <em> intimidate </em> other Alphas. Vernon and Dudley had quickly lost their ability to use Command on her as shed grown. Her instinctive tuning out of the Dursleys had also gotten better.</p><p>Which was most of the reason Holly didnt pick up on the plan until it was too late. She was <em> not </em> at her best.</p><p>She knew as soon as she sat still, as soon as she let the wizarding world get ahold of her shed be stuck. Maybe that would be an Auror, perhaps an Unspeakable, even a Ministry worker. And maybe, the wizarding world would remember she was a <em> Guide </em> and try to force her into the role of subservient puppet and baby maker to continue the Lines she had inherited now that they didnt need a <em> good little soldier </em> . That much </em> responsibility. <em> ) </em></p><p>Holly wasnt planning on sitting still long enough to find out. Especially since Thug One had <em> knocked her out via slamming her head into the van floor. </em></p><p>And here she was. Handed off like trash to some sort of Muggle Nazi organization without a second look or even the slightest bit of <em> hesitation. </em> Even considering her shields had been up, she cant believe she missed <em> this </em>. She had been in worse shape than she thought if this had been the results.</p><p>The only saving grace seemed to be that Vernon- and she <em> knew </em> it was that bloated warthog-faced beached whale of a buffon- while he had handed her over to the highest bidder, had told those that captured her she was a <em> mutant freak </em>.</p><p>From what her casual and very limited use of <em> Legilimency </em> could gather these men had <em> no idea </em> she was a witch. She cursed her magical exhaustion. <em> Legilimency </em> was a magic consuming art, and while Holly was a <em> very </em> powerful witch she had her limits and had to be choosy about whose mind she took a peek in. Shed chosen the head Doctors mind of course, and learned all about who and how they had acquired her, what they thought she was, what the drugs should have done, and <em> who had sold her out </em>.</p><p><em> All she had wanted to do was </em> <b> <em>warn them</em> </b>.</p><p>And now she was a prisoner of some Neo-Nazi group who wanted to pull her apart, see how she ticked, and then put her back together for their own use. They were so <em> excited </em> to have a Omega Guide in their midst- it was <em> disgusting </em>.</p><p>Batair prowled the edges of the room, almost rabid in appearance but it was obvious by the lack of reaction that no one could see him.</p><p>Which brought her to the reason she hadnt escaped. She was magically exhausted to a dangerous degree, but her magic, weak as it was for the moment, was acting up. It was why shed taken a chance with the <em> Legilimency </em> probes in the first place. If it was solely to her Guide abilities, or if defying death had more consequences then she realized. </em>)</p><p>On top of that, despite the drugs they had used to dim and repress her Guide abilities, and Omega senses, they were still active enough for her to know <em> something </em> in the building was pulling at her. Not <em> painfully </em> , but noticeable enough that she could pick out which direction and that the source wasnt very far away. And the fact that Batair kept looking at the door of the room like he wanted to <em> go </em> somewhere specific- and not just escape- but refused to leave her by herself had her curious.</p><p>That didnt usually happen. She made no sudden movements, kept her heartbeat at a steady pace and her eyes closed. She waited. She knew they were thawing the Soldier out now, that it would take a while before he was in working order. She pulled their plan from Pierces mind before he left. They had an <em> assignment </em> for him.</p><p>Such a <em> pity, </em> as it seems that Bucky would never make it to that assignment. Shed be <em> interrupting. </em> It would be a tragedy, of course, that no one would make it out of the building. That an unexpected malfunction would cause an explosion big enough to kill everyone inside. Their Asset would be lost. A major blow to HYDRAs continued operations.</p><p>Shed have to make sure there were no other prisoners like herself and Bucky before hand of course, but that would be a simple matter with a creative bit of magic.</p><p>She was thankful in this case that she was so used to doing powerful acts of magic without a wand <em> and </em> with little to no power left. Her magic was <em> dense </em> on top of everything else, having adapted to require a fraction of the power others did. Being the sole magical anchor to blood wards- no matter how weak- had been a deadly dance between draining too much and working her magic muscles so to speak until her magic had adapted to ensure its hosts survival.</p><p>(<em>Especially considering the amount of healing that was required for her to survive her childhood in as good as health as she had been.) </em></p><p>It allowed her to reach out, still faking sleep, and short out any technology in the room when the time came. She opened her eyes, sitting up and pulling the tubes and needles free. A quick twist of magic and fire bloomed, burning her blood and the computers holding the data collected from it into ash. She knew from the Doctors mind that he had not backed the data up like he should have- hed been worried someone would steal his work if he did so, and now it would work to her advantage as there were no other records beyond what was here in this room, and what the Doctor had handwritten. Her blood samples were much the same, all stored in this room, and within easy range of her fire.</p><p>Shed take care of the handwritten things when she had Bucky.</p><p>In the meantime, she had a prisoner of war to liberate.</p><p></p><hr/><p></p><p>Holly layered a subtle bit of magic over herself, making whoever looked her way see someone they thought should be where she was. It was a variant of <em> notice-me-not, </em>one that Ginny had developed with the help of Fred and George. She couldnt help the smirk that came as she met the eyes of a soldier clad in black. He nodded in acknowledgement before she stepped past him. Bless the Weasleys in all their clever and brilliant glory.</p><p>So far her plan was working surprisingly well. She must be on the upswing of the infamous Potter Luck. Although that might change, at least she started out on a high note.</p><p>She strode with purpose through the halls, following mental map she had taken out of Pierces head, alongside the subtle tug on her magic and Guide instincts. Batair stalked through the halls ahead of her, leading without hesitation, and still invisible to those around her as the drugs were still in her system. <em> (Though shed have hidden the Grim Spirit in this case even without the drugs in her system.) </em> She could hear the men cleaning the Soldier up as he went through the last stages of thawing.</p><p>She moved without hesitation to the door, confident in her magic holding <em> (half of magic was </em> belief <em> after all, and Holly </em> knew <em> her magic would work) </em> even in the face of so many.</p><p>Besides- in this particular case she knew <em> exactly </em> who she wanted everyone in that room to see, <em> exactly </em> who she needed to appear as to be obeyed without question. The only one who would see her as she actually was would be Bucky.</p><p>She opened the door, and as far as everyone in the room apart from Bucky was concerned, Alexander Pierce walked in. Her magic even ensured she carried a Beta scent to all but Bucky. She knew the man wouldnt be here, not yet, that he liked to <em> taunt </em> the Soldier when he was fully thawed and aware, and so he was in his own rooms, unaware of her impersonation of his person. The lack of cameras in his room, and his penchant for using the secret passageways through the base to remain off the cameras as much as possible was a boon.</p><p>No one would question Pierce suddenly appearing on screen form the angle she <em> knew </em> a passageway opened, stepping into view on the camera. She knew he used the passageways to enter and leave his room as well, so that no one knew where he was staying just in case.</p><p>Holly felt the vicious amusement well in her chest. Hed handed her everything she needed to make her magic convey him when he stared her down in an attempt to intimidate her.</p><p>She stepped up to Barnes, the scientists and doctors moving aside as she did so. He tracked her with alert blue eyes. She leaned down to his ear, much as Pierce liked to do, whispering softly as she provided the trigger words to him. She ignored the heavy scent of ice and cold and chemicals that muffled his core scent so intensely Holly could not place it. The only thing clear to her Omega senses was the fact that Bucky Barnes was an Alpha.</p><p>Sure enough, the Soldier straightened, icy eyes sharp and focused on her person as he provided the correct response-</p><p><em>  </em></p><p>-ready to comply.</p><p>She smiled to herself, even as fury twisted her expression into more of a barring of teeth than a show of amusement. Making sure not to touch him, she leaned closer, keeping her lips close to his ear, whispering to him while ensuring no one in the room could hear.</p><p>Hydra is compromised, <em> soldat. </em> From this point forward no other handler may use your trigger words apart from myself, until I provide you verbal and willing permission to respond again alongside the activation phrase: <em> I solemnly swear I am up to no good </em> . You will treat any use of these words without my permission as an attempt of an enemy to gain use of the Asset. This includes the man known as Alexander Pierce, as he is <em> compromised </em>.</p><p>Holly paused, watching as something flickered in the mans eyes.</p><p>I am to be the only Handler acknowledged by the Asset until such time as I provide willing and verbal permission along with the pass phrase <em> Mischief Managed </em> . If at any point I provide you permission without the use of those phrases, you are not to acknowledge the permission. You will restrain myself and retreat to a safehouse. Acknowledge, <em> soldat. </em></p><p>Acknowledged.</p><p>Holly inwardly grimaced at the monotone response, but she sharply inclined her head. Suit up <em> soldat </em>, we have a mission.</p><p>Acknowledged.</p><p>He stood and the doctors and scientists allowed it, unaware that Holly had just taken their queen off the board. She would bring him to help, but she would not make him go anywhere unarmed. She would not take his weapons, his <em> protection </em> from him. She watched silently as he moved with a mechanical precision, eyes constantly scanning the room of compromised agents on his way to his gear.</p><p>She turned away as he began to strip without care for modesty, only for the order she had provided.</p><p>Leave us, she commanded the room at large. This mission briefing is classified.</p><p>The doctors and scientists responded as she knew they would- a <em> Hail Hydra </em> and instant compliance. Her lips pulled upwards, as the predator that had already cornered unknowing prey. She remained turned towards the door until the Soldier approached her side, staying slightly behind and to the side of her. She turned to face him, meeting the blank ice of his eyes and providing the mission briefing.</p><p>We will be leaving this base, she explains, meeting and holding his stare head on in a way she knew even Pierce could not do. I will be enacting a <em> No Survivors </em>  protocol myself, <em> soldat. </em> Should you come across any Hydra agent that has touched you in a way that exceeded mission parameters, or participated in your training, in use of the Chair, you have permission to do as you please. You need not participate if you do not wish it, but regardless of your hand or mine, there will be <em> no survivors. </em> Do you understand?</p><p>Understood.</p><p>Something behind those glacial eyes flickered as he looked down at her. Holly held his eyes another moment before she turned away. She <b> <em>refused</em> </b> to order him to fight with her, to <em> kill </em> as he had been commanded so often. She <em> would not </em> abuse the words that had given her command over a person. She would allow him what choice she could, provide him the option. If he found it therapeutic to help her then he could. If he did not want to kill another soul she would never make him.</p><p>Her goal was simply to get him somewhere he could get help.</p><p>Luckily, she knew exactly where to find a highly capable healer with guaranteed confidentiality and quality service.</p>
