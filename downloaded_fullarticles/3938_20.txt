        <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Work Text:</h3>
          <div class="userstuff"><p>“Not a single house colour,” she said to him. “I mean it Draco.”</p><p>“Yes, yes, I know,” he said, kissing the tip of her nose with exaggerated patience. “Enjoy your lunch with Ginevra, I’ll be here when you get back and you can help.”</p><p>Here she was staring at the walls he’d already painted, watching him work with tears in her eyes.</p><p>The wall opposite the door was painted sky blue, a bronze eagle soaring towards the window.</p><p>The wall to the left was a pale yellow, a black and white badger looked to be burrowing below the skirting board.</p><p>To the right was a gradient of cinnabar bleeding into vermillion towards the bottom, a pack of gold lions prowling the wall.</p><p>She walked to the middle of the room and looked at the wall containing the door. It was still blank.</p><p>“I was thinking teal,” Draco told her. “I prefer teal to that horrible green they made me wear in school.”</p><p>“I said no house colours,” she managed to say.</p><p>“No, you didn’t,” Draco said, pulling her into his arms. “You said not a single house colour. This way we have all of them.”</p><p>She really should have known better than to give carte blanche to a Slytherin.</p></div>
