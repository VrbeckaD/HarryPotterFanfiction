    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p align="left">Teddy knows, deep down inside, that hes really fucked things up this time. Like, properly.</p>
<p>Anyway, he says to the room at large, sounding unconvinced even to his own ears, It wasnt <em>completely</em> my fault.</p>
<p>Across the table from him, Harry gives this little sigh and pushes his glasses up the bridge of his nose. Theyve been at this for so long. Even Dominique, who <em>loves</em> watching other peopleand especially Teddyget dressed down, looks bored. Shes started rummaging through the drawer beside her, idly picking up various utensils and then dropping them while everybody else tries not to wince at the crash of metalware.</p>
<p>Teddy splays his hands helplessly on the table. I mean, how was I to know? I didnt, like, realise they were that closely related, or that they would be ballsy enough to make a play for it. Nan said I should have known better, since theyre <em>purebloods</em>, but I thought was stereotyping and frankly rude. And, anyway, I feel like they <em>sort of </em>have the right?</p>
<p>Ginny sucks in a cross breath from behind Harry, and Dominique pauses with a spatula in one hand and an expression of utter delight on her face. Teddy hunches his shoulders in the chair. Hes twenty four years old, damnit, and he will not be bullied by a room full of Weasleys.</p>
<p>Im just saying, he continues in what is supposed to be a firm voice but that comes out sort of strangled, mostly because Ginny has started to slowly and threateningly lower herself into a chair, I know Sirius left you Grimmauld Place, Uncle Harry, butI mean, you dont even use it, you know? And youve not got Black blood. Not that thats a bad thing, obviously. In fact its a good thing, because James and Al and Lily with Black blood would <em>probably</em> be even scarier than they are now. In a good way, he adds so quickly he nearly falls over the words, because Ginnys and Harrys brows have drawn down and Victoire has kicked him quite sharply in the side of the leg, which hurts a <em>lot</em>, But I mean the Blacks have handed that down for, like, <em>generations</em>. So it was kind of a dick move for Sirius to give it to you?</p>
<p>There are <em>too many people</em> in this kitchen. It would be bad enough with just Ginny and Harry, but to have members of practically every branch of Weasleys seems overkill. Like Teddy <em>said</em>, it wasnt even his <em>fault</em>. And, anyway, what investment does Angelina have? Shes not even <em>married</em> to a Weasley anymore. No reason at all for her to be sat in the corner giving him the fish-eye.</p>
<p>Feeling very ganged up on, Teddy turns his hair a sorrowful shade of midnight blue, and grows his eyes so he can cast one of those huge puppy-dog looks up from beneath his lashes at his godfather.</p>
<p>Stop it, says Harry, frowning, Cmon, Teddy, thats not fair</p>
<p>Dominique throws her spatula back into the drawer with a crash. Momentarily distracted, all eyes in the room fly to her. She pushes one hand through her sleek hair and gives them all a great shrug. Next to Teddy, Victoire makes this little French <em>tch</em> noise, like she knew the whole time her sister wouldnt be able to resist getting involved.</p>
<p>Okay, if youd had something in your family since, like, the <em>dawn of time</em>, youd be pissed off if somebody gave it away, wouldnt you? Especially if that person got kicked out of the family yonks ago. And Teddys Nan clearly doesnt want it, so, like, the Malfoys are the next in line, right?</p>
<p>You cant just arbitrarily <em>challenge</em> the laws of inheritance, young lady, Ginny retorts. Dominique raises one cool red brow. Teddy feels a little bit in love with her for a second. Shes the only person he knows who can stand up to the adults in her family without ending up backing down.</p>
<p>Um, she says, leaning back against the Potters kitchen counter, I mean, if youve got the money, you can. And weve heard enough times about how the Malfoys definitely do.</p>
<p>Beside Dominique, Young Molly has started nodding. Yeah! Dads, like, <em>always</em> moaning about it. Theyve got, like, offshore accounts, or something?</p>
<p>Precisely, Young Moll, replies Dominique with a sage inclination of her head. Together they stand and stare at the rest of the room, Dominique full of frowns and Molly hopeful as a puppy.</p>
