    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>Two men sit within their respective homes. One sits with his head buried in his hands, mourning every wrong choice he ever made. The other stands at his kitchen sink, looking out the foggy window into the cold night, mourning every life he could not save. Ironically, or perhaps unavoidably, both men found themselves thinking of the other, both wishing to right the wrongs of the past, both feeling as if there was no hope for the future. Unbeknownst to the men, fate would soon intervene. For it believed that the two men, despite all that happened between them, were the only ones who could help the other heal. So that night, as Draco Malfoy and Harry Potter were falling asleep, fate snuck in and planted a seed, a plan, an idea. That maybe reconciliation isn’t so far-fetched, that maybe there is a chance to mend the broken pieces of the past into a whole and fruitful future. Smiling, fate retreats, deciding that it was now up to the two men to heal, and one day, to find each other.</p><p>
  <em>1-day post-Battle of Hogwarts</em>
</p><p>
  <strong>Harry’s P.O.V.</strong>
</p><p>The lake laps gently against the rocky shore where Harry sits with his feet in the water. The cool wind pulls at his hair and he’s sure if he looked down his feet would be blue, but he’s not ready to go back. Not yet. Here, he can just sit and be still. The water has no expectations of him; it does not look to him for guidance or strength, it doesn’t expect a saviour. The Battle of Hogwarts ended barely 24 hours ago, they’d barely collected all the dead, before reporters swooped in like vultures.<br/>
<br/>
<em>Harry, what were He-Who-Must-Not-Be-Named’s last words? How did it feel to vanquish the Dark Lord? What are you going to do now?<br/>
<br/>
</em>How’s he supposed to <em>know</em>? He’s not even eighteen years old yet, and until this point, his entire life was about survival. The future is such a foreign thought to Harry, thinking about it makes his throat feel tight and his heart start racing. He’s not like Ron and Hermione. Ron, with his aspirations of being Head Auror and the brain to actually do it. Hermione, who’s passion and determination could outlast even the most hardened Ministry worker. Not to mention their burgeoning relationship that is sure to keep them busy and comforted throughout these next few months of rebuilding. Harry sighs, he supposes he has a future too; one that the press reminds him of every waking moment. Fresh out of Hogwarts, Harry’s sure to go into Auror training and soar through the ranks with his gorgeous childhood sweetheart on his arm. His career will be illustrious, his eventual marriage, perfect, and Harry will live out his life like the saviour he is. It’s the fairytale end to his perfect story. Only, his story isn’t perfect, it’s far from that. Because when you strip away his Chosen One status, he’s just a seventeen-year-old orphan with average marks and a tendency towards nihilism.<br/>
<br/>
The sound of crunching grass draws Harry out of his forlorn thoughts. He’s aiming his wand at the interloper before they have a chance to blink. Wide gray eyes stare at him about thirty feet away. They sit in silence for a bit before Draco says, “So, how’ve you found <em>Lord of the Rings</em>?” Which starts a conversation that carries on until the end of the match when Harry and Draco go down to the player’s lounge to greet Ginny.<br/>
<br/>
“Hey Harry, Malfoy!” She calls once the step inside. Her red hair is pulled back in a sweaty ponytail, fly-aways all around her face and there’s a smudge of dirt on her left cheek, but Ginny still looks beautiful. Her hair’s quite a bit shorter than when he last saw her a little over a month ago, but it looks good.<br/>
<br/>
“Hey Ginny.” Harry says, giving her a big hug despite her being coated in sweat. “You did great out there.”<br/>
<br/>
“Yes, truly a stellar performance, Ginevra.”<br/>
<br/>
Ginny smiles, clapping Draco on the shoulder once she pulls back from Harry.<br/>
<br/>
“Thanks guys, I’m glad you came out.”<br/>
<br/>
One of her teammates comes over and whispers in her ear. Ginny nods before turning back to them.<br/>
<br/>
“Would you two like to join the team for dinner?”<br/>
<br/>
Draco shifts, “I don’t-“<br/>
<br/>
“We’d love to go.” Harry cut in, shooting Draco a pointed look.<br/>
<br/>
“Great!” Ginny says, holding up two fingers in the direction her teammate walked off in, “I’m glad to finally get to spend some time with my boyfriend’s boyfriend.”<br/>
Harry and Draco both flush and stammer objections but Ginny just laughs.<br/>
<br/>
“I’m only joking, guys. Come on, we’re leaving soon.” She turns and walks in the direction of the parking lot.<br/>
<br/>
Harry looks at Draco who’s already looking at Harry, “She likes to tease.” He says.<br/>
<br/>
“It’s whatever, Potter. Let’s go.” Draco strides off in the direction Ginny went, Falmouth scarf flapping in the wind. Harry stares after him for a moment before following.<br/>
<br/>
Dinner goes great, and when Draco hugs him before flooing home, Harry’s heart feels full and he sleeps better than he has in weeks.</p><p><em>60 months (5 years) post-Battle of Hogwarts<br/>
</em><br/>
Harry Potter is well and truly fucked. Grabbing his coat, wand, and the bouquet of tulips he picked up earlier, he runs out of his flat, barely managing to say the locking spell before he closes his eyes and disapparates away with a pop. He lands in a hidden alley about 400 meters from his Ginny’s house. Honestly, Harry cannot believe he’s late. His track record with punctuality isn’t stellar up to this point, but he had really wanted to be on time today, despite how nervous he feels. Oh, well, nothing he can do about it now.<br/>
<br/>
He sprints to the small yellow house at the street as fast as his feet can carry him. He tires embarrassingly fast and makes a mental note to start working out again. Just because he’s a surrogate dad to twelve doesn’t mean he has to have a dadbod. Coming to a stop in front of the dark oak door, Harry takes a moment to straighten himself out before taking a deep breath and knocking, loudly, as to be heard through the thick wood.<br/>
<br/>
Harry smiles as the door opens, holding out the bouquet of tulips, “You look beautiful, baby.”<br/>
<br/>
Ginny smiles, taking the bouquet, “Thank you, Harry.” She says, stepping onto her porch, locking the door behind her.<br/>
<br/>
Harry slips his hand into Ginny’s, interlacing their fingers, “Ready?”<br/>
<br/>
Ginny nods, “Ready.”<br/>
<br/>
And with their destination clear in mind, Harry disapparates.</p><p>Arriving at the burrow has never lost its magic for Harry. Every time he appears in front of the large, lopsided building, he’s left breathless. The windows are lit warmly, the pink willow trees Molly planted last May sway softly in the breeze, laughter always pours from inside the house, and Harry feels like he’s home. Ginny squeezes his hand, and he looks at her. The moonlight washes her face in an ethereal glow, reflecting beautifully in her eyes and hair. Looking at Ginny, something twinges in his chest. This lighting always makes her look like Lily. Harry squeezes her hand back. Ginny is his home; she is what connects him to his home. Without her, Harry’s not sure he’d fully belong. They walk, hand in hand, into the burrow. The Weasley clan shout their greetings as Molly pulls him into a tight hug. Harry allows himself to be coddled, smiling and nodding as Molly asks her usual questions.</p><p>
  <em>Are you eating enough, dear? How are the kids doing? When are you two going to get married?</em>
</p><p>The last question makes Harry and Ginny share a secret smile. They’d been planning this proposal for a little over a month now, since Draco had sent him the copy of <em>Pride and Prejudice, </em>and they decided the bi-monthly Weasley dinner was the perfect time to do it. Harry stays talking to Molly for a few more moments, updating her on the home and talking to her about what he’s currently reading. She hasn’t heard of most of the titles, but Molly’s motherly instincts kick in and she smiles and nods and acts interested none-the-less. Eventually, Ginny comes over and Harry leaves her to talk to her mom in private, wandering off in search of Ron and Hermione.<br/>
He spots the pair sitting in the front room sitting in a makeshift loveseat in front of the fire. Harry takes in the sloppy transfiguration and knows immediately that Hermione’s trying furniture transfiguration again. She’s never had much luck in the very specific subject, but every so often, she’ll give it another go, only the be dejected when it doesn’t turn out perfect. Ron’s never minded though. He reminds himself to subtly ask Ron where his parents got the new loveseat when Hermione wakes up to boost her confidence. Right now, though, she is clearly not awake, with her head leaning against Ron’s shoulder and her eyes closed. Catching Ron’s eye, Harry goes to sit in front of him, back getting warmed by the fire.<br/>
<br/>
“Hey, mate,” Harry whispers, trying to avoid waking the pregnant Hermione, “where’s Rosie?”<br/>
<br/>
“Upstairs taking a nap,” Ron says, “She was a right wanker to put down tonight. Kept saying she wanted to wait for her Uncle Harry before her nap.”<br/>
<br/>
Harry smiles, “I love that kid.”<br/>
<br/>
Ron chuckles, and they settle into a comfortable silence, listening to the crackling fire and Hermione’s soft breathing.<br/>
<br/>
“Are you going to do it tonight?”<br/>
<br/>
Harry nods, turning away from Ron to stare into the fire. He’s still not sure he’s making the right decision. Yes, his parents got married at nineteen, but he’d hardly call that the norm. Plenty of people date for twenty years before getting married. Or they never get married and that’s okay too. But when Ginny hesitantly broached the topic a month prior, saying her mother had been asking again, Harry had been in the right mood, <em>Pride and Prejudice’s </em>romantic words making the idea sound more appealing. Ron sighs and Harry hears shuffling behind him before Ron plops himself on the floor beside Harry and puts an arm around his shoulders.<br/>
<br/>
“I know marriage is a big deal to you, mate, but Ginny will say yes.”<br/>
<br/>
“I know.” Harry says, still not looking away from the fire.<br/>
<br/>
He knows, Harry knows Ginny will say yes. That’s not the issue. If he closes his eyes, Harry can picture a dozen Christmases and birthdays. He can see holidays at the Burrow, Ron and Hermione at his wedding, the irreversible tying of him to the Weasleys. He can feel the comfort of truly belonging. But underneath it all, he can feel a wriggling sense of <em>wrong. </em>Harry pushes away the feeling, how can marrying his girlfriend of six years be wrong?<br/>
<br/>
“You know,” Ron says, a goofy smile spreading across his lips, “for years now I’ve called you my brother, but now you’ll actually be in the family.”<br/>
<br/>
Harry knows Ron means no harm by saying that. That he’s just excited about the looming wedding and having his best friend be related to him by marriage, however, every time Ron says something about Harry <em>really</em> being in the family, it makes him feel like maybe he was never in the family in the first place.<br/>
<br/>
In the end, Harry and Ginny spent way too much time planning this proposal for him to chicken out, so drawing on all his deep rooted stubbornness, Harry is determined to see it through. So, after dinner, right as Molly serves up dessert, Harry drops to one knee. Hermione screams and Rose screams in her own three-year-old way, Arthur nods approvingly, Ron takes pictures, Molly drops her pie, and Ginny stares at him for a moment, an unidentifiable emotion flickering across her face before she whispers “yes” and gives him a chaste kiss.<br/>
<br/>
As various friends and family members come up to him to give their congratulations, Harry couldn’t help but notice that nagging feeling is back, telling him something isn’t right, but looking at Ginny, smiling and talking with Hermione and Molly, Harry shoves the feeling away again, locking it deep in the depths of his heart.<br/>
<br/>
“This is where I’m meant to be.” He whispers to himself, smiling as Ron shoots him a thumbs up.<br/>
<br/>
“Well,” Ginny says, turning to face Harry, “I guess we better find a wedding planner.”</p>
