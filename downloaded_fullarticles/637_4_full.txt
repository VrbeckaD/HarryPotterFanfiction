      <a href="/works/17636087/chapters/41585093">Chapter 1</a>: The First Spell

  <div class="userstuff module" role="article">
    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>
  <b>AUGUST 6, 1971</b>
</p><p></p><p>Monsieur Auclair? Regulus asked, his eyes wide and curious. What will Hogwarts be like?</p><p>Sirius Black<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span>looked up from his book<em>Infamous Tales of European Wizarding Families</em>and eyed his tutor, curious as to how hed choose to respond. Regulus, of course, had asked their parents this question nearly a dozen times before Walburga had hexed his mouth shut. Neither Orion nor Walburga were particular forthcoming with answers to this particular question, other than furious mutterings of Mudbloods and blood traitors, roaming the halls like they have the right to be there.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Alphonse Auclair, the gruff and most recent tutor for the Noble and Most Ancient House of Black, let out a grunt. Sirius highly doubted Ollivander would give such an offer to anyone other than a highly displeased Walburga Black, but the alternative seemed to be incurring her wrath, which, from experience, Sirius knew to be a far worse fate.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Walburga didnt speak to him the whole way home, but Sirius couldnt be bothered to care. He finally had a wand. A wand that had chosen him, specifically, and one laced with contradictions, down to its very making, much like Sirius Black himself.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p>Apparating right outside the Muggle-repelling wards that surround Number Twelve Grimmauld Place, Sirius immediately sensed something was<em> off</em>. His mother, calm and collected as ever, shoved Sirius away from her side and opened the front door. House-elf skulls and ancient family members in ornate portraits adorned the entrance hall.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Orion and Kreacher shall be around shortly, Walburga said. We shall discuss your punishment for disobeying my direct order and consorting with that <em>Potter</em> boy then.</p><p>Sirius bowed his head and winced, almost anticipating the punishment. Whatever his parents cooked up, it was bound to leave him aching for days. Yes, Mother, he said, before slinking off.</p><p>Temporarily off the hook, Sirius wove his way through the halls, twirling his new wand between his fingers. Regulus? he called. Reg, wherere you at?</p><p>He paused, waiting for the reply. The house remained silent. Then, he heard the front door open once more, and the booming voice of his father carry throughout the halls as Orion asked Walburga how their trip to Ollivanders had gone. His parents voices defended into hushed, angry whispers as Sirius turned another corner. Still no sign of his brother.</p><p>Sirius frowned. Regulus? he called again.</p><p>Still no reply.</p><p>Sirius turned on his heel, heading back towards their study, where hed left Regulus with Auclair earlier. Regulus? he said, hesitantly, as he pushed open the door.</p><p>Sirius registered the muffled cry before his brain fully wrapped himself around the sight. Auclair, still with that brilliantly pink streak in his hair, had Regulus pinned against the opposite wall, both hands clasped around his throat. Reguluss feet skimmed the groundhe wasnt tall enough to touchand Auclair supported Reguluss entire weight by the hands around his throat. Sirius gaped in horror, dazed for a second, as he watched Reguluss lips turn blue.</p><p>Youre a <em>filthy</em>, entitled little <em>bastard</em>, you are, Auclair hissed, his face contorted in fury.</p><p>What the fuck are you doing?! Sirius shouted, pointing his wand at Auclair. Let him go!</p><p>Auclair whirled his head around, but did not release Regulus. Regulus clawed at the tutors hands and mouthed Siriuss name, over and over, his eyes wild with panic.</p><p>LET HIM GO! LET HIM GO! LET HIM <em>GO</em>! Sirius roared.</p><p>Hell get what he deserves, right bastard, Auclair growled. Only need one heir, after all.</p><p>Before Sirius even had time to properly think about it, he raised his and shouted, <em>CRUCIO!</em></p><p>A bolt of green light shot out from his brand new, enchanted-willow-with-unicorn-hair-core wand. Auclair went rigid for a split second, the fell to the ground, wailing out in pain. He contorted on the ground, trying to wiggle out of the curse, but to no avail. Sirius held him there, writhing in unspeakable pain.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Regulus dropped to the ground, clutching his throat and coughing.</p><p>SIRIUS! yelled his mother, from somewhere behind him.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Then, out of the corner of his eye, Sirius saw his father come to stand next to him. Orion drew his wand and pointed it at Auclair. Release him, Sirius, Orion said, remarkably calm, given the situation.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>After three more seconds, Sirius obeyed and lowered his wand. Auclair let out a long, drawn out moan of relief. Sirius glanced at his father, suddenly terrified of incurring punishment for torturing their tutor. He Sirius started, pointing an accusing finger at Auclair. He was And Regulus</p><p>Sirius cut off, when suddenly, Regulus ran across the room and threw his arms around Siriuss neck, sobbing. Ignoring both their parents, Sirius held his brother tight against him, carding fingers through Reguluss thick, black hair, trying to soothe him. Eventually, he forced Regulus to step back, so Sirius could get a good look at his neck. Dark purple bruises were already forming on Reguluss skin, in the perfect shape of Auclairs hands. Sirius growled in anger and gently pulled Regulus back against his chest. Only then, does Sirius dare to look up at his father.</p><p>Orions expression had hardened. Auclair, what happened?</p><p>From his place on the floor, Auclair let out a broken, bitter laugh. Little bastard he coughed, loudly, and Sirius was sure he saw blood come up. Fucking <em>bastard</em> said my family were nothing but blood traitors, the whores of Europe, that</p><p><em>Crucio! </em>Orion yelled, flicking his wand. Auclair collapsed again, letting loose another wail of pain. You will not speak of my <em>son</em> in that manner, Auclair, he spat. Orion turned back to Sirius and Regulus. In your own words, Regulus. What happened?</p><p>Regulus kept his face buried in Siriuss chest, but risked a glance up at their father. What he said, Regulus muttered. Then he grabbed me by the throat and started choking me.</p><p>Orion nodded, then turned his focus back to the writhing tutor. You were given the terms of discipline upon your employment, Auclair. No one of lesser blood was to lay hands on either of my sons. If you had problems, you were to take it up with Walburga, who would then handle their punishment. Only a Black is fit to punish a Black.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Auclair had now been reduced to mere moans and whimpers, as Orion continued to hold the curse.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Then, catching Siriuss eye once more, Orion said, Im proud of you, Sirius. Cruciatus as your first curse. Not many can claim that honor, even amongst the Blacks. Perhaps that new wand of yours is not as useless as your mother seems to think.</p><p><em>Im proud of you, Sirius</em>.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>In his nearly eleven years of life, Sirius Black had never heard his father utter those words. He managed a small nod, in response, not quite knowing what to say.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Since it was you that cast the first curse, I find it only fair for you to deliver the final punishment, Sirius, Orion continued. He nodded to Siriuss new wand. Finish it.</p><p>Sirius was well aware that that was supposed to mean something, but his brain seemed to be having immense difficulty in computing his fathers words. F-finish it? he asked, rather stupidly.</p><p>Avada Kedavra, Sirius, Walburga said, impatiently. He deserves to die, for what hes done. Now, on with it!</p><p>Siriuss jaw dropped and he clutched Regulus closer to him. No, he choked out. Im not going to kill him.</p><p>His mothers eyes flashed. You <em>dare</em> disobey me again, after what you pulled in</p><p>Orion raised a hand. Enough, Walburga, he said. He turned to Sirius and gave him a long, hard glare. Ill do it this time, Sirius, but just this once and only because you had the gall to perform the first curse. Next time, if you refuse to finish it, youll take his place.</p><p>Sirius felt his blood turn to ice at the threat. Regulus let out a sob.</p><p>The Blacks all turned their attention to their sniveling tutor, still huddled on his knees. No! Please, Master<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>He never got to finish.</p><p><em>Avada Kedavra! </em>A green bolt shot from Orions wand and Alphonse Auclair dropped dead.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Sirius stared down at those empty eyes and felt cold sneaking up his spine. Auclair didnt <em>look </em>dead, at least nothing like his grandmother had, lying pale and lifeless in her coffin. Auclair looked as though he were merely staring off into space, ready to be roused by him or Regulus, in time for their next lesson and bout of bickering.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Orion tucked his wand back into his robes, as if it were nothing at all that hed just killed a man in front of his two, young sons. Turning to his wife, he said, Have Kreacher clean this up. Sirius, well discuss your punishment for your actions in Diagon Alley tomorrow.</p><p>Sirius found himself nodding along.</p><p>Good, Orion huffed. Now, off with you. No use standing around gaping, staring at a dead blood traitor. Ill be in my study.</p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p>Sirius didnt go down to dinner that night. Though, he doubted if he did show up, that hed actually be given anything to eat. He was well aware that whatever pride his parents had felt at him performing the Cruciatus curse was short-lived, especially given his afternoon spent with James Potter and the fact that Sirius apparently didnt have the backbone to perform the killing curse.</p><p>He lay flat on his back, on his four poster bed, the dark green duvet tucked right up to his chin. Hes not entirely certain how long hes been laying there, staring straight up at the ceiling, but hed guess a few hours, at least.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Hed performed the Cruciatus curse, willingly and without hesitation. Andromeda had told him a few years ago that, in order to perform any one of the Unforgivable Curses, you had to mean it, had to feel the rage welling up in your very soul.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Sirius had felt it, alright. Hed meant it and, to what he thinks should be his great dismay, hes unwaveringly certain that hed do it again.</p><p>Sirius Black was eleven years old and, were it not for the wards on Number Twelve Grimmauld Place, the Ministry of Magic would throw him in Azkaban for the rest of his life.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Maybe he deserved it.</p><p>Maybe hed been fated for Azkaban from the moment hed been born.</p><p>Eventually, after what was probably another hour or so, Sirius pulled out his wand. It felt heavier than it had in Ollivanders and the smooth, willow wood appeared half a shade darker. Maybe it was his imagination, but he doubted it. His wand had a unicorn hair core. Ollivander had said the core would wither into nothing if it were forced to perform Dark Magic, and that the first spell would determine the wands fate.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>It seemed, then, no matter what he chose, Sirius Black was fated for darkness. Hed wither into nothing, just like his wand.</p><p>Sirius sat up when he heard his door creak open. Soft feet padded across the wood floor, until there was a rustle of covers and Sirius felt Regulus wiggle close to him on the bed. Regulus lay on his side, facing his older brother, black eyes wide, even in the darkness.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Siri? Regulus whispered.</p><p>Yeah, Reg?<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Thank you.</p><p>Sirius turned on his own side, facing Regulus. They were pressed close together, their feet tangling and fingers brushing underneath the duvet. Sirius reached up, his fingers skimming the dark bruises on Reguluss neck.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>I can heal that for you, Sirius said, suddenly. Reguluss eyes went wide. I know a spell. Or, well, I read about one. Cant really fix that sort of thing without a wand, but I could give it a go. Do you trust me?</p><p>Regulus nodded and bared his neck.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Sirius wasnt entirely certain he trusted himself, especially with his recently-cursed wand now pointed at his little brothers throat. He drew in a sharp breath, sent a prayer to whoever or whatever might be listening, and muttered, <em>Episkey.</em></p><p>Sirius watched in relative amazement as the bruises faded into pale, smooth, untarnished skin.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Reguluss hand flew to his neck and he poked the skin, his face breaking out in a grin once he realized it no longer hurt. Thanks, he said, earnestly. He gestured to Siriuss hand. That your new wand? Can I see it?</p><p>Sirius hesitated only a second before handing his wand off to his brother.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Wow, Regulus said, running a finger across the smooth, straight wood. Whats it made of?</p><p>Enchanted willow wood, Sirius said, with a burst of pride that frankly surprised him. Ollivander said its from an special tree, at Hogwarts. He said it guarded a secret.</p><p>Reguluss eyes went wide. What kind of secret?</p><p>Sirius huffed a laugh and poked Regulus in the side. His little brother let out an indignant squeak. If he told me what it was, it wouldnt be a secret, now would it?</p><p>Regulus giggled.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Ill try to find out, yeah? Sirius said. Once Im at Hogwarts. Kind of want to know myself, after what Ollivander said. He seemed to think this wand was real special. Sirius again thought about the dying unicorn hair core of his wand and felt an unbelievable twinge of guilt.</p><p>Regulus, of course, didnt know that the wand was slowly dying. Youll write me and tell me the secret, once you find out, right? he asked.</p><p>Course I will, Sirius said, with a huff.</p><p>Regulus grinned and handed the wand back to Sirius. Do you think Ill get a special wand next year, too?</p><p>I dont doubt it. Sirius poked him again, this time tickling him a bit more. Regulus giggled relentlessly and retaliated.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>After theyd both regained their breath (and Sirius had taken a good kick to a most undesirable location), the brothers lay on their backs, shoulder to shoulder, and stared up at the ceiling.</p><p>Sirius?</p><p>Yeah?</p><p>Did you get me anything?</p><p>Merlin, I almost forgot. Sirius sat back up, a wicked grin on his face. He pointed his wand towards his dresser and said, <em>Accio Cosmos.</em></p><p>The little tea-cup fluttered over to them. Regulus snatched it out of the air, running his fingers over the intricate patterns. What is it?</p><p>Cosmos-in-a-Cup, Sirius said, with a smirk. Tossing his wand aside, Sirius flicked his wrist and the tea cup filled with water. The two brothers stared into the cup, millions of millions of stars swirling in the tiny space. Splash the water on the ceiling or something, to see all the stars.</p><p>Really? Regulus asked, his eyes wide, reflecting the tiny stars twinkling in the cup.</p><p>Try it.</p><p>Without another word, Regulus held the teacup up and splashed the water towards the ceiling. Instead of liquid, the splash rained down stars around them, engulfing them, swirling everywhere, unto infinity. The Milky Way streaked across the ceiling of Siriuss bedroom, all purple and blue and astoundingly white light.</p><p>S beautiful, Siri, Regulus whispered, and yeah, it was. Regulus nudged Sirius. So where are we at, then?</p><p>Sirius smiled, studying the cosmos above him. Theres Orions belt, he said, pointing, and neither of them bothered to hide a grimace. Which means Ah, <em>there</em>. Right south of Orion, thats Sirius. The heart of Canis Major and the brightest star in the night sky.</p><p>Regulus reached up, tracing the outline of Canis Major with his finger. Good dog, he giggled.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Sirius poked him. Fucking sap, you are, he muttered. He pointed back at the swirling stars. Regulus is there, brightest one in the Leo constellation.</p><p>Leo? Regulus said. The lion?</p><p>Yep, Sirius replied. Probably means youll be in Gryffindor.</p><p>Regulus grabbed the pillow under his head and swatted Sirius. No! Take it back!</p><p>Sirius laughed and shoved him away. Nah, youll be Slytherin, like the rest of us, he said, after a moment. Besides, itd be a shame if all those Slytherin scarves Mother got went to waste.</p><p>Suppose so, Regulus said. He looked back at the Cosmos-in-a-Cup, still clutched reverently between his fingers, even as the galaxy swirled above them. Sirius? he asked. Sirius hummed in response. Regulus gestured to the tea cup. Is this why youre in trouble?</p><p>Sirius let out a sigh. Nah, he said. Then, he reconsidered. Well, not specifically.</p><p>Specifically, Regulus said, slowly, what did you do?</p><p>I snuck away from Mother while she was at Gringotts, went to a joke shop, exchanged friendly banter with the infamous son-of-blood-traitors, James Potter, blew all the feathers off a rather annoying psychic pigeon, bought you a cup of stars, then paid a traitorous goblin twenty Galleons to shut up about all of it. He then betrayed me to Walburga Black, and here we are.</p><p>Regulus seemed to run that all through his head again. Youre friends with James Potter?</p><p>Sirius shrugged. Spose so. What of it?</p><p>Mother and Father wont like it.</p><p>Didnt think they would.</p><p>Then why bother?</p><p>Hes funny, Sirius said, considering it. His hair sticks up in every direction and he likes Quidditch. He wants me to teach him wandless magic.</p><p>Regulus nodded. Doesnt it he trailed off, reconsidering his question. Doesnt it <em>hurt</em>?</p><p>Yeah, Sirius replied, letting out a sigh. He thought of the three separate times James shook his hand, of James throwing his arm over Siriuss shoulder, of the casual touches, of the easy, friendly intimacy James Potter constantly initiated. Sirius shuddered, as the memory of the pain nearly overwhelmed him. Course it does, he said, his voice quieter now. But I figure if were going to be friends, Ill just have to avoid touching him, is all.</p><p>Sirius, of course, had no idea how he was going to deter James Potter, but it felt good to say, at least.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Was it like Dromedas boyfriend?</p><p>Nah, Sirius replied. It still hurt. <em>Merlin</em>, it hurt, but not like Ted. James is still a pure-blood, even if hes from a family of blood traitors.</p><p>Regulus hummed in response. Lot of those today. Blood traitors.</p><p>Sirius felt that darkness creeping back up his spine. Despite the cosmos above him, despite the comfort of his brother next to him, Sirius felt the weight on his chest, overwhelming and all-consuming. He was sure he was about to drown, lost in the darkness forever.</p><p>Orion-fucking-Black said he was proud of me, Sirius said, rather surprising himself. He didnt want to talk about this, didnt want Regulus to see any more of the darkness and the nothing than necessary. If Regulus knew... Merlin, if Regulus knew what that Unforgivable Curse might have cost Sirius, hed go running for the hills, leaving Sirius alone, once and for all, with a suddenly empty cosmos shining above his head.</p><p>You dont sound happy about it, Regulus muttered, reaching for Siriuss hand. Sirius wove their fingers together and held on tight.</p><p>Im not, he said, his voice harsher than he meant. Not even a little bit.</p><p>Regulus gave him a strange look. Why not?</p><p>Sirius scoffed at his brothers indignant tone. Because to get Father to say that again, Id have to do <em>that</em> again, and probably worse. And And I dont think I can.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>In fact, Sirius Black is quite certain that hed rather die, in any one of the myriad of painful ways<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span>his parents might devise.</p><p>Regulus frowned and squeezed his hand. But Siri, you saved me. Youd do that again, wouldnt you?</p><p>Sirius swatted him. Of course, but Reg, did you really say all that to him? Call him a blood traitor and all?</p><p>Only after you did! Regulus protested.</p><p>Sirius closed his eyes, swallowing his regret. I know, he muttered. I know, okay?</p><p>Then what</p><p>I tortured him to make up for my own loud mouth, which, in essence, means I tortured him because he was a blood traitor.</p><p>Regulus was genuinely confused. So?</p><p>Sirius opened his eyes and stared at his brother. Reguluss black eyesso unlike his ownreflected the galaxy above them, around them, inside them. They were beautiful, his brothers eyes, and so undeserving of the darkness that surrounded them.</p><p>He didnt get to choose his family, Reg. He couldnt help what he was, Sirius said, his voice quiet. Neither can we.</p><p>He chose to attack me, Sirius.<span class="Apple-converted-space"></span></p><p>Sirius shook his head. Hes a pure-blood wizard, called inferior and a blood traitor his whole lifewhether its true or notbecause his lineage will never measure up against the Noble and Most Ancient House of Black. Sirius couldnt keep the sarcastic edge out of his voice at the mention of his own bloodline. Years later, some snot-nosed punkor rather, a snot-nosed punk and his dashing big brotherremind him of what he is, of the type of blood runs through his veins, so he snaps. He attacks said snot-nosed punk, forcing his dashing big brother to step up and throw down the Cruciatus curse. This pure-blood wizard falls dead a blood traitor, because he stood against the Noble and Most Ancient House of Black, thereby fulfilling every taunt and jeer thats been thrown at him over the years. He never had a choice, Reg. He was born to become a blood traitor. He was born to die as one.</p><p>Theyre silent a long moment, the stars blazing bright and beautiful just out of reach.</p><p>Finally, Regulus said, If thats true, then what are we born to be?</p><p>Sirius swallowed the lump in his throat. Hed give anything not to be the one to share this particular tragedy with his younger brother.</p><p>Theres darkness in our veins, Sirius said, eventually, gazing into the infinite mysteries of the cosmos spread out above them. This God, this hand of fate, or whatever it isthe very monster that wrote the destinies of pure-bloods and blood traitors, who wrote the darkness into our very souls Thats something I can never forgive. You and me, Regulus? Well, we were born to kill God.</p><p>Regulus looked at him for a long moment, then sighed. He curled up next to Sirius, his head on Siriuss chest, right below the tattoo that caused so much pain. Regulus clutched the Cosmos-in-a-Cup carefully between them. Thanks for the stars, Siri.</p><p>Sirius carded his fingers through his brothers hair and planted a kiss on the top of his head. Youre welcome, Reg.</p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p>
