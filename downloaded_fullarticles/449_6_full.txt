    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>
  <strong>
    <em>JAMES POTTER, T-MINUS 3 DAYS</em>
  </strong>
</p>
<p>James' mother always told him not to meddle in places he didn't belong.</p>
<p>Which was real rich coming from her, given that Euphemia Potter would probably meddle in nearly every aspect of James' life given the opportunity, but she'd insisted that a mother being invested in her son's well-being was a totally different concept entirely. But James meddling in his friends' lives? Entirely off limits, she'd told him. Highly dangerous territory.</p>
<p>And like many other tidbits of motherly advice he's received over the years, he's made the (questionable) executive decision to ignore it completely.</p>
<p>In his defense, James is largely convinced that meddling is the<em>only</em>way anything's going to happen at this point; his idiot of a flatmate has fancied the bloke down the hall for<em>months</em>now, and it's rather obvious to anyone paying even the slightest bit of attention that Remus fancies Sirius right back.</p>
<p>Unless you're Sirius, that is.</p>
<p>Remus is also equally oblivious, so really, James really has no other option than to get involved himself. Otherwise, he'll probably be subjected to their stupid pining glances every day for the rest of his life, because it's becoming rather clear to him that neither of them are going to own up to their feelings themselves.</p>
<p>It's ridiculous, really.</p>
<p>So since they've left him no other choice, the obvious next step here is to recruit a partner in crime. And James has the perfect one: Remus' flatmate.</p>
<p>Who James just so happens to be<em>slightly</em>in love with.</p>
<p>Lily Evans is, in his mind, quite possibly the most stunning human being to ever grace the planet with her presence. She's fit as hell, which is admittedly the first thing James noticed about her, but she's also brilliant and sarcastic and passionate, and every time she opens her mouth, James becomes just a little bit more infatuated.</p>
<p>For the first few weeks after Lily and Remus had moved into the flat three doors down from him and Sirius, James had assumed Lily was Remus' girlfriend. And it felt fitting, in a karmic sort of way - it'd be just the perfect sort of hell for both him<em>and</em>Padfoot to both be madly attracted to two people in a happy relationship with one another.</p>
<p>Although really, the news that they were just completely platonic roommates has created a different, but equally torturous kind of hell. Because if she's<em>not</em>in a relationship, it means there's nothing stopping James from actually having a shot with her.</p>
<p>Well, nothing except his fantastic ability to put his foot in his mouth every time he even so much as attempts to flirt with her.</p>
<p>It's really quite pathetic, the way he gets around her. He's never been<em>bad</em>with girls - he was known in secondary school for being a massive flirt, for heaven's sake - so the fact that this one redhead sends every suave comment he's got right out the window and turns his brain to putty is absurd and massively unfair, really.</p>
<p>And even when he<em>does</em>manage to say something remotely smooth, she just flushes and laughs it off, making it obvious that she sees him as nothing more than a friend.</p>
<p>So honestly, it would be in his best interest emotionally to just avoid Lily altogether, especially with Valentine's Day looming and all those stupid fucking teddy bears and chocolates and pink hearts serving as a constant reminder that the one girl he'd really like to be taking on a date is completely and entirely uninterested in him.</p>
<p>But he's putting that particular self-serving interest aside in favour of helping his best friend. Lily's undoubtedly the best person for this job, given that she's Remus' roommate and has the same level of vested interest in seeing Sirius and Remus get together as he does.</p>
<p>Which has brought him here, knocking on Lily and Remus' door while he<em>knows</em>that Remus has class and Lily doesn't, just so he can recruit her for this task.</p>
<p>There's some rustling on the other side of the door after he knocks, and then the door swings open to reveal Lily, looking a bit perplexed. She was clearly in the middle of tying her hair up when he knocked, because she's still wrapping an elastic around the giant mass of auburn hair atop her head with one hand as she appraises him.</p>
<p>"James?" she says, eyeing him. "What's up?"</p>
<p>"I - I'm here on a matchmaking mission," he tells her, determined not to get distracted by the way that the green in her tank top matches her eyes, and how one strap of it is dangling precariously off her freckled shoulder to reveal a black bra strap, and -</p>
<p>Mission. He's here on a mission.</p>

<p>"For your roommate and my roommate, both of whom are entirely oblivious sods," he explains.</p>

<p>Sirius hums - a contented sort of sound - and Remus feels the vibrations of it resonate through his chest. Remus freezes, convinced that Sirius is waking up and about to jump out of bed and away from Remus at any second, but nothing comes of it. He stays exactly where he is.</p>
<p>He chances a look upwards at Sirius' face - partially out of some sort of uncontrollable curiosity as to what Sirius looks like when he's sleeping - and is startled to see a pair of grey eyes looking down at him with an unbelievably soft expression.</p>
<p>If Remus didn't know any better, he'd say that look belongs to someone who's<em>incredibly</em>besotted.</p>
<p>But the moment - and the expression - fade as soon as their eyes meet. The soft look is replaced by one of mild panic, and Remus instantly starts trying to untangle himself from Sirius.</p>
<p>"Sorry," Remus says, his voice still rough from sleep. "I tend to get cuddly when I sleep."</p>
<p>That's a blatant lie - Remus has shared a bed with another person platonically on numerous occasions before, and never once has he woke up literally wrapped around them.</p>
<p>Sirius just shrugs, sitting up groggily. " 'S all good. I'll never complain about waking up with a pretty boy in my arms."</p>
<p>It's too damn early for Remus' stomach to be doing fucking somersaults like this.</p>
<p>He tries to brush it off, like he usually does with Sirius' comments like that, because he makes them at everyone - they don't mean anything special. But he still can't get that soft look on Sirius' face out of his head - that one's probably been branded into his head for perpetuity.</p>
<p>Could it mean?</p>
<p>Sirius picks up his phone and barks with laughter, which pulls Remus out of his own head.</p>
<p>"I've got like twenty unread messages from James," Sirius explains, holding his phone out for Remus to see.</p>
<p>The screen is indeed a constant stream of grey text bubbles.</p>
<p></p>
<p>
  <em>sirius</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>what the fuck</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>unlock the fucking door</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>you royal fucking arse</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>Missed FaceTime Audio call (2)</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>fuck you guys are already asleep aren't you</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>fuckkkkkk</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>fuck fuck fuck</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>i've set a record for fucks in this series of texts haven't i</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>but it's warranted now</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>i'm so fucked</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>i came back to lily's place bc i'm locked out and she's wearing this black lace thing and FUCK</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>i'm SO fucked</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>and it's all your fault</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>like, congrats on sorting your shit out with remus and all</em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>but also fuck you</em>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Remus is already laughing before he finishes the stream of texts. "I think this probably means we can call this mission a success?"</p>
<p>"Well, considering it sounds like Lily bloody seduced him in the end, I think so."</p>
<p>Sirius types back a response - something along the lines of<em>not sorry, glad you two had fun last night ;)</em>and gets up out of bed abruptly. "You want breakfast? As promised yesterday, it's the one thing I'm not rubbish at cooking."</p>
<p>"Er, sure."</p>
<p>He's trying<em>real</em>hard not to imagine that this is a<em>real</em>morning-after with someone he's<em>really</em>hooked up with, because it sure is starting to feel like one.</p>
<p>Sirius throws a T-shirt and joggers on - which, in all honesty, is probably very good for Remus' sanity - and leaves the room.</p>
<p>By the time Remus has gotten up, checked his phone (which<em>also</em>had two missed calls from James), and gone to the bathroom, Sirius is smack dab in the middle of making eggs and bacon, with two cups of coffee sitting on the counter, steam swirling up out of the mugs.</p>
<p>"The black one's yours - one packet of sugar, no milk."</p>
<p>He's briefly shocked that Sirius knows his coffee order - when was the last time they even had coffee together? - and just opens and closes his mouth like a fish a few times before formulating a, "Thanks."</p>
<p>He sits down in one of the barstools, in front of the Monopoly game left from the night before, and contents himself to just watch Sirius cook while he gingerly sips at his own coffee.</p>
<p>"James texted back," Sirius says casually, while loading up the plates. "Go check his Snapchat."</p>
<p>Remus does as he's told, and sure enough, there's a video on his story of James and Lily, presumably from this morning, where both he and Lily have the heart filter on their faces as they grin at the camera, before James turns to kiss her on the cheek. The caption underneath reads,<em>is it too late to call her my valentine?</em></p>
<p>"You weren't wrong that James wouldn't waste any time," he tells Sirius, setting his phone back down.</p>
<p>When the other man comes over, brandishing two plates loaded with eggs, bacon, and toast that he sets on the counter, shoving the Monopoly board out of the way, Remus has sat there pondering things for just long enough that he's determined that there is another, majorly significant flaw in their plan.</p>
<p>"Sirius?"</p>
<p>"Hm?"</p>
<p>"What are we going to tell them? James and Lily, I mean."</p>
<p>He looks confused at that. "What do you mean?"</p>
<p>"Well, we, er," Remus begins, stumbling over his words a bit, "we kind of made it seem like we got together last night, and if we<em>don't</em>want them thinking that we played them back the same way they did to us, we er, we kind of have to deal with that somehow."</p>
<p>Sirius falls into his chair with none of the dramatic flair he usually has. "Oh."</p>
<p>"Yeah."</p>
<p>He seems to think on that for a while, staring at his plate, before turning to Remus with an uncertain look. "We could, er, fake being together for a little bit?"</p>
<p>That's certainly an option, but part of it doesn't really sit right with Remus. Namely, the 'fake' part. He's not sure how effectively he could<em>fake</em>being in a relationship with Sirius without it absolutely destroying him.</p>
<p>He takes another sip of his coffee, willing it to give him some sort of previously unearthed confidence.</p>
<p>Fuck, he's really going to do this, isn't he? But he<em>can't</em>have imagined the look in Sirius' eyes this morning though. Even his wildest stretches of the imagination can't explain that away, can't explain away the depth of affection there. He takes a deep breath, and his response comes out in a rush. "What if it wasn't fake?"</p>
<p>Sirius just blinks at him - Remus isn't sure if he's still processing what he just said, or if he just genuinely couldn't make out Remus' words. "What?"</p>
<p>"I - look," Remus begins, rubbing the back of his neck nervously, "I kind of fancy you. A lot. And I don't really want to fake being together with you, because I'd much rather<em>actually</em>be together with you."</p>
<p>"What?" Sirius repeats, beginning to sound a bit like a broken record.</p>
<p>"And maybe I totally misread the situation, but I'm starting to think that that maybe there's a chance you feel the same way?" Remus picks up his mug again, clutching it in his hands to stop them from fidgeting. "I just - last night and this morning, and I could be<em>completely</em>wrong, but - "</p>
<p>"You're not," Sirius interrupts. "I've been fucking head over heels for you since the day you moved in and I found you on the fourth floor stairwell kicking your coffee table and calling it a 'shitting tonne of shit-bricks.' "</p>
<p>Now it's Remus' turn to be stunned into one-word responses. "Oh."</p>
<p>And then, "Really?<em>That</em>was the moment?"</p>
<p>Sirius just shrugs. "You're cute when you're flustered."</p>
<p>"You do realise I'm flustered, like, all the time, right?"</p>
<p>"And you're cute all the time," he replies resolutely. "Although really, I can't believe you're<em>just</em>now picking up on this - I've been flirting with you since the day we met."</p>
<p>"You flirt with everyone though!" Remus defends, setting his mug down on the counter with a thud.</p>
<p>Sirius cocks his head at that. "Do I, though?"</p>
<p>"You make those comments to James all the time."</p>
<p>He snorts. "Yeah, well, James is practically my brother. We say stupid shit to each other all the time. But anyone else though? It's only ever been you."</p>
<p>The more Remus thinks about it, he actually can't remember a single time Sirius has been flirty with Lily, or with any of their other university friends.</p>
<p>It's it's just been him.</p>
<p>"Shit."</p>
<p>Sirius laughs at that, and it's happy and full-bodied and makes Remus feel warm inside in all the best ways. "So we're doing this for real then?"</p>
<p>Remus nods, more sure of this than he's been of anything else all morning. "Yeah."</p>
<p>"In that case" Sirius stands up from his seat and moves towards Remus, stepping into the space between his legs, so close that Remus has to turn his head upwards to see Sirius' face from where he's sitting.</p>
<p>And then Sirius' lips are on his own, and it's nothing short of fucking<em>brilliant</em>. He tastes like coffee, and Remus is delighted to discover, as he wraps his arms around Sirius' neck and pulls him closer, that his hair is<em>exactly</em>as soft as he'd imagined it would be. He finds out that Sirius makes these little noises of pleasure into the kiss every time Remus tugs on it a little bit, and Remus quickly decides that this is quite possibly the best discovery he's ever made.</p>
<p>Sirius' fingers grip onto Remus' shirt - the one that's technically<em>his</em>- like it's the only thing keeping him grounded, and Remus feels the exact same way. He feels like he's flying, floating through the breeze on nothing but pure joy and bliss.</p>
<p>After what could've been thirty seconds or fifteen minutes, Sirius pulls back. They're both breathing hard, hearts hammering out a frantic beat in perfect time, and Sirius' face splits into a blindingly bright grin.</p>
<p>"Just think," he says breathlessly, eyes roaming Remus' face like he's trying to commit this moment to memory, "we could've been doing that for<em>months</em>."</p>
<p>Remus smirks. "Looks like we've got a lot of lost time to make up for."</p>
<p>A few hours later, back in Sirius' bed all over again but this time under very different circumstances, Sirius is absentmindedly running his hands through Remus' hair while Remus drags his fingers in abstract patterns across Sirius' skin. "As much as I hate admitting that James was right about<em>anything</em>," Sirius says, "I guess his attempts at meddling worked after all."</p>
<p>Remus snorts. "I think the moral of the story here is that we<em>all</em>needed outside intervention. We're suchfucking pathetic sods."</p>
<p>"That's okay," Sirius replies. "I happen to like pathetic sods."</p>
<p>Remus grins, looking Sirius in the eyes. "Is is too late to call you my valentine?"</p>
<p>Sirius laughs at the clear callback to James' caption. "Never too late," he answers resolutely, before leaning down to kiss him once more.</p>
