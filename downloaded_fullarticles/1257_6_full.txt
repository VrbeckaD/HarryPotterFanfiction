</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>The reality was that he had no job offers, no future. He had money from war reparations, with which he purchased a flat in muggle london. But that was it. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>George had sent him a letter apologizing, and asking if he would consider running Weasleys Wizarding Wheezes with him. Ron had accepted a little prematurely. The thought of taking Freds place put a feeling of dread in the pit of his stomach. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I always knew Hermione would take the top spot when we graduated,” Harry said easily as they walked towards her. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Harry hugged her tightly, and Ron stood to the side, a small smile on his face. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Wotcher, Mione’,” he said, and watched the way her eyes tightened slightly. “You were brilliant, and I’m proud of you.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Thanks Ron,” Hermione said graciously. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>She made no move to touch him. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Ron did his best not to let those thoughts get to him. He had a job to do. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I just wanted to say, I know things have been rough between us lately,” he said, and she waited with baited breath whilst Harry pretended to be oblivious and meander off towards their collective friends and family. “But, I love you, and I wanted you to know how sorry I am. You’re the reason I’ve made it this far, and I don’t think I ever thanked you enough.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>He could see her getting upset, and he cursed himself for always cocking everything up. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Anyway,” he said, “I just wanted to say that, and to let you know Mum will expect you to come around soon.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Ron walked away then, leaving her shell-shocked and made his way over to Harry. The two friends hoped Hermione would have accepted Harrys invitation to go to the Leaky, but Hermione and Ginny decided they’d like to have some girl time, whatever that meant. Ron knew that Hermione hated that kind of thing, and figured she was just wanting to avoid him. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“You ready, Ron? Harry said breezily, and Ron raised his head to meet his best friends eye. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>A brilliant shade of emerald green. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Killing Curse Green. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Most of the wizarding world was terrified of that spell, but Ron thought it might be peaceful. It was painless, there would be no suffering. The thought was appealing. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Yea, Mate,” Ron said, smiling. “I think I am.”</span>
</p><p><br/>
<br/>
</p><p align="center">
  <em>
    <span>“Remember me, and smile.”</span>
  </em>
</p><p align="center">
  <em>
    <em>
      <span>-Dr. Seuss </span>
    </em>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p><br/>
<br/>
</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>“</span>
  </em>
  <span>Okay, you can do this,” Hermione whispered furiously to herself and she trudged up the stairs. “Don’t be a coward, Granger.” </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>She had gotten his address from Harry after a little nitpicking. She knew he’d asked to be alone, and Hermione had thought it would be the most opportune moment to get to him without interruption. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>The Wizarding World had knocked some of the common sense out of the usually brilliant Witch. She knew what depression looked like. She suffered from it herself, and she’d read enough Muggle Psychology books that she should have recognised his symptoms for what they were. She had been berating herself for days. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Their break up had been heartwrenching. But Hermione had respected his decision to end things for what it was; selfless. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Now it was Hermiones turn to do the same for Ron. She loved him. So, so much, and she would mend things between them. She would get him the help he needed, and they would be okay. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>She could envision the smile on his face, the smattering of freckles across his nose. Those were her favorite things about him - besides his heart. She made a silent promise to herself to take better care of him. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>The flat was on the third floor, and Hermione huffed and puffed as she trudged up the stairs because, </span>
  <em>
    <span>honestly? Who doesn’t have an elevator? </span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>She made her way to the end of the hall, and, taking a deep breath, she knocked. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>And waited. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>And waited some more. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Hermione twisted the door handle, not surprised when it didn’t budge. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Withdrawing her wand, she whispered, “Alohamora.”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>The door creaked on its hinges as she swung it open, and the first thing she noticed was that the apartment was barren, and quiet. Too quiet. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Ron?” she called out hesitantly, “It’s Hermione!”</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Stepping across the threshold, she noted that there were no pictures, no blinds, no typical creature comforts of any kind. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Ron?” she called again, and a deep sense of foreboding settled into the pit of her stomach, though she wasn’t quite sure why. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“I just wanted to talk to you,” she called out again. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>The door to the bedroom was closed, and Hermiones sense of dread grew because putting two and two together had always been relatively easy to her. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>No one had heard from Ron in days. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>No one had seen him out and about. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>No one had been invited over. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Ron had been suffering from depression. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Unmedicated. Alone.</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>“Ron!” her voice grew louder, more frantic, and her hand trembled against the bedroom doorknob. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <em>
    <span>He’s just not home. He’s just not home, </span>
  </em>
  <span>she thought desperately, and opened the door. </span>
</p><p><br/>
<br/>
</p><p align="center">
  <em>
    <span>“Goodbye,” you said. </span>
  </em>
</p><p align="center">
  <em>
    <span>“Goodbye,” I said. </span>
  </em>
</p><p align="center">
  <em>
    <span>And no one thought twice </span>
  </em>
</p><p align="center">
  <em>
    <span>About it. </span>
  </em>
</p><p align="center">
  <em>
    <em>
      <span>-Unknown</span>
    </em>
  </em>
</p><p align="left"> </p><p align="left">
  <em>
    <span>12 hours earlier… </span>
  </em>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>No one had ever been inside of Ron Weasleys flat. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>If they had, they might have found it odd. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>The furniture was sparse. In fact, there was only a single chair, and a table. The rest of the apartment was empty save for a bed in the back bedroom. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>A little like he was. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Harry wasn’t set to stop by for a day or two. Ginny either. His family knew he’d asked to be alone for a few days, and they respected his wishes grudgingly. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>He counted out the letters on the table for his parents, Harry, Ginny, George, Percy, Bill and Fleur, his Slytherin friends. One slightly longer one for Hermione. All set out and easy to find. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>He was fully dressed, because he didn’t want anyone to be embarrassed. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>He pondered for a second, lying down would be good, right?</span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>He made the bed with quick, precise movements, and laid down on top of the covers. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>He hadn’t felt this jittery since the last time he’d played on the Quidditch team, and smiled at the thought. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>Anticipation - that’s what it was. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <span>He grabbed his wand, and took a couple of deep, calming, breaths. </span>
</p><p> </p><p>
  <em>
    <span>“Avada Kedavra.”</span>
  </em>
