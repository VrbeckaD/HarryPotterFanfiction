    <p align="justify"><em>Dear Ms. Granger,</em><br/>
<br/>
<em>The Ministry of Magic has decreed that all witches and wizards of ”<span class="s-rg-t">pureblood</span>” background are expected to spend a minimum of one (1) hour per week socializing with a witch or wizard born to non-magical parents. </em><br/>
<br/>
<em>The performed activity can <span class="s-ja-t">be</span> any event that complies with the standard laws of the wizarding world.</em><br/>
<br/>
<em>The Ministry hopes that this will help in rebuilding <span class="s-ja-t">new</span> connections between spell casters of diverse backgrounds and help undo the importance of blood status in the wizarding world.</em><br/>
<br/>
<em>Please find attached the information about Your first meeting.</em><br/>
<br/>
<em>We thank You for Your co-operation.</em><br/>
<br/>
<em>Signed Kingsley <span class="s-rg-t">Shacklebolt</span>, </em><br/>
<em>Minister for Magic. </em><br/>
<br/>
Hermione stared at the piece of parchment. She wasn’t quite sure what to think about this.<br/>
Sure, it was rather <span class="s-ja-t">nice</span> of the Ministry to try and actually do something for a change. However, she was uncertain if civil conversation was enough to undo decades of prejudice.<br/>
<em><span class="s-ja-t">Well</span>, it is a start.</em><em> We all have to start somewhere</em>, she thought with a wry smile on her face, recalling how she had tried to get Harry and Ron involved with <span class="s-rg-t">S.P.E.W.</span><br/>
<br/>
Hermione waved her wand absently, making the teapot on the kitchen table float upwards and tip more of its contents to her mug.<br/>
It had been over five years since the war.<span class=""> The first year had been hard and chaotic for all involved, with all the trauma and wounds, both mental and physical, but slowly, the Golden Trio had settled in on their lives.</span><br/>
<br/>
Harry was working as the DADA teacher at Hogwarts, living at the castle during the term. On holidays he traveled wherever Ginny Weasley, the star chaser of the Holyhead Harpies, went. <br/>
<br/>
Ron was working with his brothers at <span class="s-rg-t">Weasleys</span>’ Wizard Wheezes. He lived with Fred and George in a small flat above the store at <span class="s-rg-t">Diagon</span> Alley. </p><p align="justify">And herself? She worked as an assistant at Flourish &amp; <span class="s-rg-t">Blotts</span> and lived in a cozy apartment in London, quite near where her parents used to live. It had been a hard decision, but in the end she had decided that it would be kinder to leave Monica and Wendell Wilkins to live in Australia with their new son, Henry.</p><p align="justify">She was perfectly happy living alone.  Well, not all alone.<br/>
After the war she had gone back to the Burrow, where she had reunited with her beloved cat, <span class="s-rg-t">Crookshanks</span>.<br/>
<br/>
Ron had respected her wishes of taking it slow and <span class="s-ja-t">giving</span> her space. She <span class="s-ja-t">needed</span> time to figure what she <span class="s-ja-t">wanted</span> to <span class="s-ja-t">do</span> with her life. She cared <span class="s-ja-t">deeply</span> about Ron and felt relieved that he was <span class="s-ja-t">happy</span> with just them going on dates now and then, <span class="s-ja-t">just</span> enjoying each others company.<br/>
<br/>
Hermione took a sip of her tea and lifted up the other piece of parchment that had come with the decree.<br/>
<br/>
Dear Ms. Granger,<br/>
<br/>
<em> Your first meeting will be on Monday at 2:00 PM </em><br/>
<em> at the Fleur de Lys restaurant. </em><br/>
<em> Your expenses will be taken care of by your host. </em><br/>
<br/>
<em> We trust that you will be punctual. </em><br/>
<em> Ministry of Magic </em><br/>

<em> Fleur de Lys? </em>That was the <span class="s-ja-t">posh</span> French restaurant in <span class="s-rg-t">Diagon</span> Alley.  Hermione had <span class="s-ja-t">never</span> been there, as it was <span class="s-ja-t">quite</span> expensive, but she had dreamed about going there some day with Ron.  But now she had to go there with some strange <span class="s-rg-t">pureblood</span>.  <br/>
She wondered if her mysterious partner had <span class="s-ja-t">chosen</span> this restaurant on purpose.<br/>
Seeing how they’re <span class="s-rg-t">pureblood</span>, they are probably rolling in galleons, she thought.<br/>
I <span class="s-ja-t">just</span> hope it’s nobody too insufferable.  Like <span class="s-rg-t">Zabini</span>.  Or even worse, <span class="s-rg-t">Malfoy</span>.  Though Draco might have <span class="s-ja-t">grown</span> some brains after school.  <br/>

Sighing, she <span class="s-ja-t">threw</span> the piece of parchment on the table.<br/>
<span class="s-ja-t">Well</span>, <span class="s-ja-t">at least</span> she <span class="s-ja-t">had</span> a week to <span class="s-ja-t">plan</span> on what to discuss with her Ministry appointed <span class="s-rg-t">pureblood</span>.<br/>

<span class="s-ja-t">Maybe</span> she’d <span class="s-ja-t">get</span> them to join <span class="s-rg-t">S.P.E.W.</span></p>
