        <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Work Text:</h3>
          <div class="userstuff"><p>Harry was horrible at Occlumency.</p><p> </p><p>He had never been able to “clear his mind”. His attempts at occluding had done nearly nothing to block Voldemort’s access to it, and vice versa.</p><p> </p><p>Harry had tried incredibly hard to stop the nightmares, the visions, and the blazing pain whenever Voldemort was angry.</p><p> </p><p>He couldn’t stop Snape from seeing his memories, nor his private thoughts.</p><p> </p><p>Night after night he would be terrified of sleeping, knowing that when he closed his eyes he would be trapped in the mind of a madman.</p><p> </p><p>Harry felt too deeply–was too much of a Gryffindor–to hide behind walls and wait it out.</p><p> </p><p>But, he couldn’t help but think that maybe, just maybe, if he could’ve learned, then maybe he could have a part of himself that was <em> his </em> and <em> his alone </em> and not exposed to the world.</p><p> </p><p>
  <em> (everything about himself, everything he owned, it was all defined by someone else) </em>
