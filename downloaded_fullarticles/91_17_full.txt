      <p>So, my friends and I started talking about ripping up the HP epilogue and making our own canon up at the end of December 2020, and it extremely quickly spiralled into a fully-fledged RP with some wonderful alternative pairings for the Trio Gen. Pearl wrote <a href="https://archiveofourown.org/works/22868923" rel="nofollow">when the future's architectured</a> to share Theo Nott's story (which involves both Ginny Weasley and Astoria Greengrass) and it's stellar. </p><p>I've always been intrigued by the idea of an obnoxious, hyper self-obsessed, phlegmatic son of Luna Lovegood and Draco Malfoy - and then I played myself by getting obsessed with the two of them and all their potential.   <span>"Lovegood-Malfoy," she repeats, and goes to her knees to join him, pressing as close as she can get. "I like the sound of that."</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Two months later, he marries her in an ancient wizarding house in the middle of Sarajevo. Draco has no best man, and his mother and father scowl through the entire ceremony, and he has absolutely no desire to know what’s going through the minds of the only two people Luna chose to invite. Harry Potter and Ginny Weasley are not the two people he thought he’d have witnessing his wedding, but then there are a lot of things that Luna has changed.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>After excruciatingly awkward drinks and stilted conversation, Draco escapes to a balcony and tilts back his head. The wind plays with his hair, August evening sunshine catching on the city roofs and turning everything gold and pink.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Bare arms slide around his waist and a body presses into his back. He smiles without opening his eyes and draws Luna round, holding her into his side. One palm splays out against the round shape of his son. Luna sighs, just this tiny little noise, and Draco glances down at her. She’s staring out at the rooftops too, her expression placid and pleased. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“What are you thinking?” he asks quietly. He doesn’t bother to suppress the surge of proprietal desire that flares inside him when the setting sun catches at the gold band on her left ring finger.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Oh,” she says idly, smiling, “nothing much. Oh! Careful, there’s a wrackspurt—”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Draco laughs even as he gathers her closer. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Well,” he murmurs against the line of her jaw, “it is seven-twenty-five.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It is,” she agrees solemnly, and then she’s kissing him soundly enough to make him see stars.</span>
</p></div>
