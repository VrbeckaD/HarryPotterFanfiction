<hr/><p>4.0 Knitting Needles<br/>
7” x 59”</p><p>1x1 rib<br/>
Cast on even number of stitches</p><p>Yellow Section<br/>
1. K, P to end<br/>
2. K, P to end</p><p>Repeat 1. and 2. until the length measures 5” inches</p><p>Black Stripes<br/>
Change Color to black<br/>
1. K, P to end<br/>
2. K, P to end</p><p>Change to yellow<br/>
3. K, P to end<br/>
4. K, P to end</p><p>Change to black<br/>
6. K, P to end<br/>
7. K, P to end.</p><p>Repeat Yellow Section followed by Black Stripes until the scarf measures 59” in length.</p><p>At the end of the scarf make a Yellow Section without Black Stripes.</p><p><b>Tips</b><br/>
1. I picked a one by one rib because I like the Stockinette look without the curling that stockinette has.</p><p>2. On the color changing Right Side rows I knit the last stitch instead of purling it.</p><p>3. To attach the fringe I cut two strands of yarn that are twice the length I want (6 inches in my case, give me three inch fringe). I take a 5.5 mm crochet hook and put it into the stitch where I want to attach the fringe. I take the two strands of yarn and fold them in half. I pull the loop part way through the stitch with the crochet hook and then pull the ends of the strands through the loop.</p></div>
